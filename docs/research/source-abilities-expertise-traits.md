**Origin:** politicslounge.com forum, board "A More Perfect Union (AMPU)"
**Author:** Arkansas Progressive (game designer)
**Thread dates:** 2025-05-13 → 2026-05-12
**Status:** BINDING design reference. This is the canonical spec for the

## ABILITIES (six, each 0–5)

### Command (0–5)
Ability for a politician to run for or hold President and/or Vice President.
Given at random % chance unless specified.
- **Earned:** anytime evos; becoming faction leader; becoming party leader;
  leaving the career track (except backroom); becoming protege of a master
  kingmaker (guaranteed); becoming committee chair or ranking member;
  becoming President of the Continental Congress; initial appointment to
  Secretary of State; Senior General/Senior Admiral (and equivalent) while a
  major war is active; national ambassadors/UN Ambassador if the US is at war
  with their nation; successful event implementation (unless they have
  Efficient and handle it); succeeding at a gov action (except guaranteed, not
  5 Gov) and again if that action improves a meter; proposing crisis
  legislation that's signed; successfully filibustering; legislation that
  improves a meter; winning a battle; winning a majority of battles during the
  phase; winning a major war (to every active admiral/general).
- **Use:** limit on executive actions per half-term in Executive Office; sways
  during Congress in Session; the number of actions taken during a presidential
  primary or general election (homebrew, not official).
- **Loss:** anytime evos; old-age rolls.

### Legislative (0–5)
Ability to navigate the labyrinthine maze of Congress.
- **Earned:** anytime evos; State Legislative track (primary); all other tracks
  but Backroom and Judicial (secondary); congressional leadership; appointment
  to committee or committee chair; becoming faction leader; being re-elected as
  party leader; winning difficult battles.
- **Use:** being elected/appointed to House, Senate, or Continental Congress;
  50%+1 in Senate elections (if you have the highest ability between both
  general-election candidates); 2 ability required to hold Committee
  Chair/Ranking Member; 2 ability required to filibuster; sometimes required to
  implement executive actions.
- **Loss:** anytime evos; old-age rolls; losing moderate or easy battles.

### Governing (0–5)
Ability to pass an agenda (governor's actions) through the state legislature.
- **Earned:** anytime evos; State Executive track (primary); all other tracks;
  becoming faction leader; becoming party leader; improving a meter with a gov
  action; winning difficult or medium battles.
- **Use:** being elected/succeeding to the governorship; sometimes used to
  implement executive actions; enacting state laws (each gov gives a cumulative
  20% chance to enact governor actions).
- **Loss:** old age; anytime evos; losing moderate or easy battles.

### Judicial (0–5)
Ability to elucidate the law from the bench.
- **Earned:** anytime evos; State Judicial track (primary).
- **Use:** appointment to the Supreme Court; implementation of executive
  actions.
- **Loss:** old age; anytime evos.

### Administrative (0–5)
How well a politician navigates the bureaucratic hellscape of Washington, D.C.
- **Earned:** State Admin track (primary); all other tracks but Legislative;
  becoming faction leader; anytime evos; confirmation to cabinet (doubled if
  Egghead, Efficient, and stacks if both); winning difficult and moderate
  battles; winning the majority of battles that phase of the war; being
  re-elected to two or more terms as governor; being elected/appointed to two
  or more terms as Senator.
- **Use:** 2 minimum for cabinet appointment (unless no other eligibles exist);
  3 required for DCIA/DNI, DFBI, UN Amb; primary implementation for actions and
  event responses; moving meters during the lingering phase; used in battles
  for the planning phase; +1 in primaries (with econ crisis and econ
  expertise); make an appeal to credibility at the convention (with ≥1 admin and
  econ meter <5, OR has Justice and ≥1 admin).
- **Loss:** anytime evos; old age; Easily Overwhelmed (if retained); losing
  moderate and easy battles; losing the majority of battles that phase of the
  war.

### Military (0–5)
How effective a man is at commanding the men beneath him.
- **Earned:** Military track; anytime evos; 2 Rev War-era evos; winning
  difficult and medium battles.
- **Use:** affects the Military Preparedness chart during the Lingering Phase;
  2 minimum for Sr Gen/Army CoS and Sr Adm/CNO; 3 minimum to be auto-confirmed.
- **Loss:** anytime evos; losing difficult, medium, and easy battles; old age.

## EXPERTISE (nineteen tags)

> Each displays what the politician studied in higher education, or worked
> their way through a job's rankings. **This is a distinct axis from abilities
> and traits.**

Agriculture · Business · Economics · Education · Energy · Environment ·
Foreign Affairs · Healthcare · Housing · Justice · Labor · Media · Military ·
Naval · Science · Technology · Trade · Transportation · Welfare

- **Gain:** leaving the career tracks; getting re-elected as governor;
  appointment to a committee; gained during appointment/reappointment to
  cabinet offices and ambassadorships.
- **Use:** primarily determines who is eligible for appointment to cabinet
  offices. Also determines faction ideologies, and determines certain governor
  actions that can be done.

## TRAITS (fifty-seven total)

> "All of which are covered in 3.0.19-2." Trait effects on elections;
> Kingmakers give +1 in-state, Master Kingmakers give +1 nationally; some allow
> extra gov actions or proposals (Efficient); Iron Fist gives control over
> subordinates (good as party leader and Speaker/Senate Majority Leader).
>
> **Gain:** traits not born with are gained by coming off all of the tracks,
> becoming a protege (with some exceptions), winning or losing elections.
> **Loss:** lost with old age (the advantageous ones); or, if you gain a trait
> that conflicts with one you have, you roll to lose it on a d6.

**Bookkeeper** — generational accounting genius / unparalleled economic
instincts; reacts quickly and efficiently in an economic administrative
position. Born to be Sec of Treasury.
**Carpetbagger** — recently moved to a new state hoping to gain office; their
ambition is transparent and their manner alien to locals, so they struggle to
establish themselves in the new state.
**Celebrity** — nationwide recognition among voters for accomplishments outside
politics.
**Charisma** — magnetic appeal (looks + personality + enrapturing speaking
style), recognized even by opponents; some voters are drawn to charisma over
party or ideology.
**Controversial** — reputation for saying/doing morally wrong things; harder to
get elected; in particular danger against an opponent known for Integrity.
**Lawful** (master sheet name "Cop") — generational administrative
prosecutor/defender; brilliant at wielding a justice department for civil
rights, civil liberties, crime, or insurrection. Uniquely skilled to be
Attorney General.
**Cosmopolitan** — more national figure than regional; attuned to the nation
over their state/region; typically urban. Advantage in national offices (e.g.
the presidency), disadvantage in local races (Governor, US Rep). Easy
party-wide appeal.
**Crisis Admin** — generational cabinet officer who shines during a major
crisis primarily handled by their department.
**Crisis Gov** — may not have presidential crisis-leadership, but phenomenal at
contributing to ending a crisis at the state level; generational skill at
getting their state's mechanisms working toward the national effort.
**Crisis Manager** — born to lead the nation during a crisis regardless of
other abilities; an otherwise uninspiring politician can become a legend.
**Rare trait.**
**Decisive General** — military leader who rises to save the nation when near
defeat or needing one major victory to end the war favorably.
**Debater** — incisive debater; finds the best rhetorical methods to defend
their ideas and attack an opponent's.
**Delegator** — outsources responsibilities (well-served by experts, or lazy);
likes the appearance/title of leadership more than the work.
**Disharmonious** — lashes out / creates disruption when their opinions aren't
well received; may break from their own party; some cabinet officers with this
flat-out resign; acts selfishly.
**Domestic Apathy** — chronic neglect of/limited empathy for domestic issues;
regardless of admin skill, better in foreign affairs, military, or trade posts.
**Domestic Warrior** — heart committed to domestic affairs; extra effective in
domestic-focus administrative positions; may neglect foreign relations.
**Easily Overwhelmed** — can only handle so much work/responsibility, especially
in a crisis; performs some tasks poorly unless they lose this trait.
**Efficient** — mastered the art of policy; gets work done quickly and above
competent level.
**Egghead** — expert/political nerd, research-oriented and fact-based.
**Flipflopper** — seen as having recently deviated from a once-firm stance;
voters question trustworthiness. Can disappear over time, but further political
evolution before it does risks "Two-Faced."
**Frail** — more prone to fatal illness or accident than others.
**Geostrategist** — generational geopolitics expert; increases State Department
effectiveness if placed there.
**Hale** — more prone to mental and physical longevity; can hold office into
their 80s/90s or beyond.
**Harmonious** — seeks the middle ground; more liable to cave to avoid
disruption; less partisan, but not so bipartisan as to anger the base; aims for
no drama.
**Illicit** — sociopathic concept of law; laws don't apply to them personally;
dangerous in any law-enforcement position, especially Attorney General; breaks
or ignores the law to enforce policy, leading to blundering.
**Incoherent** — difficult to follow / routinely tongue-tied; gaffe-prone in
speeches and debates.
**Integrity** — adheres tightly to right and wrong even at personal cost; breaks
with their party over fundamental moral lines; recognized even by opponents as
honest and incorruptible.
**Iron Fist** — control freak who masters getting what they want from allies in
a leadership position; expects the final word; prefers "yes men."
**Jurisprudence** — generational legal mind; the knowledge and innate instincts
of a canonical judge.
**Kingmaker** — influences politics via other politicians; prefers grooming a
more electable protege; may never hold elected office.
**Lackey** — little independent initiative; thrives on fulfilling orders; parrots
their leader even when the leader's opinions change.
**Late Bloomer** — in no hurry to reach high office; not fit for much until
they've learned the ropes slowly; once ready, could be great.
**Leadership** — reputation as a leader, recognized by their party; expected to
be approached to guide the party to success. (Full earn/use list below.)
**Likable** — hard to dislike; even opponents mention liking them; affects
undecided voters, especially against an Unlikable opponent.
**Everyman** — speaks/thinks like the average working-class American; tangles
with egghead policy wonks; decides by gut, experience, or faith.
**Magician** — generational legislative strategist, capable of legislative
tricks the top ranks cannot do; shows promise before mastering legislating.
Named after Martin Van Buren ("The Little Magician"). **Rare trait.**
**Manipulative** — understands political psychology enough to switch a vote or
convince a president to appoint an ally; one of the most useful traits in
politics despite the negative connotation.
**Master Kingmaker** — unparalleled backroom politician with so many proteges
they effectively control their state; may never hold elective office but is
theoretically in several via political pawns.
**Micromanager** — trust issues / superiority complex; tries to be their own
chief of staff, Sec of State, Sec of Treasury, etc.; gets in their own way and
breaks cabinet communication; useful leading one department, disastrous leading
all of them.
**Military Leader** — recognized as capable of leading the entire military, on
the battlefield or administratively.
**Naive Strategist** — even if a geopolitics expert, laughable lack of
grand-strategy instinct; may be a great administrator who shouldn't apply
knowledge in this area.
**Numberfudger** — laughably incompetent with colossal economic duties even if
otherwise a great administrator; should not be Sec of Treasury.
**Obscure** — most reliable voters have no clue who they are; name recognition
confined to (or unknown even within) their state.
**Orator** — one of the greatest speechmakers of their era; can create a flood
of support from undecided and bipartisan opposition voters.
**Overeager** — unrestrained ambition; wants to be US Rep at 25, Senator at 30,
president at 35.
**Passive** — hands-off to a fault; may not lead when needed; lets aggressive
advisors/allies hold more influence than usual.
**Pliable** — easily convinced to change a vote/decision; easier to convert to
another faction or party.
**Predictable** — consistent in word, action, and values (comforting to some
voters), but a lack of evolution to major events or manipulative strategies is
poison; a predictable president is easily stymied by a manipulative opposing
Speaker.
**Propagandist** — mastered broadcasting their/their faction's opinions via the
mass communication of their era; may use misleading or cherry-picked info; the
worst create bold-faced lies established as truths.
**Provincial** — keenly attuned to local/regional politics; thick regional
accent; appeals almost exclusively to their region (a Deep South provincial has
no appeal in New England and vice versa); advantage for Governor/US Rep,
struggles to become a major presidential candidate.
**Puritan** — unbending devotion to ideology; exists to promote their ideals;
sees little merit in compromise even when compromise prevents the opposite
outcome; cult following but ineffective party/national leader.
**Southern Unionist** — fervent States' Rights believer who finds Union even
more sacred; will not secede with the Deep South, Upper South, or Southwest if
those regions secede.
**Teflon** — routinely avoids lasting damage from major scandals (great at
defense / convincingly blaming others / deflating fallout).
**Two-Faced** — flip-flops so much/so drastically that few voters trust them to
say what they mean.
**Uncharismatic** — scripted/wooden; hard to put a personal touch on a campaign;
struggles to win uninspired and undecided voters.
**Unlikable** — strongly disliked even by a noticeable fraction of their own
party; allied politicians dislike them personally; can increase opposition
turnout more than their own.

### Leadership — full earn/use (separate post)
- **Gain:** all career tracks except Judicial; ContCong President; Speaker /
  Maj-Min Leader; Maj/Min Whip (only if congressional officers are created);
  Cmte Chair; Cmte Ranking Member (if cong officers are active); being on a
  committee; becoming faction leader (if you didn't have it); becoming party
  leader; winning difficult or medium battles.
- **Use:** Kingmakers/Master Kingmakers get +2 protege limit; becoming Speaker,
  Majority/Minority Leader, Majority/Minority Whip, Committee Chair or Ranking
  Member; becoming faction leader; becoming party leader (100%); compel SCOTUS
  judge retirements (as faction leader); with Iron Fist, block primary
  challengers 90% of the time (opposition PLs can do so 50%); +1 candidate
  strength; +1 in the debate (+2 with Charisma); 33% to unilaterally change
  party rules at convention; 33% to whip the party into compliance (+1
  momentum); an Obscure VP can gain Leadership on a 1/d6; +1 in presidential
  general debate; 33% after losing the presidency by under 60% of the EC
  (PopVote if EC is abolished); 20% if from a Big State, 10% Medium, 5% Small
  upon election/re-election as governor.
- **Loss:** unelected president who doesn't become party leader; losing medium
  or easy battles; old-age rolls.

## CABINET (structure)

- **Big 4 / Upper Cabinet:** State, Treasury, War (later Defense), Attorney
  General — always face a confirmation hearing (committee/floor vote).
- **Lower Cabinet:** any "Secretary of" position created by legislation; PMG
  (when the elevation bill is enacted).
- **Cabinet-Level:** FBI, CIA/DNI, Bank President/Fed Reserve Chair, UN
  Ambassador, National Security Advisor, PMG (when initially created).
- **Ambassadors:** initially Ministers before elevation. Special non-meter
  Ministers include Indian Sovereignty, Confederate States, Northern
  Confederacy (petition to rename to Free States of America), and Western
  America.
- **Key Advisor** is a distinct non-cabinet position.
