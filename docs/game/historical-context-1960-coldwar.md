## ★ The load-bearing framing: a 1960 start is mid-realignment (the South is still BLUE)

**This is THE thing to get right about a 1960-start board.** The batch's 10
factions use *near-modern* polarity — **BLUE = Democrats, RED = Republicans** —
but the **Civil Rights / Southern realignment has NOT happened yet**, so the
Democratic Party is a **single big tent containing both poles of the coming
split**: **[consensus]**

- **BLUE (Democrats) at the 1960 start contains, simultaneously:**
  - the **segregationist Southern Democrats** — game faction led by **Strom
    Thurmond** (chunk-001 post 2: "the epitome of Southern Democrats … notorious
    for the longest filibuster in United States history with his opposition to
    the Civil Rights Act of 1957");
  - the **Establishment / "King of Congress"** — **Lyndon B. Johnson** (Senate
    Majority Leader, the dealmaker who "can work with the conservative and
    liberal blocs");
  - the **Corporatists** — **John F. Kennedy** (the establishment-backed
    front-runner);
  - the **Liberal Bloc** — **Hubert Humphrey** (the 1948 civil-rights-plank
    champion); and
  - the **Radical Reformists** — **Estes Kefauver** (a *Southern* liberal /
    anti-corruption crusader).
- **RED (Republicans) at the 1960 start:** the **Business Republicans** (VP
  **Richard Nixon**, the near-certain nominee), the **War Hawks** (Senate
  Minority Leader **Everett Dirksen**), the **Globalists** (incumbent President
  **Dwight Eisenhower**, locked unless a retirement roll passes), the
  **Technocrats** (aerospace CEO **Charles F. Adams IV** — a *fictional-role*
  faction head, the great-great-great-grandson of John Adams, real person but
  never in politics), and the **Rockefeller Republicans** (liberal NY Governor
  **Nelson Rockefeller**, the possible challenger to Nixon).

**Why this is load-bearing:** the defining political tension of a 1960 board is
**intra-party (within BLUE)**, not BLUE-vs-RED. The segregationist Thurmond bloc
and the Humphrey/Kefauver civil-rights bloc are *in the same party*, and the
LBJ/JFK center is brokering between them. This is exactly the **§6→§7 hinge** in
the parent doc: 1960 sits *after* the economic New Deal realignment (1932, done)
but *during* the racial/regional Southern realignment (c. 1948–1994, mid-flight).
The "white South starts to leave the Democrats" story is **just beginning** in
1960 and is **not** complete until the 1990s — beware narrating it as a clean
1964 flip. **[consensus]** (See `historical-context-1948-coldwar.md` ★ section.)

**Period terminology at the 1960 start (and the polarity traps):**
- **"Liberal"** already means its **modern** thing — pro-government, pro-labor,
  pro-civil-rights — since the 1932 New Deal realignment. A "Liberal" faction
  (Humphrey) is the *civil rights* wing. **[consensus]**
- **"Conservative"** in 1960 spans **two distinct things**: Republican
  business/anti-New-Deal conservatism (Dirksen, Goldwater-to-come) **and**
  Southern Democratic **segregationist** conservatism (Thurmond). They are *not*
  the same coalition yet — that fusion is what the realignment builds.
- **"Dixiecrat"** = the segregationist States'-Rights Democrat (Thurmond ran as
  one in 1948). Still a **Democratic** sub-bloc in 1960. **[consensus]**
- **Avoid** importing modern "red state / blue state" intuitions: in 1960 the
  **Deep South is the most reliably Democratic (BLUE) region in the country**,
  and the Northeast holds the **liberal Republican (Rockefeller)** wing — both
  of which the modern map inverts.

## Real timeline (key dated events a 1960→1968 game touches)

**Pre-game backdrop (named in chunk-001):**
- **Oct 4, 1957** — USSR launches **Sputnik**, the first satellite; touches off
  the "missile gap" panic and the Space Race. **[consensus]**
- **May 1, 1960** — a U.S. **U-2 spy plane** is shot down over the USSR (pilot
  Francis Gary Powers captured); collapses the Paris summit and Eisenhower's
  planned Moscow trip. **[consensus]**

**The 1960 election (the divergence point):**
- **Sep 26, 1960** — first-ever **televised presidential debate**, Nixon vs.
  Kennedy, ~70 million viewers (~⅔ of the electorate). Kennedy widely seen as the
  apparent winner. **[consensus]** ([History.com](https://www.history.com/this-day-in-history/september-26/first-kennedy-nixon-debate), [JFK Library — Campaign of 1960](https://www.jfklibrary.org/learn/about-jfk/jfk-in-history/campaign-of-1960))
- **Nov 8, 1960** — **JFK def. Nixon**, one of the closest elections in U.S.
  history: popular-vote margin ~118,550 of ~69 million; **EV 303–219**.
  **[consensus]** ([JFK Library — Campaign of 1960](https://www.jfklibrary.org/learn/about-jfk/jfk-in-history/campaign-of-1960)) — **In this game, the divergence overrides this: see "Divergences" below.**

**JFK term (real history, 1961–63):**
- **Apr 12, 1961** — USSR's **Yuri Gagarin** is first human in orbit; **May 5,
  1961** Alan Shepard is first American in space (suborbital). **[consensus]**
- **Apr 17–19, 1961** — the **Bay of Pigs** invasion fails within ~2 days; a
  major early embarrassment. **[consensus]** ([State Dept — Bay of Pigs](https://history.state.gov/milestones/1961-1968/bay-of-pigs))
- **May 25, 1961** — JFK's **"man on the Moon by the end of the decade"** speech
  to Congress; launches **Apollo**. **[consensus]** ([NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/history/the-decision-to-go-to-the-moon/))
- **Oct 16–28, 1962** — the **Cuban Missile Crisis** ("thirteen days"): U-2 finds
  Soviet medium-range missiles in Cuba (photographed Oct 14, JFK briefed Oct 16);
  naval "quarantine" announced **Oct 22**; Khrushchev agrees to remove missiles
  **Oct 28** in exchange for a U.S. no-invasion pledge and (secretly) U.S. Jupiter
  missiles out of Turkey. **The real "big red button" near-miss — the closest the
  Cold War came to nuclear war.** **[consensus]** ([JFK Library](https://www.jfklibrary.org/learn/about-jfk/jfk-in-history/cuban-missile-crisis), [State Dept](https://history.state.gov/milestones/1961-1968/cuban-missile-crisis))
- **Nov 22, 1963** — **JFK assassinated** in Dallas; **LBJ** sworn in same day.
  **[consensus]**

**LBJ term (real history, 1963–69):**
- **Jul 2, 1964** — **Civil Rights Act of 1964** signed (LBJ). **[consensus]**
  ([National Archives](https://www.archives.gov/milestone-documents/civil-rights-act))
- **Aug 1964** — **Gulf of Tonkin Resolution** (reported attacks on U.S.
  destroyers; the Aug 4 attack later judged not to have happened) gives LBJ a
  broad mandate; **1965** brings the vast escalation of the Vietnam War.
  **[consensus]** ([State Dept — Gulf of Tonkin](https://history.state.gov/milestones/1961-1968/gulf-of-tonkin), [National Archives](https://www.archives.gov/milestone-documents/tonkin-gulf-resolution))
- **Nov 3, 1964** — **LBJ def. Goldwater** in a landslide: **EV 486–52**,
  **61.1%** popular vote (still the highest popular-vote share of any presidential
  candidate). **Goldwater carries only his home Arizona + five Deep South states**
  — the first crack of the white-South realignment at the presidential level.
  **[consensus]** ([Britannica — 1964](https://www.britannica.com/event/United-States-presidential-election-of-1964))
- **Aug 6, 1965** — **Voting Rights Act of 1965** signed (LBJ). **[consensus]**
  ([National Archives](https://www.archives.gov/milestone-documents/voting-rights-act))

**1968 turmoil (the era's endpoint, real history):**
- **Mar 31, 1968** — LBJ announces he will **not** run for re-election.
  **[consensus]**
- **Apr 4, 1968** — **MLK assassinated** (Memphis); riots in 100+ cities.
  **[consensus]**
- **Jun 5–6, 1968** — **RFK assassinated** in LA after winning the California
  primary. **[consensus]**
- **Nov 5, 1968** — **Nixon def. Humphrey** (and George Wallace, the segregationist
  third-party "law and order" candidate): **EV 301–191–46**; Nixon **43.4%**,
  Humphrey **42.7%**, Wallace **13.5%**. **[consensus]** ([National Archives — 1968 EC](https://www.archives.gov/electoral-college/1968))

## Key figures (with what they really were in 1960)

- **Richard Nixon** — sitting VP (1953–61), the **real** 1960 GOP nominee; lost to
  JFK by a whisker. The game starts with his nomination "assured, should he want
  it." **[consensus]**
- **John F. Kennedy** — MA senator, the real 1960 Democratic nominee and winner.
  In-game cast as the BLUE **Corporatists** leader. **[consensus]**
- **Lyndon B. Johnson** — Senate Majority Leader ("King of Congress"), JFK's real
  1960 running mate, became president on JFK's assassination. In-game the BLUE
  **Establishment** leader. **[consensus]**
- **Hubert Humphrey** — MN senator, leader of the **civil-rights liberal** wing;
  real 1948 convention civil-rights-plank champion; LBJ's eventual VP and the 1968
  Democratic nominee. In-game the **Liberal Bloc**. **[consensus]**
- **Strom Thurmond** — SC senator; record-setting 24-hour filibuster against the
  1957 Civil Rights Act; 1948 Dixiecrat presidential candidate. **In real history
  he switched to the Republican Party in 1964** (a marker of the realignment) —
  but at the 1960 start he is **still a Democrat (BLUE)**. In-game the
  **Southern Democrats**. **[consensus]**
- **Estes Kefauver** — TN senator, a rare *Southern* liberal / anti-corruption
  crusader; Stevenson's 1956 running mate. (Died Aug 1963 in real life — worth
  flagging if the game keeps him alive past then.) In-game the **Radical
  Reformists**. **[consensus]/[inference on the date caveat]**
- **Everett Dirksen** — IL senator, GOP Minority Leader; pivotal in passing the
  1964 Civil Rights Act (rounding up Republican votes to break the Southern
  Democratic filibuster) and later a Vietnam hawk. In-game the **War Hawks**.
  **[consensus]**
- **Dwight D. Eisenhower** — incumbent two-term president (term-limited by the
  22nd Amendment, ratified 1951). In-game the **Globalists** (locked unless a
  retirement roll). **[consensus]**
- **Nelson Rockefeller** — liberal NY Governor (took office Jan 1959), the great
  hope of the GOP's Northeastern liberal wing; repeatedly sought the GOP
  nomination and repeatedly lost to the party's rising conservative/Western base.
  In-game the **Rockefeller Republicans**. **[consensus]**
- **Charles F. Adams IV** — real Raytheon president and Adams-family scion who
  **never held political office**; the game's only **invented-role** faction
  head (the **Technocrats**), explicitly "no historical expectations"
  (chunk-001 post 2). Treat anything the game does with him as **pure fiction**,
  not divergence-from-record. **[consensus]**
- **John W. Bricker** — *not* a starting faction leader, but **the pivotal figure
  the divergence produced** (see below). Real history: arch-conservative Ohio
  Republican, **Governor 1939–45**, **U.S. Senator 1947–59**, **Dewey's 1944 VP
  running mate** (lost to FDR), and namesake of the **Bricker Amendment**
  (1953–54, to curb presidential treaty/executive-agreement power; defeated in the
  Senate by one vote). He was an **isolationist anti-New-Dealer who had already
  LEFT the Senate (defeated 1958, out by Jan 1959)** — i.e., in real history he
  was a *retired* figure by 1960, never a 1960 contender. **[consensus]**
  ([Britannica — Bricker](https://www.britannica.com/biography/John-W-Bricker), [Bricker Amendment — CFR](https://www.cfr.org/articles/remembering-the-bricker-amendment))

## Divergences this batch produced (alt-history vs. real record)

**The run diverges hard and early — flag these for the game-pm / game-master so
era framing isn't accidentally "corrected" back to reality:**

1. **GOP nominee is JOHN W. BRICKER, not Nixon.** Through in-game convention play
   the Republicans nominated **Senator John Bricker** — *"not just their best
   choice, but the Republican's ONLY choice at the convention"* (chunk-005 post
   ~89, "@Vols21 Senator John Bricker is the Republican nominee for President").
   This is doubly counterfactual: the real GOP nominated **Nixon**, and Bricker
   was a *retired* (1959) arch-conservative, not a 1960 contender at all.
   Players openly noted the oddity — *"The Republicans are tearing themselves to
   pieces, and saddling their party with Mr. Personality of a Bricker"* (chunk-005
   post ~209). **[divergence; real-history anchor: JFK Library Campaign of 1960 +
   Britannica Bricker]**

2. **Bricker WINS the 1960 presidency in a landslide over Kennedy.** Initial
   tally **Bricker 284 – Kennedy 251** (chunk-009 post 596); the GM kept the
   result as canon — *"Bricker and his team earned their landslide victory …
   not going to take that from them"* (chunk-009 post 601) — even while
   retuning the election formula (a +3 platform bug; recomputes showed it would
   have been near-tied at +1, with CA/NY flipping). **Real history: JFK won,
   303–219.** So from the 1960 outcome onward this is a **Republican-administration
   alt-timeline.** **[divergence; anchor: JFK Library Campaign of 1960]**

3. **"President Bricker" governs through 1962–64** and personally signs an
   alt-version of the **Nuclear Test Ban Treaty** in the 1962–64 scripted-event
   block (chunk-022 posts ~1677–1684: *"President Bricker will sign the treaty"*).
   In real history the **Partial Nuclear Test Ban Treaty was signed Aug 1963 under
   JFK**, and the Cuban Missile Crisis (the treaty's real catalyst) was an LBJ-era
   *no* — it was **JFK's** crisis. The game compresses/reassigns these to its
   Bricker administration. **[divergence; anchor: State Dept Cuban Missile Crisis;
   the real PTBT is 1963/JFK]** *Restart date-pin:* the treaty + a Democratic House
   recapture (255–182, +20 since 1960) both land in **1962** (restart chunk-001
   post 1), with **Tip O'Neill taking the Speakership from Joseph Martin** in the
   '62 midterms (Sam Rayburn lost his primary). The real Limited Test Ban Treaty
   was Aug 1963 under JFK — the game runs it ~a year early under Bricker.

4. **A different Democratic legislative leadership.** With the White House lost,
   the game's BLUE coalition builds a **congressional** power base: a **Democratic
   majority taken in '62**, **LBJ as Senate Majority Leader + Tip O'Neill as
   Speaker** ("A Political Marriage," chunk-022 post 1683). Real history: O'Neill
   did not become Speaker until **1977**. This is the run's compensating alt-path —
   the Democrats run the legislature while a Republican runs the executive.
   **[divergence; inference that this is the run's design logic]**

5. **The "big red button" is a literal nuclear-war LOSS condition,** not just
   flavor. The GM frames the whole campaign around it — *"no one wins if that big
   red button gets hit"* (chunk-001 post 1) — i.e., a campaign-ending nuclear
   exchange is on the table as an end state. The **real** analog is the Cuban
   Missile Crisis near-miss; treat the game's nuclear-war condition as a
   *mechanic dramatizing* that real fear, not a real event. **[design framing;
   real-history anchor: Cuban Missile Crisis]**

6. **Scripted-event handling reshuffles real chronology.** The 1962–64 block also
   scripts *"Martin Luther King Jr Seeks Political Office: He'll be drafted in
   1964"* (chunk-022 post 1677). In real history MLK **never sought elective
   office** — he led the movement from outside electoral politics. Treat MLK-as-
   draftee as a game affordance, not history. **[divergence; inference]**

7. **Samuel Pierce appointed the first Black U.S. Attorney General (1962)** — from
   the `3c3c46f0` "Out of the Ashes" restart. Bricker names **Samuel Pierce** AG,
   confirmed 88–12 over a Southern-Democrat bloc (Byrd, Thurmond) (restart
   chunk-001 posts 2 & 10). The game's bio is **accurate to the real man** (Harlem
   native; NY state judge 1959–60; argued *NYT v. Sullivan* before the Supreme
   Court for MLK and the NYT). **The divergence is the office and era:** the real
   **Samuel R. Pierce Jr.** was **Reagan's HUD Secretary (1981–89)** — never
   Attorney General; the **first real Black U.S. AG was Eric Holder (2009)**, so
   the game advances a Black AG by ~47 years. **[divergence; anchors below]**
8. **The 1964 Democratic primary axis is Byrd (right) vs. Earl Long (left)** —
   restart. With the presidency held by Republican Bricker, the BLUE fight is
   intra-party: party leader **Sen. Harry Byrd (VA)** the Traditionalist
   front-runner vs. **former Gov. Earl Long (LA)** leading the left wing (+ minor
   candidates incl. James Farley, Tip O'Neill's New-Deal pick). **Earl Long won
   the first primary (New Hampshire), Byrd second** (restart chunk-001 posts
   23–27). Pure alt-history (real 1964 = LBJ uncontested). A 1960-ticket detail the
   restart adds: **Bricker/Laxalt** beat **Kennedy/Reagan**. **[divergence/fiction]**

## Pop-history simplifications to avoid (era-specific)

- **"The South flipped to the GOP in 1964."** No — 1964 (Goldwater's five Deep
  South states) is the **first presidential crack**; the realignment is **gradual,
  decades-long**, not complete until the 1990s. At the 1960 start it has barely
  begun. **[consensus]** (parent doc §7; `1948-coldwar.md` ★)
- **"The 1964 Civil Rights Act was Democrats vs. Republicans."** No — it was
  **regional**: a Northern Democrat + Republican coalition (Dirksen pivotal)
  *against* a Southern Democratic filibuster. A **higher %** of Republicans than
  Democrats voted for it. **[consensus]** (parent doc §7)
- **"Kennedy clearly won the 1960 debate (and the election) because of TV."** The
  "radio listeners thought Nixon won, TV viewers thought Kennedy won" claim is a
  popular trope but **thinly sourced and contested** by scholars — don't state it
  as fact. The *election* itself was razor-thin (≤0.2% popular margin), not a TV
  blowout. **[contested]** ([Purdue — scholarly analysis of the debates](https://www.cla.purdue.edu/academic/history/debate/kennedynixon/kennedynixonscholarly.html))
- **"The Cuban Missile Crisis was a clean American win."** The public deal was
  a Soviet missile withdrawal for a U.S. no-invasion pledge; the **secret** half
  was the U.S. quietly pulling Jupiter missiles from Turkey — a **negotiated**
  off-ramp, not a one-sided victory. **[consensus]** ([State Dept](https://history.state.gov/milestones/1961-1968/cuban-missile-crisis))
- **"Gulf of Tonkin = a clear North Vietnamese attack."** The Aug 2, 1964 attack
  was real; the **Aug 4 attack (the one that triggered the Resolution) is now
  judged not to have happened** (declassified NSA analysis). **[consensus]**
  ([State Dept](https://history.state.gov/milestones/1961-1968/gulf-of-tonkin))

## Game treatment vs. real history — notes for game-pm / game-master

- **The factions are accurately era-keyed.** A 1960 board that puts Thurmond's
  segregationist Southern Democrats and Humphrey's civil-rights liberals **in the
  same BLUE party** is *exactly right* for the period and is the single best
  era-fidelity signal in this batch. Preserve that intra-BLUE tension; do not let
  it drift toward a clean modern BLUE=left / RED=right read.
- **From the 1960 result onward, this is a Republican-administration
  alt-timeline (President Bricker).** Anything the game-pm narrates about
  "the administration" 1961+ refers to **Bricker**, not Kennedy/Johnson. Real
  events tied to the JFK/LBJ presidency (Bay of Pigs, the Apollo pledge, the
  Civil Rights/Voting Rights Acts, Vietnam escalation) **cannot be assumed** to
  occur as recorded — they are now contingent on the alt-administration's choices.
- **Real dates are still the right scaffolding for *unscripted* era texture.**
  Use the timeline above to time pressure (Space Race milestones, Soviet
  saber-rattling, civil-rights marches) even as the *political* outcomes diverge.
- **Charles F. Adams IV (Technocrats) is fiction by design** — never flag the
  game's use of him as a historical error.
- **Watch the death/retirement clock if the run resumes:** real-history exits the
  game may keep alive — **Kefauver (d. Aug 1963)**, the JFK assassination (Nov
  1963, which here did not put LBJ in the White House since Bricker is president),
  and Eisenhower's term-limit. The game's roster will legitimately diverge from
  who was alive/in-office in real 1962–68.
