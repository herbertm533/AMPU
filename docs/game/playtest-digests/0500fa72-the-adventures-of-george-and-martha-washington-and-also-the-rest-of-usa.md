## ★ THE HOUSE RULES (the headline — each = a gap signal)

Lars lists his rules up front (post 1) and applies them throughout. Player-invented fixes are the highest-signal content here: each marks where the documented rules are silent or feel wrong.

1. **★ GENDER-ELIGIBILITY MODEL — NEW (no gap-log row, not in code).** Post 1, 19:
   *"Women can enter backrooms politics despite the lack of suffrage. Likewise, if her husband becomes president, she can become key advisor."* Ted endorses it as desirable (post 5: *"I like your spouses can be Key Advisor rule"*).
   - This implies a **`gender`/sex attribute on Politician** (the build has **none** — `grep -i gender|sex|spouse|wife src/` = ZERO) and a **suffrage/eligibility era-gate keyed to gender**: women are barred from *electoral* office (no suffrage in 1772) but **permitted in non-electoral backroom roles** (lobby/backroom track) and **Key Advisor via spousal link** to a sitting President.
   - Requires a **spousal-relationship link** between politicians (husband→president unlocks wife→Key Advisor). No relationship/family graph exists in the build (only loyalty/faction).
   - In play: women are drafted and active — Abigail Adams (Franklin/RedCon faction), Martha Washington (player faction, post 20), and several "what-if" women (rule 2). Abigail dies post 34 *"but not before giving birth to all of the three pols she was meant to create"* — a hint that **women may be modeled as pol-generators** (a what-if descendant mechanic) on top of the eligibility gate. This is era-faithful (suffrage is itself a Convention/amendment plank elsewhere — `ted1772` #159 had "no women's suffrage" as an article).

2. **★ "Most-realistic what-if" SPECIAL DRAFT POOL — extends #115/#24, distinct player-curated angle.** Post 1, 14:
   *"I'm adding the most realistic of the what-if special draft pols. Say hello to a bunch of women, Toussaint Louverture… and a few more."* Toussaint Louverture is drafted to the American Whigs (post 6) and persists as a real draftee (post 20).
   - Distinct from the #115 *generate-a-candidate backfill* (auto-creating filler pols to fill empty slots): this is a **curated, opt-in "what-if" historical pool** — real people who never served in the actual US (women barred by suffrage; Louverture is Haitian) added to the draftable dataset for alt-history flavor. The codebase's `ERA_FIGURES` (CLAUDE.md: founding-era notables who never served in Congress, additive-only, sub-floor stats) is the closest shipped analog — this thread argues that pool should **explicitly include women + non-US figures** as a toggleable expansion, not just male non-Congressmen.
   - Signal: a **"what-if draft pool" toggle** at game setup (the boot-procedure #115 / draft-pool #24 cluster), separate from procedural backfill.

3. **No moving pols in the very first phase; CPU gets an extra ideology-shift chance based on draft ideologies.** Post 1, 22.
   - A boot-procedure house rule (the #115 setup cluster): suppress the player's phase-1 pol-movement, compensate the CPU with an extra conversion roll seeded from each faction's draft-ideology profile. Reads as a hand-balance for the solo human's first-mover advantage — relevant to the **CPU-conversion heuristic (#70–#78)** and **boot setup (#115)**.

4. **★ "Calvinball" rule — consult experienced dice-rollers to redo an event when the rules are unclear.** Post 1:
   *"If I dont understand what happened, I reserve the right to consult more experienced dice rollers to redo the event."* Exercised constantly (posts 15–17, 45–59, 74, 76–77 — the Lee's-Resolution and Rev-War-Ends tangles; post 76 a format dispute with `@Cal` over how to run the Convention).
   - The cleanest statement that **the rules require a human to interpret** — corroborates **DH-69** (in-app rules surface) and the ★ subsume-the-GM charter. A solo player still cannot self-serve on rule ambiguity.

5. **"I won't report every single little roll"** (post 1) + **an automated death/retirement Python script** (posts 24, 34, 43, 63: *"Lars' Death and Retirements Rolling Python Script! Rolls everything 200 times faster!"*).
   - Direct corroboration of the **GM-burden lesson (#114 / DH-36)**: even a *solo* dice-roller offloads the most mechanical, deterministic per-turn work (death/retirement rolls) to a script and refuses to narrate every roll, because hand-running the full apparatus is unsustainable. The death/retirement roller is exactly the kind of pure-math subsystem the engine must own (same family as the `Lingering.py` scripts in `85f8e6b4`). NB: this is **the same "Lars death/retirement script"** later reused in `arkzag` (cross-referenced in #114) — a recurring community automation.
