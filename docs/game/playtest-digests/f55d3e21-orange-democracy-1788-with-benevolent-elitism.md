## The federalism / early-republic era — how it actually plays (the headline)

This is the richest source yet for an era the build cannot reach. Key facts:

### Parties & factions of 1788 (post 2)
**10 factions, 5 per party**, Right-to-Left, with *period-specific* identities (cf. the build's generic `factions1772.ts` proto-names like "Sons of Liberty"):
- **BLUE = Democratic-Republicans** (Jefferson's party): B1 Old Republicans (Jefferson, RW Pop), B2 Republicans (Madison), B3 Democratic-Republicans (John Milton, moderate), B4 Fusion Democratic-Republicans (Gerry, business-friendly), B5 Democrats (Boudinot, liberal wing).
- **RED = Federalists** (Hamilton's party): R1 States' Rights Federalists (Henry Laurens, Southern wing), R2 Arch Federalists (Hamilton — the player), R3 Moderate Federalists (Washington), R4 Moderates (Franklin), R5 Rush Federalists (Benjamin Rush).
- **Faction names & nicknames drift heavily per era**, tracking real party evolution: across the thread R5 Rush → Benson → Knox → Moderate Federalists; B1 Old Republicans → Anti-Federalists → Old Republicans; B2 Republicans → Traditionalists; B5 Democrats → Populists (LW); R1 States'-Rights → Arch-Federalists → Populists (RW) → Arch-Federalists. **This confirms the per-era faction-relabel mechanic** seen in the 1868 digest (delta #40) and shows it is dense in federalism. Nicknames are gated by the faction-leader's traits/ideology and by a "names table" (post 184: "the names table is hard on the eyes").

### The defining era issues (from era events + bills, posts 26–28, 38, 64, 140, 194, 368, 475, 555, 702)
The era's spine is the **Hamilton vs Jefferson financial/constitutional fight + the wars of the French Revolution**:
- **Hamiltonian financial program** as foundational bills (post 38): **Assume Rev War debt** (Crisis, free), **Bank of the United States** (Crisis — "the most controversial proposal"), **US Mint**, **set average tariff to 10%** (Crisis), **Militia Act** (grant Pres power to call state militia, Crisis), **bimetallic currency** ("US dollar … gold and silver bullion … to incur some inflation to aid debt repayment," post 38 — same bill as the 1868 thread's bimetallism).
- **Compromise of 1790** (era event, post 28): accept Assumption ⇄ move capital to the Potomac (DC). Decided by Pres.
- **Pinckney's Treaty** (Spain, post 28), **Jay-era UK tension**, **Essex Junto** secession scare (New England, post 140 — "-2 to elections for 4 years outside New England/NY"), **Whiskey Rebellion** (post 368), **Fries's Rebellion** (post 702), **XYZ-style France friction**.
- **French Revolution → war** (post 194): Jefferson (CPU) joins France, declaring **War with the UK** ("War of 1795"); reward for winning = "rights to Canada (and the state of Quebec)"; losing = loss on battlefield (post 389, US "got its teeth kicked in"). Later **Barbary War** (post 475) and **War of 1812 / Impressment** (post 702).
- **Party-formation as scripted era events** (post 140): "Federalists are Formed", "Jeffersonian Republicans are Formed" — the **party names literally don't exist until an era event fires them** (~1792 here). Big deal: party identity is itself event-driven in this era.
- **Westward admission as legislation**: Vermont, Kentucky, Ohio, Tennessee, Alabama statehood bills + Mississippi/Indiana/Michigan/Illinois/Louisiana **Territory** bills (posts 81, 158, 302, 379, 560, 718). **Louisiana Purchase** as an era event (post 702, "one term behind schedule").
- **Slavery is a live, *state-level* axis even here**: PA/NY/New England abolish slavery via era events (posts 28, 64, 292); Cotton Gin / Cotton Textile Boom era events make slavery "much more sustainable" (posts 64, 194) — the seed of the nationalism-era crisis.

### The era's draft-ideology profile
Federalism is a **Mod/Cons-dominated era**. Repeated GM remarks: "those ideos [LW Pop/Prog/Lib] don't really matter this era" (post 136); the Senate is "all Mod or Cons except 2 Trad and 1 Prog" (post 330); enthusiasm meters peg toward Mod. So the era's *typical-ideology drafting mix* is heavily center-right — strong support for delta #4 (per-era ideology drafting profile). Andrew Jackson, JQA, Henry Clay, Monroe, Burr, Gallatin all enter via normal draft classes in this window (posts 118, 510, 638) — confirming the **dataset draftYear ≈ birthYear+25** rule produces era-correct rookies.

### Era-transition mechanics observed (federalism → nationalism, ~1800)
- **Points reset at the era boundary** (post 518).
- **A new card pool / new bill catalog appears** ("new things entering with the new era," post 485; Bureau of Indian Affairs, Smithsonian, 36°30′ slavery line, Marine Hospital Service, compensated emancipation, colonization/Liberia appear only at/after the boundary, posts 475, 485, 560, 702).
- **Era-locked content & a documented bug** (posts 521–535): era events "never deactivate; they only deactivate if your *start date* is past the era." Confirmed design ruling by `@Ich_bin_Tyler` (post 524). Consequence: **an 1800-start game wrongly loses the Louisiana Purchase** because its start is past federalism (posts 523, 534). This is a **real, named build bug** for non-1772/non-1856 start years. Also SCOTUS case `Chisholm v. Georgia` should be gated `"11th Amendment not ratified"` (posts 145–150, filed to the dev's Basecamp).

### B. War system (generic, beyond the 1772 Rev War)
