## Mechanics signals (1772 era)

### ★ "Washington Rule" presidential candidacy — and it can FAIL (POST 1, Arnold)
- POST 1: Benedict Arnold (R5), war hero + CC President, was put forward for the first Presidency and became **"the first unsuccessful 'Washington Rule' candidate (in game test history)."** He "finished 4th in the balloting even behind the VP candidate George Washington."
- This names a **no-campaign / candidacy-by-acclaim path to the Presidency** ("Washington Rule") that **does not guarantee a win** — detractors "leaked scandalous stories," and he lost. First-President was won by **John Hancock** (POST 1, Rising Stars), with **Jefferson 2nd → first VP** (POST 1 #7) and **Washington 3rd** as a VP candidate (POST 1 #6).
- **Build cross-ref (SHIPPED, related):** the "Washington Rule" is a documented shipped mechanism — a politician who gains **Military Leadership + Celebrity** (e.g. by winning a marquee battle) acquires the "Washington Rule qualifications" and becomes presidentially eligible (`game-mechanics.md:1171`; the `ted1772` audit, gap #153/#104, where 0-Command CPU Arthur St. Clair rode Long Island → Military Leadership + Celebrity → faction leader → first President). The retrospective confirms the rule is live **and** that the resulting candidacy is a real *contested* election a marquee figure can **lose** — a nuance worth holding for the gap log (a Washington-Rule run is not an auto-coronation).

### ★ Women's suffrage enacted at the Constitutional Convention → DOMESTIC-STABILITY crisis (POST 1, Rising Stars / Hancock)
- POST 1: incoming first President Hancock must address "the revenue crisis **and the budding domestic stability crisis that erupted when women were given the right to vote at the Constitutional Convention**."
- This is a concrete **CC-plank → meter-consequence** interaction: a women's-suffrage plank decided at the CC **triggers a Domestic-Stability crisis** (a destabilizing reform with a meter cost the next administration inherits).
- **Build cross-ref — DELTA (DESIGNED, not shipped):**
  - The shipped CC (`constitutionalConvention.ts:6-67`, `makeConvention`) offers exactly **7 article votes**: `legislature, executive, judiciary, slaveCompromise, amendmentProcess, presidentialEligibility, termLimits`. **There is NO women's-suffrage / voting-rights plank.** A `Grep` for `suffrage|women.*vote|right to vote` finds **zero** in `src/` (only roadmap/research/other-era hits).
  - `meters.domestic` (Domestic Stability) **is** a shipped numeric meter (`types.ts:1453` `domesticStability?`, `Meter.tsx`), and a bad Domestic-Stability value already imposes an incumbent-party election penalty (game-mechanics.md). So the *meter* exists; the **CC-plank that moves it (suffrage) does not**. Requirement: a women's-suffrage CC plank wired to a Domestic-Stability hit. (Aligns with the existing roadmap suffrage cluster — `roadmap.md` #xxx gender/suffrage / Tubman-condition items — this thread is a *live confirmation that the suffrage→DomStab consequence is intended at the CC, not just at later eras*.)

### Gov-action: ban slavery in your state, at a real reelection cost (POST 1 #4 Chittenden; POSTS 3-5 Irwin)
- POST 1 #4: Gov **Thomas Chittenden** (B1, CT) was **"the first Governor to ban slavery in their state."**
- POST 1 #4 + community thread: Gov **Jared Irwin** "followed suit by banning slavery in **Georgia of all places**" — i.e. a **Deep South** state — and "**He was ousted in the next primary election as well**." POSTS 3-5 single this out: a player asks why Irwin (who "abolish[ed] slavery in a deep south state, **even if it cost him re-election**") wasn't listed; the author calls it a top "shocking / history-changing event."
- Signal: a **Governor gov-action that abolishes slavery in a state**, flipping that state's slavery status, carrying a **genuine electoral cost** (ouster in the next primary) that scales with how pro-slavery the state is (CT = cheap; Deep-South GA = career-ending).
- **Build cross-ref:** per-state slavery is modeled as a flag — `State.isSlaveState: boolean` (`types.ts:1329`; populated e.g. `states1856.ts`; 1772 states carry it too per `lobbies-expertise-industry-historical-context.md`). The **abolish-slavery gov-action + its reelection-cost feedback** is the **b47 "Slavery" gov-action genre** + the per-state slavery flag (b47 digest, gap #288); this thread is independent 1772-era confirmation it's *in play* with the costed primary backlash.

### Senior General / Senior Admiral: promotion-by-performance + stripping for failure (POST 1 #1 Arnold, #2 Sumter)
- POST 1 #1: **Senior Admiral Esek Hopkins was stripped of his command after a disastrous encounter** with the British; **Arnold** then "executed a brilliant raid in Barbados which led to his **promotion to Senior Admiral** of the entire US Navy" (won 3 of 4 naval battles).
- POST 1 #2: **Thomas Sumter** "skills improved" through the war and "was recognized as the best General **as Sr. General Horatio Gates lost battles and his military skill dwindled**" — Sumter won 2 of 3 incl. the final battle (Bunker Hill).
- Signals: (a) **military skill changes with battle results** — rises on wins, **falls on losses**; (b) the **senior slot reassigns by performance** — a failing senior commander is **stripped / overtaken**, a strong performer is **promoted into the senior slot**.
- **Build cross-ref (SHIPPED):** `revolutionaryWar.ts` confirms both directions precisely:
  - **Loss degrades skill:** `applyBattleLoss` drops the commander's stats per `ABILITY_LOSS_RULES.battle.*` (naval-loss penalty for admirals line 203; ground-loss-by-tier + a campaign-level `majorityGroundLossAdmin` admin hit lines 233/243) — and can strip the **Leadership** trait on a loss (lines 144-151). This is exactly Gates's "military skill dwindled."
  - **Win earns skill:** survivors can gain Military (`runRevWarBattles` line 121, 10% per battle) and a majority-win campaign grants the senior general **+admin** (`applyBattleEarn` line 248) — the "skills improved" path.
  - **Senior-slot model:** `seniorGeneralId` / `seniorAdmiralId` + `generalIds`/`admiralIds` pools, auto-appointed best-first by `military` then `command` (`appointMilitary` lines 41-65). The narrative's "stripped → promoted into the senior slot" maps onto reassigning this id. *(One nuance to confirm: the shipped `appointMilitary` only fills an **empty** senior slot — it does not visibly re-rank an occupied slot when the incumbent's skill falls below a subordinate's. The retrospective shows **mid-war senior-slot turnover by performance** (Hopkins→Arnold, Gates→Sumter), which may rely on a GM hand-reassignment or a not-fully-shipped auto-demotion. Minor open question — see below.)*

### "Command" gate for the Presidency + faction-leadership churn (POST 1 #1 Arnold; (dis)Honorable Mention Middleton)
- POST 1 (dis)Honorable Mention: **Henry Middleton**, as faction leader of the Traditionalist Arch Federalists, "twice oversaw elections that ended with every faction candidate for Gov being defeated and no one appointed to Congress." As a result the faction "once again turned to the hero of the Revolution, **Benedict Arnold**, to lead them. **This resulted in Arnold finally earning command and enabling him to run for President.**"
- Two signals: (a) **becoming faction leader confers `command`**, and **command is the gate to presidential eligibility** ("finally earning command and enabling him to run for President"); (b) **faction-leadership churn** — factions **replace leaders after bad election cycles** (Arnold's faction cycled William Franklin → Henry Middleton → back to Arnold, POST 1 #1; Washington was likewise "replaced as faction leader by James Duane" as his faction drifted conservative, POST 1 #6).
- **Build cross-ref (SHIPPED):** the **command → presidential-eligibility gate** and **leader-pick → +1 Command** are the documented #153/#136 command-bootstrap mechanics (`game-mechanics.md` §17.3.y; the `ted1772` "doubled odds to gain command" + emergent-President audit). `constitutionalConvention.ts:158,168` also shows command-granting on CC honors (Father of the Constitution +1, Federalist authors +1). Confirms: **Command is never given at draft, must be earned forward, and is the hard gate to the Presidency** — this retrospective is another live witness (Arnold reaches the Presidency *only* after leadership finally yields command).

### War-funding / foreign-policy levers (POST 1 #8 Tillinghast; Robert Morris)
- POST 1 #8: **Thomas Tillinghast** (R5, RI; "the first Federalist" CC delegate), chair of the **Foreign Policy and Military Committee**, exercised a string of foreign/war levers:
  - **Thwarted "efforts to pay slave holders to convert slaves into soldiers (spending money we didn't have)"** — a rejected war-funding/slavery measure (cross-ref b47 slavery genre + a treasury/affordability constraint).
  - **Secured the Dutch loan** — "requesting a loan from the **United Provinces (Dutch)** to help keep the war effort funded."
  - **Treaties / diplomacy:** Treaty of Amity and Commerce with **Sweden**; appointed first **Minister to Spain**; an **official proclamation encouraging foreign soldiers to join** the cause — to which **Friedrich W. von Steuben** answered.
  - **Treaty of Paris:** led the envoy that signed it, **ceding land west of the Appalachians to the Mississippi**.
- **Build cross-ref (SHIPPED):**
  - **Dutch loan** is the shipped `dutch_recognition` era event (`eraEvents1772.ts:248`, "the 1782 Amsterdam loan — the only in-era finance fact"; resolver `phaseRunners.ts:3142` sets `graphFlags.loansEnabled`). Also the b48 Credit/Debt digest's "Secure Dutch Loan." Scenario boots with `diplomacy.Netherlands: 1` (`scenario1772.ts:82`). **Confirmed.**
  - **Treaty of Paris** is shipped `applyTreatyOfParis` (`revolutionaryWar.ts:279-297`) — ends the war as a US win, **grants western territories** (admit/expansion), disbands most of the army/navy but retains the senior general & admiral, and sets `diplomacy.Britain = -3`. Matches the narrative ("possession of large swaths of land … to the Mississippi"). **Confirmed.**
  - **Foreign Envoy / treaties** map to the CC "Foreign Envoy" action ("Send envoys to France, Spain, or the Netherlands," `continentalCongress.ts:241`) and the per-power `diplomacy` meters. von Steuben/foreign-soldiers proclamation and the Sweden treaty read as flavor over these systems (a foreign-volunteers proclamation isn't a separately verified shipped node — minor open question).
  - **"FA & Military Committee" chairmanship** as a *named* committee chair with foreign-policy authority is consistent with the CC committee model; the specific chair-power to **block a funding bill** ("thwart … pay slaveholders to convert slaves") is the committee-gate mechanic (consistent with the broader legislation/committee system; no extra verification needed for this digest).
