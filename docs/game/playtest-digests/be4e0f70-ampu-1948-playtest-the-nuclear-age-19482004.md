### 1. The era model has TWO LEVELS (refines, does not contradict, #92)

The thread settles a conflict the upstream partials created. The correct model
has **two distinct mechanics**, and an earlier extractor conflated them:

**(a) Historical Eras = the big point-banked content-bands** (the #92 mechanic).
This thread spans only TWO of them:
- **"Era of the Nuclear Age" = ONE Historical Era spanning 1948–2000.** The
  thread/scenario title "Nuclear Age 1948-2004" names this single band. (mid-C H1;
  POST 11287)
- **"Era of Terror" = 2000–~2012**, explicitly named and entered at a 2000
  boundary: "Welcome to 2000-2002 and to the Era of Terror (2000-2012)!" It is a
  **real band with its own rule deltas**, not flavor — the GM gates rules on "Now
  that we are in the Era of Terror…" / "From the Era of Terror on through the
  future…". (mid-C H1; POST 11295, 11307, 11421, 11724)
- **Downstream bands (Populists / Normalcy / Future) are NOT named in this
  thread** — only "the future" / "the 2030s" generically. (The successor thread
  `3a9ac985` then names **Era of Populism 2012–2024**.) (mid-C H1; POST 11421,
  11613)
- **Transition trigger = YEAR hitting 2000** (reconciled from a partial
  disagreement): an **end-of-era POINT-BANKING formula + standings reset** runs at
  the boundary (most points = +5; most vs other party = +3; whole winning party =
  +3; last/next-to-last allied faction = −1; non-banked points reset to 0; banked
  points carry to the end-of-GAME tally — "the scoring spine"), but the *trigger*
  to cross is the calendar year, not a manual ceremony. (mid-C H1; POST 11287,
  11295, 11637, 12027)

**(b) Decade / census boundaries WITHIN an era** (1972, 1980, 1990) = a SEPARATE,
schedule-fired mechanic. Each census does THREE things together:
1. **Census EV reallocation** state-by-state vs the prior census (e.g. 1972: CA

   column (HistEVs tab cols U/V). (mid-B B; POST 6374–6377, 8361–8364; mid-C S2;
   POST 8410, 8547, 10008–10010)
2. **Wholesale state-bias re-lean** on the Blue5..Tossup..Red5 scale (1970→72
   shifted ~30 states mostly toward Tossup; 1980 shifted 10 states almost all
   toward Red; 1990: GA Blue+2→Red+3, MS Tossup→Red+3, CA/NY/IL/MD bluer). (mid-B
   B; POST 6376, 8364; mid-C S2)
3. **Content rotation** — draft pool, lobby cards / "Faction Personalities", and
   era-activated industries (Alt-Energy, High-Tech) rotate. (mid-B B; POST 6538,
   6589–6592, 7443, 7654)

### 4. ★ The Cold War is NOT a system — it is the GENERIC WAR ENGINE RELABELED + event-flavor

The single biggest structural finding, re-confirmed across all 156 chunks.
**Despite the "Nuclear Age" title there is NO purpose-built Cold-War /
containment / nuclear subsystem.** What exists:
- **The same naval→land d100 battle engine** that runs in every era, relabeled
  with era-appropriate battle/war names. It ran: **US–Spain/Franco** (~8 in-game
  yrs; "across all playtests it's always been Spain" — a misfit legacy war
  spawned by a bad-relations roll), then the **"Korean War"** (explicitly "our
  timeline's Vietnam quagmire", dragged to its **~17th–19th year** in place of
  Vietnam), then anachronistic **GOP-opened wars** (Invasion of Cuba, Cambodia/
  Mayaguez, Somalia, Rwanda), then **Gulf War** (Iraq-invades-Kuwait EraEvo), then
  the **Early War on Terror**. (mid-A C1; mid-B D/§War; mid-C H2/S10; POST
  494–500, 4851, 5422–5424, 6142, 6354–6358, 8236, 8331)
- **Battle resolution formula (stable across all eras/bands):**
  ```
  Chance% = base − Difficulty (Easy 0 / Moderate −10 / Difficult −25)
          + (Sec-Defense skill + Chair-JCoS / Chief-of-Staff or Chief-of-Naval-Ops skill)
          + (battle officer Command × 10)   ← a 5-command general = +50
          + Military Preparedness (+5..+15)
          + Benchmarks (+10/+15)
  ```
  d100 under Chance = WIN (officer +1 Military / Military Leadership / loses
  Obscure); LOSS = −1 Military, often gains **Incompetent** and is auto-fired, and
  **generals/admirals can be KIA on a loss-roll**. After each battle d100 vs 50
  decides whether another is fought this phase. **Momentum** compares this phase's
  war-score to last; **war-end check** = `total war score × end-multiplier (0.5
  losing / 1 / 2 winning) × %` — odds so low **wars never resolve on their own**
  (Korean War ran ~two decades). **Overseas wars require winning the NAVAL phase
  before the ground phase.** (mid-A C1; mid-B §War; POST 116, 4851, 5094, 5427,
  5732, 6354, 6789, 7089–7090, 7514, 7519, 7730–7733, 8332–8333)
- **Wars CAN be lost, with lasting penalties.** US LOST the US-France War
  (war-score −7 → 70% loss roll hit → **Treaty of Paris**: President permanent −1
  all future elections, Party Pref −3, JCoS chair permanent −1 + −1 Military). War
  drains crisis meters every Lingering phase and can exhaust the general pool. A
  fresh war gives an initial **+Party-Pref patriotic boost to the incumbent** that
  decays if it drags. (mid-A C1; mid-B §War/D; POST 5620, 6070, 6354–6358, 6357)
- **★ DESIGN HOLE — no military branches.** Army generals command navies and vice
  versa; "naval" pols die in infantry attacks. Repeatedly flagged; no army/navy/
  air separation. (mid-A C1; POST 1101–1102, 1234)
- **★ End of Cold War is NOT modeled — the USSR NEVER FALLS.** The clock crosses
  1982→2005 but there is **no fall-of-USSR, no German reunification, no NATO, no
  MAD, no nuclear-exchange subsystem, no "peace dividend".** The USSR is still a
  **live, named ally + diplomatic actor in 2004–2005** (a sitting **Ambassador to
  the Soviet Union**; the Soviets **launch Mir** as a current event; the

  first event in that chain." Cold-War-end triggers are also buggy (only
  "Dissolution of the USSR" or the "Soviet Alliance" event explicitly ends

  11398, 11466, 11706, 11750, 11752)
- **NATO / détente / SALT / Berlin / Watergate are one-off A/B events, not

### 5. ★ The realignment = mechanically-but-GRADUALLY enforced (NOT a single scripted flip)

The dominant strategic story of the campaign, and a resolution of an upstream
"forced vs gradual" ambiguity. Designer's own framing: the ideological resorting
"began in 1964 (arguably the 1950s) and concluded in the early 1990s" — a
decade-by-decade gradual process. Enforced via **four cooperating levers**:
1. **Era-locked draft-pool ideology scarcity** — the per-era rookie roster starves
   Cons/Trad/RW-Pop slots; factions drift Moderate over time; Dixiecrats → Mods. A
   faction loses a non-adjacent ideology because (a) you keep the ideology you have
   most of and (b) you can't hold non-adjacent ideologies without the middle one;
   the leftover card is reassigned to a random faction. The SAME person can get a
   different draft-ideology in a new band. ("Ted Cruz will be drafted Red
   regardless.") (mid-A D; mid-B A; POST 31, 1612, 1620–1623, 3524, 3532–3534,
   6538)
2. **Enthusiasm-gated one-directional disgruntled-pol party-flips** (conversion
   step 2.1.6) — a pol auto-flips parties (25%) when its faction's enthusiasm hits
   **max the opposite way** (e.g. a Lib pol in a faction at Blue +3);
   **high/max-enthusiasm factions are SHIELDED** and cannot be poached. Direction
   is one-way toward modern polarity (liberal Republicans → Dems; Cons/Trad
   Democrats → GOP). (mid-A D; mid-B A; POST 3485, 3492–3496, 5497, 5966)
3. **Pinned party-preference** — GOP held at **Blue +3** for the realigning band so
   the sort has a stable gradient to run down. (mid-B A; POST 4881)
4. **Per-decade CENSUS state-bias doc (NOT era tables)** + a **"Revision to Mean"**
   centering damper applied every Lingering phase. (mid-B A/§Roster; POST 4879–4883,
   6677, 7018, 7427, 8237)

## CIVIL RIGHTS — distributed across systems, never a standalone mechanic

mechanics" rather than a bespoke subsystem:

   GOP-Taft faction even passed "Ban Racial Segregation in schools, employment,

3. **SCHEDULE-FIRED SCOTUS docket that ignores in-game history:** Hernandez v US,
