## 2. POST 1 — themed clusters (representative examples, NOT the full ~190)

Each cluster maps to the game era(s) it would touch. `docs/game/historical-context.md`
(+ era companions `-1820`, `-1948`, `-1960`) **already covers the real history
era-by-era** — this digest **references** that coverage and flags where Kagan's
items would *enrich* or *diverge from* it; it does **not** re-narrate history.

### Cluster A — Early-republic foreign policy & navy (Founding / Federalist, ~1788–1800)
Touches: **1772 founding scenario** + Federalist band (`historical-context.md`
Founding/Federalist sections).
- Hamilton declares **France a national-security menace** (1796); argues US falls
  if UK falls (no US navy, sheltered under RN). [POST 1]
- **John Adams + PM Pitt work out a US-UK defense plan** "not equaled until WWI." [POST 1]
- **Hamilton's hemispheric-invasion scheme** — serious about invading all Spanish
  territory incl. South America if Spain/France declare war; UK would let the US
  take the Floridas + Louisiana for joining vs France. [POST 1]
- Hamilton **conspires with South American revolutionaries** to liberate the
  continent from Spain (never launches). [POST 1]
- **Hamilton pushes a 1st-class navy in the 1790s**; "Manifest Destiny" + Clay's
  "American System" proposed by Hamilton as early as 1787. [POST 1]
- **Indian-policy moral asides** — Knox blames white settlers for tribal unrest /
  affirms tribal natural rights; Washington calls US treatment of tribes "a blot
  on the nation's character." [POST 1] (Enriches the founding-era Native-relations
  thread, which `historical-context.md` already touches.)

### Cluster B — Barbary / Quasi-War / Louisiana (Jeffersonian, ~1800–1815)
Touches: **early game / 1800-start band** (`historical-context.md` Jeffersonian
section). NOTE: **none of these ship as events** (codebase-verified, §5).
- **Jefferson's "United Nations of naval defense"** — proposed an international
  anti-piracy league (Portugal, Sweden, Denmark, Malta, Venice, Naples aboard;
  France lukewarm). [POST 1] — a striking *alt-diplomacy* primitive.
- **Barbary War** — Jefferson goes to war rather than cede the Mediterranean. [POST 1]
- **Louisiana transfer crisis** — US factions push to *invade* Louisiana the moment
  it passes Spain→France; Jefferson fears the transfer drives the US toward the UK;
  Northern-Federalist opposition to the Purchase framed as partisan. [POST 1]
- **Haitian Revolution policy reversal** — Adams aids the rebels (weakens France,
  pleases antislavery Federalists); Jefferson reverses (fears copycat slave
  revolts); **US won't recognize Haiti until Lincoln**. [POST 1] — strong
  branching-event / slavery-meter hook.

### Cluster C — Monroe Doctrine & Latin-American independence (Era of Good Feelings, ~1815–1828)
Touches: the **1820-era band**; **`historical-context-1820-democracy.md` is the
direct companion** — cross-ref it, don't duplicate.
- **The Holy-Alliance / 1823 Bourbon-restoration plot** — France/Spain/Austria
  scheme to plant Bourbon monarchies in the New World (French navy first-strike at
  Buenos Aires); London + DC discover it; this drives the Monroe Doctrine. [POST 1]
- **UK alliance offer → Monroe Doctrine** — UK proposes a joint anti-Holy-Alliance
  guarantee; **JQ Adams talks Monroe into going it alone** (the doctrine's origin),
  over a cabinet (Calhoun/Rush/Jefferson/Madison) that leaned pro-UK-alliance. [POST 1]
- **The road-not-taken price** — accepting the UK plan would have meant the US
  **renouncing Cuba and Texas forever**; Jefferson/Madison/Calhoun found that
  acceptable. [POST 1] — a clean *alt-history fork*.
- **Greek-independence intervention debate** — Clay/Gallatin/Calhoun want a US
  squadron sent; JQ Adams kills it. [POST 1]
- **Panama Congress fiasco** — JQ Adams wants a delegation; the South blocks funds
  because Haiti would seat Black delegates as equals; Buchanan's counter-resolution
  would have *abolished the Monroe Doctrine* (voted down). [POST 1]
- **Bolívar / Brazil-monarchy recognition** — US distrusts Bolívar (feared a "Latin
  American Caesar"); recognizes monarchical Brazil (1824) anyway. [POST 1]

### Cluster D — Slavery-expansion: Texas / Cuba / Mexico / Oregon (Antebellum, ~1828–1860)
Touches: the **1856 antebellum scenario** (the densest cluster — ~50 bullets);
`historical-context.md` Jacksonian + Antebellum sections.
- **Texas annexation arc** — JQ Adams *deliberately passes up* Texas (fears multiple
  slave states); Monroe/Jackson/Van Buren also wary of sectional tension; **Tyler
  reframes annexation as saving the South from abolitionists**; UK offers
  independent Texas money to abolish (Texas refuses → fuels Southern annexation
  drive). [POST 1]
- **Cuba acquisition saga** — JQ Adams eyes Havana from the 1810s; Buchanan tries
  to *buy* Cuba under Polk; the **López filibuster expeditions** (Quitman, 1852-53);
  Pierce's secret purchase scheme (dropped when exposed, links to **Kansas-Nebraska**
  as compensation to the South); **Buchanan asks Congress for Cuba-purchase money
  (1859)**. [POST 1]
- **All-Mexico / Southward filibustering** — Brown (MS) wants slave labor in
  conquered Mexican/Central/South-American mines; Quitman fears UK/France grabs
  Mexico; **Gadsden Purchase** (Pierce) buys a Southern Pacific rail route. [POST 1]
- **Polk's war-by-provocation** — Polk sends troops into the disputed zone to
  provoke Mexico (Kagan + most scholars); appoints **Whig generals (Scott, Taylor)**
  to make the war look national. [POST 1]
- **Oregon "All-Oregon" fight** — Polk baffled it isn't popular; Northern Whigs +
  Southern Democrats unite to oppose (Webster + Calhoun rare allies). [POST 1]
- **Containment framing** — Kagan repeatedly analogizes the slavery fight to the
  Cold War (free diversified North vs decaying authoritarian South; "containment").
  [POST 1] — useful for *event flavor/framing*, not a mechanic.
- **Crittenden / secession-prevention** — Douglas (1860) sees buying Mexico/Cuba as
  the way to stop secession; **Lincoln rejects Crittenden** (won't allow slavery's
  expansion). [POST 1]

### Cluster E — Pacific turn & early Asia contact (spans Antebellum → Gilded Age)
Touches: bridges the **1856** and **1868+** bands; thin in `historical-context.md`.
- **Tyler extends the Monroe Doctrine to Hawaii** + opens China trade → immediate
  annexation talk, **blocked because Hawaii sits below the Missouri Compromise line**
  (potential slave state). [POST 1] — a sharp territory×slavery gate.
- **Seward as Pacific visionary** — Midway acquisition; predicts the Philippines as
  the Pacific "trade pivot"; expects eventual annexation of all Canada + a capital
  relocated to Mexico. [POST 1]
- **Perry's 1853 Japan expedition**; US backs Britain in the **Opium Wars (1841)**;
  Japan seen as "most progressive Asian nation." [POST 1]

### Cluster F — Civil-War-era diplomacy & post-war expansion (Reconstruction, ~1861–1877)
Touches: **1868-era band**; `historical-context.md` Civil-War/Reconstruction +
Gilded Age sections.
- **European opportunism during the war** — Spain retakes Santo Domingo; **France
  invades Mexico and installs Maximilian**; in 1865 the US uses its war-swollen army
  to push France out. [POST 1] — natural *era-event triggered by the CW band*.
- **Alaska purchase (Seward)**; Russia relations sour afterward; Blaine later seeks a
  league to pressure Russia over pogroms. [POST 1]
- **Santo Domingo annexation push (Johnson + Grant)** — popular in the Senate but
  **Sumner single-handedly kills it** (Grant-corruption + would seal Haiti's fate +
  Democrats balk at admitting a majority-Black populace). [POST 1]
- **Alabama Claims / "give us Canada"** — Sumner wants the UK to *cede Canada* as
  compensation for aiding the Confederacy. [POST 1]
- **Lincoln finally recognizes Haiti** (during the war, no Southern delegations to
  block it). [POST 1]
- **Danish West Indies** — Grant opposes the purchase purely because it was Seward's
  idea. [POST 1]

### Cluster G — Gilded Age navy buildup, Hawaii, Samoa, Latin-American rivalry (~1877–1896)
Touches: **late-1868 / Gilded Age band**; `historical-context.md` Gilded Age section.
- **The navy-buildup ladder** — JQ Adams's "gradual improvement of the navy" bill
  killed by Jacksonians → Jackson/Van Buren anti-navy → **1873 Virginius Affair = the
  wake-up call** → Garfield's naval planning board (68 ships proposed, **Arthur trims
  to 4 steel ships**) → **Harrison's first 3 battleships (1890)** + the "Billion-Dollar
  Congress" → Tracy's two-ocean fleet. [POST 1] — a clean **tiered/escalating-program
  ladder** (the #221 "escalating ladder with prereq chains" shape; see §4).
- **Relative-naval-weakness datapoints** — US ranked **12th** (behind Austria-Hungary,
  Ottomans, China); Chile's navy *superior* to the US in the 1860s-80s; 1896 US had
  5 battleships vs UK 45 / France 29 / Germany 21. [POST 1] — concrete numbers for a
  *military-preparedness meter*.
- **Hawaii annexation arc (multi-decade)** — Seward's 1867 treaty → Fish passes it
  (1875) → Cleveland's Pearl Harbor naval-base rights (1887) → Harrison lands troops
  (1889) → **1893 overthrow of the Queen + provisional govt requests annexation →
  Cleveland blocks the treaty (anti-imperialist)** → Hawaii self-declares a republic
  (July 4 1894). [POST 1]
- **Samoa crisis (US-Germany-UK)** — German encroachment → competing gunboats →
  **the 1889 typhoon obliterates the fleets before a battle** → Blaine + Bismarck
  partition agreement. [POST 1]
- **Latin-American rivalry & Pan-Americanism** — Blaine revives JQ Adams's Pan-American
  dream (**1889 conference**, free-trade bloc rejected by Argentina); Venezuela asks to
  be a US protectorate (1883); **Chilean crisis (1891-92, Baltimore affair)** — Harrison
  demands an apology, readies for war, Chile backs down. [POST 1]
- **Canal politics** — Clay's neutral-canal call (1826) → Clayton-Bulwer (1850, joint
  US-UK) → Grant's "American canal on American soil" → Frelinghuysen's unratified
  Nicaragua treaty (1884). [POST 1]

### Cluster H — Spanish-American War run-up (~1896–1898)
Touches: just past the late Gilded Age band, toward **a 1896 era** (the genres'
"Era of Hollywood / 1896-1932 hinge" — see gap #206); `historical-context.md`
Progressive/WWI section is the nearest companion.
- **Cuban-revolt cycle** — five uprisings 1879-1895; recurring Spanish-atrocity press
  for decades; Cleveland sacrifices US investments rather than fight. [POST 1]
- **McKinley's reluctant slide to war** — repeated reform deadlines ignored; **USS
  Maine explodes (Feb 1898)**; Congress moves to declare war (or recognize the rebels)
  **with or without McKinley**; effigy-burnings; war declared April 25. [POST 1]
- **"Most popular war in US history"** — Kagan's framing; near-zero domestic
  opposition; even Bryan raises a regiment. [POST 1]
- **The accidental empire** — McKinley never planned to annex Cuba and **did not
  anticipate gaining the Philippines**. [POST 1] (POST 1 ends here — book 1 stops
  ~1898.)
