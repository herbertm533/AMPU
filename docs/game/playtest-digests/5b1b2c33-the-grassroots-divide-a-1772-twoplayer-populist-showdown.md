## 2. ★ Military Preparedness hard-capped at 2 during the Era of Independence (NEW founding rules/balance gap)
- **The genuinely-new finding.** MilPrep starts at 2 and **cannot rise above it until the Age of Federalism** because the meter's tier prerequisites are mis-ordered: MilPrep 3-4 require the **Militia Act** (a federalism-era bill, ~1792 IRL) while the **auto-forced 1774 war bills (Continental Army / Continental Navy)** are wired to the HIGHER tiers (5-6). So *every* MilPrep roll from the forced opening war legislation is wasted — "until the Constitution exists, AFTER the war is over, military preparedness can never increase, rendering all war legislation completely pointless… It's a glaring issue" (Cal, POST 88-90; Ted confirms the cap exists, POST 86/88). MilPrep being stuck at 2 = a permanent Military Preparedness CRISIS for the whole founding era and a recurring −1 drag.
- **Cal's proposed fix (used live as a house rule, flagged for V):** reverse the MilPrep 1-4 prerequisite ordering so Continental Army→MilPrep 3 and Continental Army+Navy→MilPrep 4 (treating Continental Army/Navy as equivalent to Standing Army/Navy), capping the founding era at MilPrep 4-5, so the forced war bills actually help. Throughout the run MilPrep is shown "capped at 5 per the suggested rules" (POST 121, 252). **Build TODO: audit the founding-era MilPrep ladder prerequisites — the auto-forced war legislation must be able to move the meter, or the 1772 RevWar's forced bills are dead rolls and the era sits in permanent military crisis.** (Pairs with #56/#155 war engine + #67 meter caps + the founding boot.)
