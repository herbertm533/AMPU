## 1) The 6 ABILITIES (0–5) — earn / use / loss SHAPE (POSTs 1–6)

Canonical set is **Command, Legislative, Governing, Judicial, Administrative,
Military**. ★ **There is NO "backroom" ABILITY** — Backroom is a career *track*, not a
0–5 ability. Granted "at random % chance unless specified."

| Ability | EARN (shape — not every bullet) | USE | LOSS |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Command** | anytime-evos; become **faction leader / party leader**; **leave any career track except Backroom**; become **protégé of a Master Kingmaker** (guaranteed); committee chair / ranking member; **President of the Continental Congress**; **initial appt to Secretary of State**; Sr General/Admiral while a **major war active**; national/UN ambassador to an enemy nation at war; successful event implementation (unless Efficient handles it); succeed at a gov-action (non-guaranteed, not at Gov 5) +again if it improves a meter; propose **crisis legislation** that's signed; successful filibuster; legislation that improves a meter; **win a battle**; win majority of battles in the phase; win a major war (to every active general/admiral) | caps **executive actions/half-term**; caps **sways** during Congress-in-session; caps **actions in a pres primary/general** (noted "homebrew", not official) | anytime-evos; **old-age rolls** |
| **Legislative** | anytime-evos; **Legislative track (primary)**; all tracks but Backroom & Judicial (secondary); congressional leadership; committee/chair appt; faction leader; **re-elected** party leader; winning **difficult** battles | gate to **House/Senate/ContCong**; 50%+1 in Senate elections if highest-ability candidate; **≥2 to hold Chair/Ranking**; **≥2 to filibuster**; sometimes gates exec-action implementation | anytime-evos; old-age; **losing moderate/easy** battles |
| **Governing** | anytime-evos; **Governing/State-Exec track (primary)**; all other tracks; faction/party leader; improve a meter w/ gov-action; winning **difficult or medium** battles | gate to **governorship**; sometimes gates exec-actions; **enact state laws (each Gov = +20% cumulative to enact gov-actions)** | old-age; anytime-evos; losing moderate/easy battles |
| **Judicial** | anytime-evos; **Judicial track (primary)** ONLY | **SCOTUS appointment**; exec-action implementation | old-age; anytime-evos |
| **Administrative** | **Admin track (primary)**; all tracks but **Legislative** (secondary); faction leader; anytime-evos; **cabinet confirmation (DOUBLED if Egghead, Efficient; STACKS if both)**; winning **difficult/moderate** battles; majority of battles in phase; **2+ terms as Governor**; **2+ terms as Senator** | **≥2 for cabinet** (unless no other eligibles); **≥3 for DCIA/DNI, DFBI, UN Amb**; primary implementer for actions/event-responses; moves meters in **Lingering phase**; battle planning phase; **+1 in primaries** (w/ econ crisis + econ expertise); credibility appeal at convention | anytime-evos; old-age; **Easily Overwhelmed** (if retained); losing moderate/easy battles; losing majority of phase battles |
| **Military** | **Military track**; anytime-evos; **2 Rev-War-era evos**; winning **difficult/medium** battles | drives **Military Preparedness** chart in Lingering; **≥2 for Sr Gen/Army CoS & Sr Adm/CNO**; **≥3 auto-confirmed** | anytime-evos; **losing difficult/medium/easy** battles; old-age |

★ Recurring pattern: **win a battle → ability up; lose a battle → ability down** (tier-
sensitive); **old-age rolls** erode all six; **anytime-evos** both grant and remove.
"Leave the career track" is a major grant event for Command/Legis/Gov/Admin.

## 2) The 19 EXPERTISES (POSTs 7–9) — exact set, gain & use

Agriculture · Business · Economics · Education · Energy · Environment · Foreign
Affairs · Healthcare · Housing · Justice · Labor · Media · Military · Naval · Science
· Technology · Trade · Transportation · Welfare. ("what the politician studied in
higher education, or worked their way up a job's ranks.")

- **GAIN:** leaving career tracks · **re-elected as governor** · committee appointment
  · cabinet-office or ambassadorship appointment/reappointment.
- **USE (3):** (1) **eligibility for cabinet appointments** (primary use); (2)
  **determining FACTION IDEOLOGIES**; (3) gating **certain governor-actions**.

## 3) The 57 TRAITS (rules 3.0.19-2; POST 10) — catalog

Post declares **57 traits**; it catalogs ~55 by name (says "as many as I can fit").
Descriptions compressed; **mechanically-loaded** traits flagged ★ with their effect.

**Election-swing traits:** **Charisma**★ (cross-party appeal; some voters pick charisma
over party), **Likable**★ (esp. vs Unlikable opponent), **Unlikable**★/**Uncharismatic**
(energize opposition / wooden), **Orator**★ (best speeches → undecided/bipartisan flood),
**Debater**★, **Propagandist**★ (mass-comm advocacy, can mislead), **Celebrity** (non-
political national name-rec), **Obscure**★ (unknown outside state), **Cosmopolitan** (+national/
−local races), **Provincial** (+local/−national), **Controversial**/**Two-Faced**/**Flipflopper**
(trust hits; FF can fade, evolving while FF risks Two-Faced), **Predictable** (comfort, but
stymied by Manipulative opponent), **Hale** (longevity, can hold office into 80s/90s),
**Frail** (death-prone), **Teflon** (deflects scandal fallout), **Late Bloomer** (slow then great),
**Overeager** (Rep@25/Sen@30/Pres@35), **Outsider**-adjacent.

**Kingmaker family:** **Kingmaker**★ (**+1 in state**; mentors a protégé; may never hold
office), **Master Kingmaker**★ (**+1 nationally**; many protégés → effectively controls a
state). **Leadership**★ — own deep-dive in §3b.

**Governance/cabinet competence:** **Iron Fist**★ (**control over subordinates / yes-men**;
great as Party Leader / Speaker / Maj Leader), **Efficient**★ (**extra gov-actions/proposals**;
handles events without spending Command), **Delegator**★ (cabinet-effect **multiplier UP**),
**Micromanager**★ (cabinet-effect **multiplier DOWN**; own chief-of-staff), **Egghead**★ (research/
fact; **doubles** cabinet admin grant), **Easily Overwhelmed**★ (perform tasks poorly; **erodes
Admin**), **Passive** (hands-off, lets advisors lead), **Pliable**/**Lackey** (easily converted /
parrots leader), **Disharmonious** (lashes out; cabinet officers may resign), **Harmonious**
(seeks middle ground), **Puritan** (no compromise; cult following, weak coalition leader),
**Manipulative**★ (switch a vote / sway an appointment — "one of the most useful").

**Crisis/war traits:** **Crisis Manager**★ (born to lead nation in crisis; **rare**),
**Crisis Admin**★ (generational cabinet officer in crisis), **Crisis Gov**★ (state-level crisis
contributor), **Decisive General**★ (rises to save the nation / war-ending victory), **Military
Leader** (recognized to lead whole military), **Naive Strategist**★ (no grand-strategy instinct),
**Geostrategist** (boosts State Dept as SecState).

**Domain-specialist traits (cabinet routing):** **Bookkeeper** (generational econ; born SecTreas),
**Numberfudger**★ (econ-incompetent; never SecTreas), **Lawful** (a.k.a. **"Cop"**; generational
prosecutor/defender; born **Attorney General**), **Illicit** (law doesn't apply to them; dangerous
as AG), **Jurisprudence** (generational legal mind; born judge), **Domestic Warrior**★ (extra-
effective in domestic admin posts), **Domestic Apathy**★ (neglects domestic; route to foreign/
military/trade), **Everyman** (working-class instinct vs egghead wonks), **Magician**★ (see below).

**Integrity axis:** **Integrity**★ (incorruptible; breaks party on moral lines; **opp-conditional
vs tainted**), **Controversial**★ (in danger vs Integrity opponent), **Incoherent** (gaffe-prone in
speech/debate).

**Sectional (1856-relevant):** **Carpetbagger**★ (just moved states; hard to establish), **Southern
Unionist**★ (Union > States' Rights; **will NOT secede** with Deep/Upper South or Southwest).

★ **Magician** (POST 10–12): "generational legislative strategist… legislative tricks even top
legislators can't do… shows promise even before mastering legislating." **Named after Van Buren
("The Little Magician"). RARE trait.** Q (Vee01, POST 11): "never seen it — still around?" →
zag (POST 12) lists the ~12 historical holders: **Elbridge Gerry, Aaron Burr, Van Buren, Stephen
A. Douglas, Joseph Cannon, Thomas Brackett Reed, John W. Kern, J. Hamilton Lewis, Joseph McCarthy,
LBJ, Mitch McConnell, Newt Gingrich** — i.e. it exists, just very scarce. Note "a couple traits
were removed a bit ago" (POST 11) — the trait list is versioned/churning.

★ **"Lawful" rename** (POSTs 14–15): 10centjimmy asks "did Lawful get dropped?" → zag: **"it's
still 'Cop' in the master sheet, renamed 'Lawful' in all other documentation."** Same trait, two
names depending on the artifact.

### 3b) Leadership trait deep-dive (POST 13)
- **GAIN:** ALL career tracks **except Judicial**; ContCong President; Speaker / Maj-Min Leader /
  Maj-Min Whip (only if congressional officers exist); Committee Chair / Ranking Member (if cong
  officers active); on a committee; become faction leader (if lacked it); become party leader;
  winning **difficult/medium** battles.
- **USE:** Kingmaker/Master-Kingmaker **+2 protégé limit**; become Speaker / Maj-Min Leader / Whip /
  Chair / Ranking / faction leader; become party leader **(100%)**; (as faction ldr) **compel SCOTUS
  retirements**; w/ Iron Fist **block primary challengers 90%** (opposition PL 50%); **+1 candidate
  strength**; **+1 in debate (+2 w/ Charisma)**; **33% to unilaterally change party rules at
  convention**; 33% to whip party into compliance (+1 momentum); **obscure VP can gain it on a 1/d6**;
  +1 in pres-general debate; 33% after losing the presidency by <60% EC (or PopVote if EC abolished);
  **20%/10%/5%** (Big/Medium/Small state) on election/re-election as governor.
- **LOSS:** unelected president who doesn't become party leader; losing medium/easy battles; old-age.

### 3c) Trait GAIN / USE / LOSS (POST 10, general)
- **GAIN:** coming off **all** career tracks; becoming a **protégé** (some exceptions); **winning OR
  losing** elections.
- **USE:** election impacts (above); Kingmaker +1 state / Master-Kingmaker +1 national; Efficient →
  extra gov-actions/proposals; Iron Fist → control subordinates.
- **LOSS:** **old age** (the *advantageous* ones fade); or **gain a conflicting trait → d6 roll** to
  lose the held one.

## 6) Cabinet structure (POST 21)

- **Big-4 / Upper Cabinet:** **State, Treasury, War (later Defense), Attorney General** — always face
  a **confirmation hearing** (Committee/Floor vote).
- **Lower Cabinet:** any "Secretary of …" created by legislation + **PMG** (once elevation bill enacted).
- **Cabinet-Level:** FBI, CIA/DNI, Bank Pres/**Fed Reserve Chair**, **UN Ambassador**, **National
  Security Advisor**, PMG (when initially created).
- **Ambassadors:** initially **Ministers** before elevation. Special **non-meter Ministers**: **Indian
  Sovereignty, Confederate States, Northern Confederacy** (petition→"Free States of America"), **Western
  America**.
- **Key Advisor:** a **distinct non-cabinet** position.

## BUILD-vs-DESIGN AUDIT (code @ `src/` HEAD 2026-06-29)

### Abilities — ★ "backroom" SKILL-vs-ABILITY discrepancy (NEW)
Code `SkillKey` (`types.ts:24–41`) = **7 keys**: `admin, legislative, judicial, military, governing,
**backroom**`. Canonical = **6 abilities, NO backroom ability** (Backroom is only a *track*; `CareerTrack`
includes `'Backroom'`, `types.ts:50`, and `TRACK_SKILL.Backroom='backroom'`, `:202`). → **The shipped
`Skills` type promotes Backroom to a first-class 0–5 ability the canonical spec does NOT recognize.**
This is a genuine naming/model discrepancy: code has **7 of the canonical 6** (one extra). The other 5
ability names match; canonical "Administrative/Command/Military" map to code `admin` / `command` (a
separate `Politician.command` field, `:1281`, not in `Skills`) / `military`.

### Expertise — EXACT MATCH (corroborates)
Code `Expertise` (`types.ts:182–192`) = **19 tags, identical set & spelling** to the canonical 19.
**19 of 19.** Engine WIRES all 3 canonical USES (corroborated, not just declared):
- Cabinet eligibility/scoring — `CABINET_SEAT_SCORING.expertiseBonus=5` + `pick.expertise.includes(primary)`
  (`phaseRunners.ts:2238–2239`); AG/SecWar/SecState event mults gate on expertise (`:2911–2966`).
- Faction-ideology — `EXPERTISE_IDEOLOGY_LEAN` (`types.ts:421–425`; Agriculture +1 / Business +0.5 /
  Labor −1) summed in faction-center calc (`phaseRunners.ts:717–718`). **PARTIAL**: only 3 of 19 tags
  carry a lean; canonical implies all expertises feed faction ideology.
- Grant paths — track-exit (`TRACK_EXPERTISE`), committee (`COMMITTEE_EXPERTISE`), cabinet/ambassador
  (`OFFICE_EXPERTISE`), lobby-trickle (`LOBBY_EXPERTISE`). **Re-elected-governor grant: NOT found** (the
  canonical "re-elected as governor" expertise gain is unimplemented).
- ★ **Gov-action gating use: NOT shipped** — there is no gov-action system at all (no `govAction` token),
  so the 3rd canonical USE ("gate certain gov-actions") has no consumer.

### Traits — ★ COUNT + ROSTER MISMATCH (corroborates trait-coverage gap, sharpened)
Code `Trait` union (`types.ts:62–117`) = **55 traits** vs canonical **57 declared** (~55 cataloged in-thread).
But it is a **substantially DIFFERENT roster**, not a near-subset:
- **41 overlap** (incl. renames: canonical **Charisma**→code `Charismatic`; **Flipflopper**→`Flip-Flopper`).
- **15 canonical traits ABSENT from code:** **Bookkeeper, Disharmonious, Easily Overwhelmed, Everyman,
  Geostrategist, Illicit, Incoherent, Jurisprudence, Lackey, Late Bloomer, Lawful (a.k.a. Cop),
  Military Leader, Pliable, Southern Unionist, Teflon.** ★ Notably **Easily Overwhelmed** is *referenced*
  in `ABILITY_LOSS_RULES`/Admin-loss design but is NOT in the Trait union — a dangling reference.
- **14 code traits NOT in the canonical catalog:** Ambitious, Corrupt, Failed Bid, Globalist, Ideologue,
  Impressionable, Incompetent, Loyal, Nationalist, Opportunist, Outsider, Reformist, Scandalous, Traitor.
  (Several are build-only mechanics: `Failed Bid`/`Ambitious` for leadership churn, `Loyal`/`Opportunist`/
  `Ideologue`/`Impressionable` for conversion & ideology-shift gating — design constructs with no canon entry.)
- ★ **Lawful/Cop is entirely absent** from code despite being the canonical AG-defining trait.
- Trait LOSS-by-conflict (canonical d6) IS shipped: `TRAIT_CONFLICTS` + `conflictD6Threshold:4`
  (`types.ts:639,658–692`); old-age fade IS shipped (`TRAIT_LIFECYCLE_RULES.fadingPool`, `:634`). Election
  impacts shipped richly (`TRAIT_ELECTION_EFFECTS`, `:738+`); governance impacts (`TRAIT_GOVERNANCE_EFFECTS`,
  `:1017+`). So the *machinery* matches canon; the *roster* diverges by ~28 traits across both directions.

### Lobby↔Expertise keying — PARTIAL (NEW vs code)
Canonical 18 lobbies → expertise. Code `LobbyCardId` (`types.ts:316–320`) = **15 era-flavored lobbies**
(Patriots/Merchants/…/Nativists), an ENTIRELY DIFFERENT naming scheme (1772/1856 scenario flavor, not the
modern "Big Ag / Wall St / Big Tech" canon). `LOBBY_EXPERTISE` (`:373–391`) keys only **7 of 15** to an
expertise (8 are `null`). → The canonical 18-lobby↔expertise table is the **modern-era** lobby taxonomy;
the shipped 15 are scenario-specific and only partially expertise-keyed. **Not a 1:1 — different lobby sets.**

### Lobby→Industry ±1 — PARTIAL (corroborates #294)
`LOBBY_INDUSTRY` (`types.ts:398–414`) maps the **15 shipped lobbies** to era-state industry keys
(Merchants→shipping/finance, SlavePower→cotton/tobacco, NorthernIndustry→manufacturing/coal, etc.). The
canonical map keys the **modern lobby names** to modern industries (Big Corps→Mining/Manufacturing, Wall
St→Finance, Labor Unions→−Mfg/−Mining). **Both express the same primitive (lobby nudges state industry ±1)
but over different lobby/industry vocabularies** — corroborates #294's lobby↔industry mechanic; the
canonical modern table is the unbuilt target taxonomy.

### Cabinet tiers — PARTIAL (corroborates cabinet system)
`OfficeType` (`types.ts:1111–1134`) ships the **Big-4** (State/Treasury/War/AG) + Navy/Interior/PMG +
GeneralInChief/Admiral + Ambassador. `cabinetSeatsForYear` (`:1196–1208`) era-gates seats (4→5→6→7 by
1789/1798/1829/1849) — matches the "created by legislation" spirit via hardcoded year transitions.
**MISSING vs canon:** the **Cabinet-Level tier** (FBI, CIA/DNI, Fed Chair, **UN Ambassador**, **NSA**) —
none are `OfficeType`s; the **special non-meter Ministers** (Indian Sovereignty, Confederate States,
Northern Confederacy, Western America) — absent; **Key Advisor** — absent. Confirmation-hearing flow exists
for cabinet (`CABINET_SEAT_SCORING`, cross-party gate `:1233`) but is auto-scored, not a Committee/Floor vote.
