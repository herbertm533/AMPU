## 3. Revolutionary War — battle-by-battle data (★ the #155 1772-RevWar-winnable evidence)
**This is the cleanest fresh-start RevWar trace in the KB. The war is genuinely HARD and very nearly loseable; it is propped up by THREE load-bearing safety mechanisms.** Battle formula confirmed (corroborates #56/#45): `Planning(SrGen/SrAdm Mil ×2) + Battlefield/Sea Command(Mil ×10, −1 if a replacement/Decisive adj) + MilPrep(+10/+15) + EraBenchmarks(+5→+15) + Difficulty(−10 to −25) → d100` (POST 265, 521, 689, 880, 1155).

**War record (the alarming part for #155):**
- **The Navy NEVER won a single battle at sea** across the entire war (~6+ naval battles, all lost — Sag Harbor/Little Egg Harbor/Cape Breton/Block Island/Cape Ann ×2, success% ~19-41%). "the Navy never won a single battle at sea" (POST 893). The colonies started with **NO Navy at all** ("Whoops, we don't have a Navy. Small oversight," POST 265).
- The Army won ~half its **land** battles, incl. 2 difficult ones (Brandywine 51%, White Plains 63%, Long Island 48%, Valley Forge 50%) — and lost many (Germantown 31%, Fort Washington 43%, Yorktown 42%).
- **War Score hovered at −2/−1** the whole war; each phase rolled ~**20-30% to win** (and ~20% to LOSE outright at a −2 score). Won on a **30% roll, pre-1782** (POST 888, 893) — "got lucky on the roll."
- **The parallel Invasion of Canada was a TOTAL LOSS** — every battle (1 naval + 3 land) failed, **80% → war lost outright** (POST 523-531). "Out of all the 1772 playtests, I don't think a single battle has been won in Canada" (POST 526). Loss package applied (corroborates #152): Ward + Barry got **−1 Mil + Incompetent + auto-fired + permanent −1 in all elections** (POST 523, 531). Confirms **concurrent multi-war** (#45) — RevWar + Canada ran in the same half-terms.
- **Officer churn was brutal:** ~6 different Senior Admirals cycled through (a generals/admirals "threshing machine," POST 887); officers KIA mid-battle (Richard Taylor at Germantown POST 265; McDougall at Block Island POST 670); Benedict Arnold turned coat and was **removed from the game** (Turncoat General event, POST 828). Senior-General promotions: any General auto-accepts Senior General w/o confirmation (POST 819).

**★ The three RevWar-winnability safety mechanisms (the #155 floor in practice):**
1. **French Alliance = the war becomes UNLOSEABLE.** Achieved via the Ambassador to France → Treaty of Amity & Commerce event: "It is no longer possible for us to lose the Revolutionary War!" (POST 630). **V ruled the France meter is pinned at ally until the war ends** — a France-relations-drop event can't undo it (POST 632). This is the decisive lever: get an ambassador to France early → the −2 war score stops threatening game-over.
2. **The 2/3 (Articles of Confederation) vote threshold saved the game from a peace vote.** The **Carlisle Peace Commission** ("yay" = game-over, colonies rejoin Britain) got **5 Aye / 4 Nay = 55.5% < 66.6%** — peace FAILED, war continued despite a *majority* for peace (POST 461). A 2nd game-over peace event (Conciliatory Resolution) was likewise rejected (POST 637).
3. **Ted's new "CPU opposes game-over 75%" rule** (POST 638) — added precisely because **CPUs lean toward peace** and could end a mostly-CPU game. Explicit design concern: "I would guess most players will be single player, and if the CPU constantly leans towards peace, there is going to be a lot of frustration… I plan to flag it for V's review… give points to most ideologies to be opposed to peace and 90% of the time that's the way the CPU will swing" (POST 459-460).

**★ Takeaway for #155:** the 1772 RevWar from a fresh start is **genuinely losable** (−2 score, 20% loss rolls, an entire side-war lost) and **only stays winnable because of the French-alliance unloseable-flag + the 2/3 peace threshold + Ted's anti-game-over CPU rule.** Any war-engine hardening (#155's enemy-strength term / battle-size weighting / Officer-Mil cap) **must preserve all three of these floors**, or a 1772 game can end before federalism. This is the concrete data the #155 counter-constraint asked for.

**The alt "War of 1812 in 1782" branch (★ corroborates DH-61 / oopscpu directly):**
- Post-war, the **NW Indian War** (Huron Confederacy event) was offered. CPU George Washington (a debater) swung **5 states against** declaring war (POST 1010). Ted then offered the loser-fork: **forfeit OH/IN/MI/IL/WI** (temporary — "It can fire 3 times," a **house rule added because CPU-dominated games kept declining the NW Indian War**, POST 1011/1021) **OR declare war directly on Britain = "kicking off the War of 1812 in 1782"** (POST 1010). **Humans chose to forfeit the NW Territory** ("do we really need the Midwest?", POST 1023-1024). **Directly corroborates DH-61** ("we've encountered this exact issue before, where cpu said no to the NW Indian war in a CPU-dominated game. That's when we added the 'three chances' rule," POST 1021) and confirms V added a later event to re-attempt the territory.
