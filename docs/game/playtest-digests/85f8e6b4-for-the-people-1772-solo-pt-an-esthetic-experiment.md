**Forum:** politicslounge.com topic 6618 ("For the People: 1772 Solo PT — An Esthetic Experiment")
**Player / narrator:** `@Saucialiste` — runs the game *himself* (no external GM). `@ebrk85` (the rules author), `@Arkansas Progressive`, `@matthewyoung123`, `@OrangeP47`, `@Vee01`, `@10centjimmy` chime in as kibitzers/rules-clarifiers.
**Read posture:** This is the single most build-relevant thread we have. It is a **single-player 1772 run** framed as an **"aesthetic experiment"** (post 1): the player is *prototyping how the eventual computerized AMPU should look and feel* while hand-running the rules in spreadsheets + image editors. The shipped game IS a single-player 1772 scenario, so this is the closest forum analog to the actual product. Treat the presentation ideas as **design intent for the presentation layer**, and the hand-run mechanics as a reference implementation of 1772.

## Thread scope

- **Years covered:** 1772 → 1780. Four half-terms: 1772–74 (posts 2–12), 1774–76 (15–25), 1776–78 (26–49), 1778–80 (52–90). Era: **Independence** throughout; never reaches the Constitutional Convention / Federalism (the player notes he'll review the era at its end, post 68 — the thread cuts off mid-Independence at "[To be continued]").
- **Solo confirmation:** Post 1 — *"I will also make use of the unlimited power bestowed by a solo RP to fiddle and tinker with rules or CPU workings."* The player **runs every faction**, including his own "Moderate Patriots," and explicitly hand-executes the CPU rules (and rewrites them as he goes). This is exactly the shipped single-player model — one human faction, engine-driven opponents — except here the human IS the engine.
- **Why it exists (post 1):** *"One day, this wonderful game is meant to be fully computerized… So I started thinking about how I would want the game to present me my choices so I'm able to make them more informed."* The thread's purpose is to **generate discussion about making AMPU "the best it can be"** as a piece of software.
- **How the player narrates:** Each post is a **named Chapter** ("Washington Blues," "The Pennsylvanian Exodus," "Death in Yellow," "The Dark Congress," "Game Over?") with a planned chapter list up front (post 1). Within a chapter he walks the **numbered phase taxonomy in order** (§2.1.1 … §2.10), marking `(skipped)` phases explicitly, narrating each result in-character ("Why keep Franklin around the capital when he can go live the beach life on the Jersey shores," post 3), and posting **custom graphics** (score sheets, sport-card infoboxes, war-battle cards, governor-election tabs, flags, AI illustrations).

## AESTHETIC / UX / PRESENTATION SIGNAL (the headline)

This is the thread's reason to exist. None of the below is in the shipped UI as a deliberate visual language — the app renders functional pages, not this "old-school fantasy-sports almanac" aesthetic. Treat each as a presentation-layer requirement candidate.

### The thesis (posts 13, 14, 23, 24)
The player's stated north star: **"A political sim, but with many trappings of sports management games. Or maybe more like fantasy sport, with how central building and wielding your faction is. But also with an old school vibe."** (post 13). Inspirations named: **Crusader Kings 2** (character sheets, *and* CK2-style **procedural portrait generation** for randomly-generated/obscure politicians, post 51, 860), the **AMPU Kickstarter concept art**, and **trading/sport cards**. Other players are explicitly excited about it as the "best test for graphic design… the maps will be glorious" (post 23).

### Concrete presentation conventions observed
1. **Politician "sport-card" infobox** (posts 13, 14): a per-politician character card with portrait, traits, stats, PV, office — built for *faction leaders* first. Community reaction: "I love this design!" (post 14). **Delta:** the build has **no portrait/image field on `Politician`** (grep of types.ts: no `portrait/image/avatar` field) and no card view; `Roster.tsx` is a table/list.
2. **Persistent score sheet / leaderboard** rendered as an image every turn (posts 15 "Here a look at the score sheet," 22, 53 "I forgot the Scoreboard!"). Per-faction cumulative points are the spine of feedback. Build has scoring + `HalfTermSummary` (types.ts:1762) but no always-on visual scoreboard styled this way.
3. **Color-coding as a first-class convention.** The player legends his graphics by ideology color (post 12: *"Red : Prog, Pink : Lib, Purple : Mod, Light Blue : Cons, Blue : Trad, Dark Blue : RW Pop"*; restated post 25). Congress composition is narrated by these colors (post 25: "more liberals (pink), moderates (purple)…"). GMs independently confirm color-coding is core to *playing* the game (post 70–71: "color coding every freshman congress-critter while I'm doing elections" is the key to making Committees tractable). **Requirement:** a **canonical ideology→color palette** used consistently across roster, congress, maps, score sheets.
4. **Military battles rendered as a "battle card"** with an itemized to-hit breakdown (posts 22, 48, 60): `Moderate battle (-10) / Planning (6) / Commodore Artemas Ward (30) / Meters (0) / Military Benchmarks (10) / Probability of victory : 36% / FIGHT! 94/36 DEFEAT`. He iterates on the *visual shading* of these cards across posts ("I feel a bit of shading is making the words pop," 48; "This is quite a nice level of shading, isn't?," 60). The build has a Rev-War dashboard (`RevWarDashboard.tsx`) — open question whether it surfaces this kind of legible additive odds breakdown.
5. **Governor-election tab** rendered per-state with period-accurate office titles (post 25): the player deliberately researches the *correct historical title* per colony — "Governor of Connecticut," "1st President of Delaware," "1st President of the Massachusetts Governor's Council," "1st President of the Supreme Executive Council of Pennsylvania," "1st President of the New Hampshire Council" — and invites correction ("Feel free to out-pedant me," post 25; titles reused as flavor throughout, e.g. posts 46, 49, 56, 90). **Delta:** the build uses a generic "Governor" office label; era/colony-specific executive titles are flavor the design wants.
6. **Flags / maps as set-pieces.** The player hand-builds a period US flag with an era-correct star count and learns the "wavy flag effect" (posts 24, 40–43); community treats accurate flags + election maps as a marquee future feature (post 23). Implies the presentation layer should render **era-correct national/state iconography** and **election-result maps**.
7. **AI-generated illustrations for events** as a *stand-in* (posts 38, 44, 51): the player uses AI art only because he can't draw/afford an artist for a proof of concept. **Explicit design stance (post 51, 860):** *"I would not want the real AMPU game to contain AI-generated imagery or text… I'd rather a proper artist be retained. However I would like the game to have procedural generation capabilities akin to CK2… to allow randomly generated and obscure politicians to have portraits."* → Requirement: **hand-authored art direction + a CK2-style procedural-portrait system** for the long tail (the ~18.5k dataset can't be hand-portraited). One image was floated as a **loading-screen** candidate (post 39).
8. **In-character GM narration voice.** Every result is wrapped in flavor prose, often with editorializing asides and humor. Events carry their full historical flavor text verbatim (posts 9, 18, 37, 56) — the build's `EraEvent` has `title`/`description`/response `description` fields (types.ts), so the data shape supports this, but the *density and voice* of the forum narration is the bar. Notable: the player flags certain event flavor as especially evocative ("I really love the vibe of that one!" — Turncoat General / Benedict Arnold, post 56; "His name will live in infamy!").
9. **"Media remembers your titles" / history tracking** (posts 16, 17): a former Senator keeps being addressed as "Senator," delegates as "Delegate," etc. — the player adds a spreadsheet column to *remember prior honorifics* and address politicians by them in narration ("very cool!" — 10centjimmy, post 16). **Requirement:** persist a politician's **highest/prior office honorific** and use it in generated narration.
10. **"Will be remembered for…" legacy tags** (post 18): the narration awards durable legacy lines — "President Benjamin Franklin will be remembered for the Declaration of Independence," "Caleb Strong… author of the Declaration of Independence," "Delegate Thomas Jefferson… proponent of Independence." A presentation-layer **legacy/epitaph** concept tied to era-event participation.

### UX pain points the run surfaces (what makes a turn *unsatisfying* to run by hand → what the software must absorb)
These come straight from the players and are gold for prioritizing what to automate:
- **Lingering (2.5.1) and Committees (2.6.x) are the most-hated, most error-prone phases** (posts 64–73). "Lingering sucks… my least favorite part" (65); "I still hate processing the cabinet the most" (69); "The whole Congress in session [has] a lot of place to slip" (68). Multiple players maintain **Python scripts** for Lingering, Career Tracks, Cabinet gains, enthusiasm-for-cabinet-lobbies, and War (posts 65, 79–84) — `Lingering.py` is literally shared in-thread (post 79). **Signal:** these subsystems are pure deterministic math the engine should own outright; the forum's bottleneck is exactly where a computer wins.
- **The rules are deeply cross-referential and easy to misapply by hand** (posts 71–78, 88–89): the recurring "cut the green wire — but only after pressing switch A" bomb-defusal joke (post 77). "Did you apply the special rule in 3.0 even though there's no reference to check 3.0 from the 2.0 section you're in" (post 88). One rule "spread out across at least three different sections" caused a real design argument (post 89). **Signal:** the value of the build is *encoding the rule graph correctly once*; flag any place the engine's rule is simpler than the forum's because that's where hand-players keep tripping.

## DELTAS — designed/observed but NOT in the build

Cited by post; `file:line` where I checked the code. These extend (do not duplicate) the gilded digest's rows; I flag overlaps.

### A. Presentation-layer gaps (the headline deltas for this thread)
- **A1. No politician portrait/art pipeline.** `Politician` (types.ts) has no image field; no card view exists. Design wants hand-authored art + **CK2-style procedural portraits** for the long tail. (posts 13, 14, 51) — *new, presentation*.
- **A2. No canonical ideology→color palette as a cross-cutting visual system.** Color-coding is core both to *reading* (post 12, 25) and *playing* (post 70–71). The app colors meters (`components/Meter.tsx`) but there's no documented 7-ideology palette reused across roster/congress/maps. (posts 12, 25, 70, 71) — *new, presentation*.
- **A3. No "sport-card" politician infobox / no styled persistent scoreboard.** The two artifacts the player most wants to leave as a legacy. `Roster.tsx` is tabular; scoring lacks a marquee leaderboard view. (posts 13–15, 22, 53) — *new, presentation*.
- **A4. No legible battle-card / no additive-odds breakdown surfaced for war.** The player's most-iterated graphic. Check whether `RevWarDashboard.tsx` shows the component breakdown (difficulty/planning/commander/meters/benchmarks → %). (posts 22, 48, 60) — *new/verify, presentation*.
- **A5. No era/colony-specific office titles.** Build labels the office "Governor"; design wants "President of the Supreme Executive Council of Pennsylvania," etc., per colony and era. (post 25) — *new, presentation/flavor*.
- **A6. No honorific-memory or legacy/epitaph system.** Persist prior-office honorifics ("Senator X") and "will be remembered for…" legacy lines from era-event participation, for use in narration. (posts 16, 17, 18) — *new, presentation/flavor*.
- **A7. No election-result maps / era-correct flags as set-pieces.** Anticipated as a marquee feature once more states exist. (posts 23, 24, 40) — *new, presentation*.
- **A8. Narration density/voice.** The data shape (`EraEvent` text fields) supports flavor, but the build's generated log voice should aim for the forum's in-character density. (posts 9, 18, 37, 56) — *aspirational, verify against `log.ts` output*.

### B. 1772 / Independence-era mechanic nuances the engine may lack
- **B1. Era-event firing model differs.** Forum (posts 9, 10, 18): events are **sorted by historical year**; the **1772 half-term fires exactly its 5 historical events at 100%** ignoring the era cap; **all other half-terms roll each event (roll ≤ %-to-fire) in historical order up to a per-era cap**; **if §2.4.3 underfills the required count it spills into another GenEvo round** (post 18: "Ten Events must happen… there's a new round of GenEvos"). The build instead uses a **precondition graph with `coreSpine` nodes that fire regardless of the probabilistic roll** (eraEvents1772.ts:23, "fire regardless of the probabilistic roll") and a `chartIndex`/precondition gate — *not* a historical-year sort + per-half-term cap + spill-to-anytime model. **This is a real structural delta in how era events are scheduled.** (posts 9, 10, 18, 37) — *new, mechanic*.
- **B2. Independence has no Party Leaders (phase 2.2.4) and no inter-party Conversion.** PLs start in Federalism (posts 6, 7); conversions need a PL so they're effectively off in 1772 (posts 3, 28, 52). Verify the engine suppresses 2.2.4 and gates inter-party conversion by era. — *verify, mechanic*.
- **B3. CC era-event decision loop:** the CC President picks a response, it is **put to a CC vote by state**, and **on defeat he re-picks among the remaining options** (post 18). `EraEvent.decider='cc-president'` exists; the ratify-then-re-pick loop likely does not. — *verify/new, mechanic*.
- **B4. Foundational vs. spending vs. crisis bill tags + budget-gated bill cap.** Forum: bills are typed (Spending/Foundational/Crisis); at Overspending only 3 spending bills pass; **Foundational bills** (Create Dept. of State/War/Treasury, Bank of North America) carry special handling (a half-point penalty the player chose to drop, post 18). No such tags on `Legislation` (grep: none). (posts 18, 46, 57) — *new, mechanic* (sibling of gilded delta #11 Crisis Bills, but adds Foundational + Spending typing + budget cap).
- **B5. Block & Replace + Bill Bundling/Packaging in the CC.** Reconfirms gilded deltas #8/#9 for the 1772 era (the CC also bundles, post 18, 46, 57). The player even extends the CPU bundling rule (bundle 75% unless hostile/random; +Crisis). — *reconfirm, mechanic*.
- **B6. Governor action library + per-state term mechanics + industry-leader scoring.** Reconfirms gilded delta #20/#35. 1772-specific action set observed: **improve an industry** (weighted 20/10/5), **lower taxes**, **set term length (2-yr/4-yr)**, **set term limits (one-term / two-term / no-limit)**, all per-state and persistent from a future cycle (posts 25, 46, 56, 90). The build has **no `state.termLength`/`termLimits` field for governors** (states1772.ts entries carry only industries/bias/ccDelegateSlots; `termLimits` in types.ts:1396 is a *Constitutional* article, not per-state governor config). Industry-leadership points-per-half-term not modeled. — *reconfirm + new fields, mechanic*.
- **B7. Industry taxonomy mismatch.** Forum 1772 industries: **Agriculture, Finance, Maritime, Manufacturing, Plantation, Mining** (posts 46, 56). Build `states1772.ts` uses **fishing, lumber, shipping, manufacturing, agriculture, finance, tobacco, cotton** (states1772.ts:8-20) — different vocabulary (no "Maritime"/"Plantation"/"Mining" buckets), and no leader table. — *new, data/mechanic*.
- **B8. Meter representation + ±3 swing cap + named ordinal meters + per-power relations.** Build = 7 numeric meters (types.ts:1399). Forum 1772 tracks **named ordinal meters** with labeled steps (Revenue/Budget: …Overspending/Very Overspending; Economic Stability: Sound/Stagnation/Panic; Domestic Stability: …Mass Protests; Military Preparedness: …Moderately Unprepared; **UK Relations**: …Enemies; **War Progress/Score**) and enforces a **±3 swing cap per phase** (posts 9, 18, 37, 57–59). Per-power foreign relations reconfirms gilded delta #25 (here: UK specifically). The ±3 cap and the war-score meter aren't obviously in the numeric model. — *new/verify, mechanic*.
- **B9. Faction Personalities (2.1.8) card-distribution algorithm — now fully specified.** Forum rule (posts 5, 15, 28, 53): (1) each faction's **ideology card = the ideology held by the most of its politicians**; (2) remaining era-minimum ideologies go to the faction with the most pols of that ideology; (3) **ideology cards on a faction must be adjacent on the 7-point scale** — gaps are resolved by dropping the card representing the fewest pols on either side; (4) interest/lobby cards distributed to the faction with the most "interested" pols, then disinterested factions get their most-represented interest — **but a ≥5-pol floor applies to the disinterested top-up (NOT to first-ever distribution)**; (5) **lobbies activate via events** (Lexington & Concord activated Military-Industrial + Big Agriculture lobbies, post 18, 28). This is materially more detail than the gilded digest captured for delta #24 (which only saw the per-half-term drift). The player even patched a rule for undistributable cards (post 5). — *extends gilded #24 with the actual algorithm, mechanic*.
- **B10. Generated/foreign-volunteer politicians join the lowest-scoring eligible faction.** Lafayette, von Steuben, etc. are **scheduled into a future draft class** (Lafayette draftable in 1784) and assigned to the **lowest-scoring faction allowed to draft that ideology** (posts 37, 56). The build has `ImportedDraftee` but no "event introduces a future-draftable figure routed by score+ideology" flow. — *new, mechanic*.
- **B11. CC delegate scoring parity.** The player rules a **CC Delegate is worth 50 pts like an elected Representative** (post 12) and seats/committee chairs carry point awards. Confirms CC participation should feed the scoring system the same way elected office does. — *verify, mechanic*.
- **B12. "Free pick-up" legislation by a skilled Treasurer** (post 57): a highly-skilled Treasury Sec can propose a bill as a free extra proposal, **double points to proposer + Treasurer, −50 to the Treasurer if not picked up**. An executive-interference legislative power not in the build. — *new, mechanic*.

### C. Balance / feel experiments the player ran (candidate design inputs, human-judgment)
- **C1. Enthusiasm as a multiplier, not a flat bonus** (posts 25, 49, 90): the player replaced the flat ideology-enthusiasm election bonus with a **graduated multiplier** ("Maximum Blue Enthusiasm → all Blue bonuses ×2.5, negatives divided," down to ×1.5 at Decent, no bonus at Lean, and at **Neutral a 3rd-party candidate becomes possible if party-pref allows**). He found the flat version "lopsided." This is a direct proposed tweak to the meters→election mapping (gilded delta #18). — *new, balance proposal*.
- **C2. Industry-bill scoring on a yes/no basis** (posts 20, 21): awarding points *per level* of an industry across delegates "would dwarf everything," so industry points are only awarded **if a state leads that industry** for the impacting bill — confirmed by a GM as how everyone actually plays it (post 21). — *balance ruling, affects B6/B7*.
- **C3. Card scoring: per-card vs. per-faction-once.** Long methodological exchange (posts 27, 29, 42, 44): the canonical rule (per `@ebrk85`/"Ted") is **every ideology/interest/lobby card a faction holds scores independently** (so a faction holding opposite-side cards nets to 0). The player had been under-counting multi-card factions. Reconfirms gilded delta #12 with the *staged tabulation method* (tabulate each card, then sum per faction). — *reconfirm #12, mechanic*.
- **C4. Experimental cross-party draft rule** (posts 2, 26): the player let the CPU **poach an other-party politician** marked *Can be Independent* / *Can Party Switch* 15% of the time if their PV is 35+ above the CPU's best same-ideology pick **and** the target's ideology-enthusiasm meter favors no party (refined in post 26 to only Prog/Lib/RW-Pop). This produced the signature "Blue George Washington" of the run. Suggests early-Independence party lines are soft and the draft should model **independents / party-switchers** as draftable across the aisle. — *new, mechanic/balance*.
- **C5. PV formula drift** (post 19): the player discovered his run used **inaccurate Political Value** (office value was missing), comparing his Franklin (245) to a 1948-playtest Franklin (207).
