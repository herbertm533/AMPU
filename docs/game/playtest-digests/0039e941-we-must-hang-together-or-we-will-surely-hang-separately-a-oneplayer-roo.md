## 4. Founding boot / CC / RevWar / Convention machinery (corroborates #86/#133/#56/#100/#101)

- **Constitutional Convention (corroborates #100):** triggered via Scripted Events, **run like a Continental Congress**; the rookie/GM explicitly struggled with **how it slots into the phase order** — "I believe we are supposed to immediately go into the Convention (before the Legislative phase)… it will be hard to track who is and is not a delegate" (POST 528); "REMOVE the titles of all current Reps because Governors have to appoint new delegates per the Convention rules" (POST 578). Per-article propose → vote; **President of the Convention elected (Langdon)**, ineligible to propose (POST 80-115). Produced an **ahistorical Constitution:** Senate **appointed by Governors** (Art I), **President-for-life** with VP = runner-up (Art II), SCOTUS by appointment+consent (Art III, historical), 3/4-state amendment (Art IV, historical), foreign-born grandfathered as natural-born to the 1812 draft (Art XII) — ratified 9/13 (POST chunk-15:8-50). A **Bill of Rights** proposed at the convention (ch14). Corroborates #100 (convention pipeline + ahistorical Constitution + mutable threshold) from a 6th 1772 angle, AND adds the **phase-order-integration friction** the rookie surfaced.
- **Era of Federalism (corroborates #101 offices-by-law):** Senate stood up (2 per state, appointed by Governors, with Class I/II/III assignment, POST 1199); **George Washington first President** (POST 811, auto-becomes faction+party leader); first PPT/Speaker/committee chairs; cabinet; then the legislative loop stands up the federal apparatus piece-by-piece — **Bank of the US, US Mint, ban-slavery amendment, Federal Direct Tax on Slaves, 1790-Compromise/Assumption-of-debts, Washington DC named** (ch17-18). Corroborates #101 (founding→federalism offices-and-institutions created by in-game law) from a 6th 1772 angle.
