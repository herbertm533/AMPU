## DC-memorials / federal-buildings — a latent "national projects" content shape

A large fraction of the Education legis-prop list is actually **monument & federal-building
authorizations** that vcczar parked here *only because they fit no other category*
(POST 1: *"the Washington DC memorials are all in here... they don't fit another category.
I'll probably redo all the policy categorizing at some point, allowing things to be in
**multiple categories**."*). These are not Education content semantically:
- **Memorials/monuments:** Jefferson, Lincoln (+ Reflecting Pool), Washington Monument,
  Washington Circle, Farragut Square, George Mason, FDR, Eisenhower, MLK, Daniel Webster,
  LBJ Memorial Grove, Theodore Roosevelt Island, Capitol/Lincoln reflecting pools.
- **Federal-building authorizations:** Robert C. Weaver, Herbert Hoover, George Bush,
  Jamie L. Whitten, Hubert Humphrey, J. Edgar Hoover, Frances Perkins, Eisenhower
  Executive, Harry S. Truman, RFK, Stewart Udall, LBJ buildings.
- **Renaming actions:** rename Supreme Court / White House / Treasury / Pentagon / US
  Capitol / DHS / VA / Energy / Transportation buildings; name reflecting pools.
- **Player extensions (POST 5):** authorize construction of a **named warship / aircraft
  carrier** (after an admiral/president); statues (cf. Statuary Hall, #518fb253 POST 37).

→ This is **prestige/national-projects content** (vanity infrastructure, soft-power /
QOL-flavor payoffs) cutting across genres. The "allow things to be in **multiple
categories**" remark is a concrete **content-taxonomy requirement**: primitives need
**multi-tag / multi-category** membership, not single-bucket assignment. Flag as a candidate
distinct genre ("National Projects / Monuments") AND as a tagging-model requirement.

## ★ Fluff-curation ruling (general content-design law — capture verbatim-ish)

vcczar restates the same CPU-realism curation rule seen in #518fb253 (and re: ChatGPT-
generated proposal lists), here in response to fun-but-trivial suggestions (warship naming,
prayer/pledge in school, school resource officers — POST 5):

> **POST 7 (vcczar):** *"It would be fun, but one issue I'm trying to avoid is having too
> many fluff proposals. If we have too many of those then the CPU will propose fluff too
> often rather than substantial things. Really what I should do is see how many laws were
> historically passed that are in the game for each two-year period... Same with Pres
> Actions."*

Two design implications, both **general (not Education-specific)**:
1. **Impact-curation rule:** the proposal catalogue must be pruned for impact; low-value
   filler degrades CPU behavior (CPU picks fluff over substance). Same rationale as
   #518fb253 POST 1/6/16/18. **Corroborates #221's importance/realm-of-possibility tier.**
2. **NEW datum — historical-density calibration:** vcczar wants to **count how many real
   laws / Pres Actions passed per two-year period** and size the per-period catalog to
   match — i.e. tune content density (and CPU proposal rate) against real legislative
   throughput. A concrete calibration method for the CPU-selection / content-density model.
