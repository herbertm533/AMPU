## What this is (the 1928 start + the "Era of Ideologies" framing)

A **1928-start interwar multiplayer campaign** opening at the Roaring-Twenties peak — **Hoover + Republicans (RED) dominating Al Smith's Democrats (BLUE)** "in the Presidential Election… a strong hold on statehouses, the Senate, the House, and even the Supreme Court" (POST 1). The era is framed as an **"international constitutional battle… Republicanism? Fascism? or Communism?"** as the world's monarchies collapse, with **"Finance and trade became even more international, making it much more likely that the collapse of one major economy would collapse all major economies… War — possibly a World War — seems likely"** (POST 1). The GA's opening teases the looming crash: *"as long as he puts his faith in the invisible hand of the free market and a blind eye towards global affairs. Surely nothing is coming around the corner…"*

**Hinge polarity (PRE-realignment):** **RED = Republicans** (Hoover; dominant, pro-business, isolationist), **BLUE = Democrats** (Al Smith — urban/immigrant + the agrarian Solid South). The modern BLUE-economic-left polarity does **not** exist at game start — it is **created in play by the New Deal (1932+)**: a player literally renames his faction the **"New Deal Democrats"** when FDR becomes party leader (POST 694), and the late-game faction roster shows the LW/Lib pole consolidating BLUE (POSTs 591-593: Progs & LW-Pops party-flip RED→BLUE by enthusiasm). Treat the cast/timing as alt-history; the era's *issues* (crash, Depression, ideological contest, isolationism, looming WWII) as real-grounded.

## Mechanics exercised (corroborations + new)

### Economic-content engine + the crash/Depression (★ corroborates #116, the EconStab meter; NEW #160)
- **The Great Depression is a SCRIPTED ERA EVENT with VARIABLE timing** — it did NOT auto-fire on the real Oct-1929 schedule. The in-game economy was still "great" in Jan 1930 (POST 567); players debated keeping prior-era party modifiers **"until the great depression event hits, if it ever does"** (POST 545, Umbrella). It finally fired in the **1930-1932 turn** (POST 862): *"The London Stock Exchange crash has led to our own crash on Wall Street… a run on banks… a shrinking money supply… the worst depression in our history!"* — the **interconnected-global-economy** idea is baked into the event text (London crash → US crash).
- **The crash is a META-EVENT carrying a bundle of meter shocks** (POST 862): **Econ Stab −4** (→ Recession & Crisis), **Rev/Budget −2** (→ overspending/Crisis), **Quality of Life −1, Mil Prep −1, Party Preference −3**. Plus a binary presidential decision (a) let markets resolve / b) congressional welfare bailout) — Hoover (via Mellon) chose "markets resolve on their own" (POST 863-864), cementing his historical fate.
- **EconStab cascade mechanics** (POST 866-868, 882-887): when EconStab falls to Recession, **2 random industries drop −1 nationwide (in every state)** + a Dom-Stab-drop chance; the industry drops can flip state industry-leadership → **EV changes** (CO +1 / OK −1 here). EconStab also **gates Dom-Stab/QoL increases** (POST 723: "would increase, but cannot as economy is in Recession").
- **The era-content bill catalog is the New-Deal/crisis economic engine** (#116 from a 1928 angle): TVA, "Allow the Federal Reserve to generate currency during economic crises" (★ **gated** — requires economy in panic/crisis to be eligible, POST 337; becomes legal only after the crash, POST 1040), "Bailout for Low/Middle-income Workers during Economic Crisis," "Create Council of Economic Advisors," "Set Income Tax brackets 25%-63% (Hoover Taxes)," Federal Farm Board, surplus-crop purchasing, Dust-Bowl agricultural relief, "Set Average Tariff Rate to 40%," "Grant President the power to set tariffs."
- **The Depression is CLIMBED OUT OF via legislation + gov actions** — the New Deal bills + gov "lower taxes" actions each give EconStab +1; by mid-1936 "we are no longer in Econ stab crisis" (POST 1601). GA's wry note: *"Good for the country… not good for the GOP"* (POST 1602). Players joke about "shrugging off the Great Depression in just 2-4 years" (POST 1046-1047).

## Design ideas / wishes
- **★ The era's static party-modifier bonuses are too strong** (GA, POST ch18-8) — they make the Republicans crater regardless of the actual economy; should be **event-gated to the Depression** rather than baked statically into the era band (Umbrella's POST 545 proposal: use prior-era modifiers "until the great depression event hits"). This is the central design takeaway for the economic engine (see #160).
- **Auto-calc industry-impact tool** (Umbrella, POST 450) — the build should own the "how many of my gov/sen/rep are in each lead-industry" tally that legislation scoring needs.
- **Career-track pre-placement** (POST 32) — bake the "seed generational pols onto tracks at start" idea into the scenario boot so marquee figures aren't stuck at floor stats.
- **Settle the start-state model** (POST 185) — pick one: election-start vs president-in-place vs president-with-historical-cabinet. Players favor president-in-place (POST 187-188).
- **Ambassador cross-party cap is too restrictive** (POST 744-746) — fine for cabinet, "dumb" for ambassadors given the many lobbies/regions to satisfy.
