## ¬ß2. ‚òÖ WV v. EPA ‚Äî the concrete #270 dual-payload, multi-interest example (POST 2)

The thread's single highest-signal datum, and the **first MODERN, real-world worked
case** for the #270 generator/docket (sibling `f8354a70` had only procedural fictional
rows like "Bao to Seitan"). CE2's exact spec:

> *"WV v EPA is a good one. Could **gain points for big business, conservatives,
> trads** and **lose points for reformists, environmentalists, progs and libs**,
> **reverse if it goes the other way**."*

Decomposed against the #270 schema (`f8354a70` ¬ß1):
- **Multi-interest payload (NOT one group per side):** a single ruling moves a **whole
  bundle** of groups together ‚Äî unlike the `f8354a70` sample rows (one group per side).
  This corroborates the dual-payload shape but **widens it to N-groups-per-ruling**.
- **Ruling A (pro-EPA-loses / deregulation side):**
  - **+** Big Business, Conservatives, Trads
  - **‚àí** Reformists, Environmentalists, Progs, Libs
- **Ruling B ("reverse if it goes the other way"):** signs flip ‚Äî the **symmetric
  Yea/Nay structure** from #270, here as a clean mirror (one side's winners are the
  other side's losers).
- **Interest vs ideology channels (same split as `f8354a70`):** the bundle MIXES
  **interest-group rewards** (Big Business, Environmentalists, Reformists) with
  **ideology-enthusiasm** rewards (Conservatives, Trads, Progs, Libs). A faithful
  payload writes BOTH channels (see ¬ß4).

This is **#270 content** (a real case to author into the generated/authored docket),
and the **clearest real-world illustration** of the dual-payload multi-interest case.
