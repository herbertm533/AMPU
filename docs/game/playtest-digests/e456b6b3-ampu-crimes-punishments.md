  **War on Drugs**, **Allow states to regulate drugs**, **Legalize Marijuana** as sibling rungs.
  This is a concrete **prereq-CHAIN within a subtype** (legalize-medicinal/supervised → full
  legalization) → corroborates **#258** (prereq-gated availability) + the #221 chain primitive;
  cross-ref Agriculture's Dept chain. ★ The headline cross-ref: **#248 (declared subtype) ×
  #258 (intra-subtype prereq ladder)** worked together on one content axis.

### ★ 2. Obscenity court-DOCTRINE progression as SCOTUS-case scripted-events that gate content (POST 3)
A contributor lays out a **precedent-as-game-state-FLAG** model — the single strongest novel
finding for the SCOTUS cluster:
- **Era-of-the-Gilded-Age scripted EVENT:** Anthony Comstock founds the **New York Society for
  the Suppression of Vice**; **Comstock Act** Legis (bans obscene materials via post) —
  era-event + paired Legis (cf. genre POST 1's "Ban Obscene Material via Post (L-Gild)").
- **Court-case scripted events forming a DOCTRINE TIMELINE:** **Hicklin test (1879)** →
  **Roth test (1957 — thread says "1950")** → **Miller test (1973)**. Each is a SCOTUS-case
  S-event that **sets a precedent FLAG**, and that flag **gates which downstream content is
  available**:
  - *"If no obscenity law OR Hicklin test exist → left to the states"* (the `*-Default` falls
    through when no precedent flag is set).
  - *"If either exists → govs can advocate for book-burnings of 'obscene materials'"* (Gov-Action
    gated on precedent-OR).
  - *"If left to states → ban sale of obscene materials (sex toys); ban import of obscene
    materials"* (state-scoped content gated on the absence of a federal precedent).
  - **★ "If Miller test exists:"** unlocks a distinct content SET — Legis (Ban sales of obscene
    materials/devices; Restrict obscene content in daytime broadcast) + a **Prostitution**
    sub-cluster (Ban / misdemeanor / felony / regulate — a graded-severity option set) + Gov-
    Actions (restrict brothels/places of obscenity; restrict access to pornographic content).
- **→ This is "court-precedent-exists" as a first-class availability predicate** — a SCOTUS
  case outcome writes a persistent game-state flag that later content's `Preq:` reads. Strong
  cross-ref to the **SCOTUS cluster (#25 / #52 docket, #218 Rule-of-Four, #249 case-importance,
  #251 judiciary overhaul)** AND to **#258** (the "precedent-exists predicate" — same shape as
  Agriculture's Munn v. Illinois / Pigford prereqs, but here the precedent flag itself is the
  gate for a whole content tier). The DOCTRINE-LADDER (Hicklin→Roth→Miller) is also a chained
  S-event sequence (each supersedes the prior), like Agriculture's invention tech-tree.

### ★ 4. Police-STRUCTURE axis as a mutually-exclusive option-set (POST 11)
A funding+structure axis distinct from individual crime bills:
- **Funding lever:** Increase funding to states for law enforcement (crime −%) ↔ Decrease
  funding (states bear greater burden) — a paired +/− toggle.
- **★ Mutually-exclusive STRUCTURE set:** {Remove law-enforcement from states + authorize a
  **National/Federal police force**} / {**Ban local police** — State & Federal only} / {**All**
  policing done by Federal gov} / {Fund Federal police} / {**Outlaw a National/Federal police
  force**}. These options are pairwise-exclusive (you cannot both create and ban a federal
  police force) — corroborates the #237 "opposed-pair / mutually-exclusive option-set" pattern,
  applied to a 5-way exclusive set. Plus Expand Federal prisons; Reduce/Increase minimal
  sentencing recommendations (the increase/decrease/repeal modifier-verb pattern, cf. #221).

## Gov-Action sub-mechanics (corroborate #20 / #221, several novel toggles)

- **Death-penalty METHOD selection + public-execution toggle as Gov-Actions** (POSTs 1/2/4):
  POST 4 confirms "setting the methods of executions as a Gov action" (someone proposed it
  earlier); POST 2 adds, *if death penalty active*, an option to **make it a public execution**.
  Both are content **gated on "Federal Death Penalty active"** (#258) and exhibit a
  **sub-selection** within a single policy (which method) — finer-grained than a binary toggle.
- **★ Split FEDERAL vs MILITARY capital punishment** (POST 13): *"split Federal capital
  punishment and capital punishment by the US military… if one is abolished the other one
  would not be abolished automatically."* — TWO independent policy flags for what looks like one
  policy; abolishing one does not cascade to the other. A non-obvious data-model requirement
  for the policy store (parallel independent toggles, no implicit linkage).
- **Prosecutorial discretion for allies under high corruption** (POST 2): *if a certain level
  of corruption / state-party machinery is reached*, a Gov-Action to **exercise prosecutorial
  discretion in favor of political allies and against adversaries** — a Gov-Action **gated on a
  corruption-meter threshold** (#258 predicate = a meter ≥ threshold, like the Dom-Stab gate).
- **Cash-bail** (POST 2): abolish cash bail for misdemeanors/non-violent felonies, or for all
  charges — graded-scope option set.
- **Stand-Your-Ground / Duty-to-Retreat** (POST 10): Federal AND state variants of each, as an
  opposed pair (SYG ↔ DTR) at two government levels — reinforces the per-state Gov-action want (#20).

## Engine facts (verified, do not re-derive)

- `src/types.ts` `interface Legislation` (1506) has **no `subtype` field**, no `Preq:`/condition
  field, and no Pres-Action / Gov-Action / policy-genre fields. The three #221 content
  primitives + scripted-event registry remain **designed-only, 0% shipped** (corroborates #221,
  #237, #248, #258 and every sibling content drop's null result).

  precedent-FLAG concept, no "court-case-sets-a-game-state-flag-that-gates-content" mechanism,
  no chained-supersession (Hicklin→Roth→Miller), no per-officer agency hook.
- No "Crimes & Punishments" / "Drugs" policy-genre store, no prereq engine, no precedent-flag
  registry, and no per-cabinet-officer (AG) per-turn action library exist in `src/`. The shipped
  court (`phaseRunners.ts` ~3397) is the 50%/turn coin-flip on hardcoded title strings (#25/#52).
- **NOTE:** the genre's "5th/6th/8th Amendment" items model constitutional amendments AS
  Legis-Props — consistent with the broader design (no separate amendment subsystem captured),
  worth a glance against #52/Convention content but no new mechanic here.
