## §1. ★ The AMPU-2 thesis (vcczar, POST 10, 37) — designer-authoritative

The durable extract. vcczar's statement of what AMPU 2 is and the constraints on
it:

- **The MAIN AMPU-2 change = day-by-day timeline (Paradox-style):** *"I plan to
  change the game from phase by phase to day by day, similar to the Paradox games.
  So votes are scheduled. Events occur on specific dates. Primaries occur on
  specific dates. Deaths could happen anytime. Court cases are scheduled. Laws and
  actions might not take effect until a specific date."* This is the headline
  architectural difference from AMPU 1's phase/turn loop.
- **Office expansion priority = every US Rep** (a full House); maybe the only new
  offices. Would otherwise just **expand the career track** (let anyone holding
  office onto it). **Won't add 100,000+ obscure pols** without trusted volunteers
  who replicate his exact authoring.
- **State-office expansion REJECTED unless data justifies it:** Lieutenant
  Governors / State Secretaries of State / state judiciaries — V won't add them
  unless **~50% of holders move on to Gov or federal office**, else he'd have to
  penalize them to avoid ahistorical fast-tracking. *"These offices already exist
  under the career track."* The **federal judiciary** is the better expansion
  (federal-level). The state element is a **deliberately supporting role**.
- **AMPU 1 is the test + the bargaining tool:** *"We also have to fix all the
  issues with AMPU 1 first, so I can use it as a bargaining tool when finding a
  Paradox-like dev to make AMPU 2. It's also possible I just sell the game to a
  dev."* AMPU 1 will reveal "what people like or find tedious" (POST 37).
- **★ Personal-toll caveat (POST 10):** vcczar describes AMPU as having "ruined
  parts of my life," become "a chore… a burden"; *"unless [ideas] light a lamp in
  my head, they're just going to stay ideas."* — i.e. the bar for AMPU-2 ideas is
  very high and the FEEDBACK he most wants is **how to make play more DYNAMIC
  without just adding more politicians** (POST 10).
- **The scouting/hidden-stats feature (POST 37):** AMPU 1 deliberately **removed**
  hidden abilities/traits + a cabinet "scouting/vetting" feature (it was in the
  first paper draft) because it **slowed the game** (goal: a full game in <24h of
  play). **May return in AMPU 2** — explicitly an AMPU-2 candidate, not AMPU 1.

## §2. The rejected / future-ideas catalog (QUARANTINED — bucketed, NOT gaps)

Recorded so they aren't re-surfaced as buildable. **None of these are AMPU-1
roadmap items.**

### §2a. Dynamic systems (the recurring wish — all deferred)
- **Federal-vs-State power meter (POST 3, theFreezerFlame):** a 1-9 meter
  (Federal Tyranny ↔ Powerless Federal Government) with per-level faction/DomStab
  modifiers; at 9, governors can secede at will without war. (Detailed but
  rejected.)
- **Dynamic state party-biases / ideology drift (POST 30, 49-55, multiple):** the
  most-repeated wish — replace static era-keyed biases with biases that **react to
  policy/elections** + let a governor slowly shift a state's ideology over decades
  (e.g. d100 ≤10/half-term → shift one step toward the gov). **vcczar/ebrk: biases
  are NOT static (they update each census) but are PREDETERMINED by history** — a
  **design choice** (AMPU is "more a history simulation than a historical
  sandbox," Saucialiste POST 55). **★ This is the SAME gap as DH-34 ("static era
  biases unfixable → AMPU 2.0") — already logged; do NOT re-log.**
- **Dynamic regions (POST 22-29, 10centjimmy):** states join/leave regions
  dynamically (census regions for balance vs malleable cultural regions for
  bonuses). OrangeP47: a nice idea with no workable definition ("you don't even
  really have an idea yet… it's 'I'm hungry' vs 'let's get KFC'"). Rejected as
  undefined.
- **Dynamic era system (POST 46, Nikk):** spend an era at war → hit a "war
  fatigue" era instead of the normal next era.
- **Dynamic foreign world (POST 11, 42-45):** branching international diplomacy
  (US skips WWI → Central Powers win; never buy Louisiana → it becomes a country).
  vcczar/Ted: "we have a bit of this but not sure how far it branches" (Ark's
  branch-path tracking).

### §2b. Office / institutional expansion (mostly rejected per §1)
- Lt. Governors + State Secretaries of State (POST 8) — **rejected** (§1).
- Larger judiciary + district attorneys (POST 4) — the **one vcczar liked**
  (federal-level), still AMPU-2.
- State Chief Justices per state (POST 12) — AMPU-2.
- Territorial Delegates + Territorial Governors (POST 3) — delegates elected like
  the House, serve on committees, no floor vote until statehood.
- Congressional nominating caucus / pre-convention nomination (POST 38-40).
- House & Senate campaign committees with a fundraising-number mechanic (POST 41,
  theFreezerFlame) — gated on a Buckley-v-Valeo-analog event.

- **★ Player-to-player POLITICIAN TRADING (POST 60-61, jnewt):** trade drafted
  pols between factions (a pol useless to you — e.g. a Puritan Mod in a Lib
  faction — is valuable to another). "I love this idea" (theFreezerFlame). **★
  This is DH-37 ("the #1 AMPU-2 wishlist item") — already logged; cross-reference,
  do NOT re-log.**
- **Draft-time ideology shift (POST 21, matthewyoung):** 25% chance a pol drafts
  one ideology step left/right of historical (like the SCOTUS-justice drift) — FDR
  could draft as a Mod.
- **Special draft rule for Donald Trump (POST 6, theFreezerFlame):** enters 2012
  as RW-Pop if the incumbent is Lib/Prog/LW-Pop, else LW-Pop. (Community liked it;
  one note Trump flirted with the Reform Party in the 90s, POST 46.)
- **Pol-trading-as-draft-extension** + a sports-draft feel (POST 60).
