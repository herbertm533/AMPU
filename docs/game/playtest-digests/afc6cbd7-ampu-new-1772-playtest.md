## 2. FOUNDING-ERA DETAIL — the value of this thread (corroborates/extends the most-built era)

### 2a. TWO sequential 1772 drafts — Inaugural + Rookie
- **Two drafts in 1772**: an **Inaugural Draft** (all established pols) THEN a separate **Rookie Draft** (rookies added to the Draft Class tab after) (POST 21, 22, 36). The shipped `scenario1772` does ONE snake draft — **forum runs two sequential drafts**. (Delta.)
- Draft order: first by **random draw** (no points yet); rookie draft **re-rolled order** by GM fiat as "fairest" (POST 47). Subsequent drafts **ordered by score, ties random, lowest score picks first** (POST 475, 480).
- **Cross-party draft rule (2.1.2):** draft freely within your faction's draft ideologies; **30% chance** to draft *outside* them if matching pols remain (fail = lose the pick); no penalty if none of your ideologies remain (drop to nearest). **Faction-leader Harmonious/Disharmonious shifts 30% → 40%/20%** (POST 28, 243). **Cannot draft opposing-party pols even if "Can party flip"** (POST 28).
- **Inaugural-draft special rule:** rookies from the **past THREE draft classes** (1768/1764/1760) may be put on career tracks retroactively and removed same turn (POST 55-56).
- **Score-based draft boost:** lowest-score faction's first pick gets **+1 to a relevant skill**; **8th-place ties for "lowest" → NO boost** (strict reading, POST 480; cf. POST 51 — all-tied-at-0 gets no boost). Every draft pick also rolls a random trait gain + random alt-state gain (POST 51, 2833). 9th/10th-picking factions get an ability bonus to their 1st pick (POST 2853).
- **Procedural filler-pol generation confirmed** when a state's roster is exhausted: TN was so empty that **"Some Dude William Martin" was auto-generated** to fill an unopposed 1796 House seat; **generated pols' factions are randomized** (POST 3581, 3583).

### 2b. Career tracks (2.1.2) — biggest recurring ambiguity of the run
- 7 tracks (Private, Military, State Exec, State Admin, State Legis, State Judicial, Backroom; **no roll to enter Backroom**, POST 2853). Pols sit up to **20 years**, gaining skills/expertise/traits at the **4/8/12/16/20-yr** marks, moving one cell right per turn; **auto-removed at 20** with a big stat/trait payout (POST 51-70, 1356, 2834-2856). Some sectors (e.g. Admin) **require a roll to enter** — failure common.
- ★ **Career-track LOCKOUT** is the single biggest rules ambiguity (POST 322-338, 374, 448-452, 772-774). Final GM rulings:
  - **Appointments & military:** pol may be pulled from track only if **no non-tracked pol is eligible** (rule 3.0.28 "sudden vacancy"); gains **rounded DOWN** to the last completed mark (POST 329-330, 772-774). Cabinet/cabinet-level/ambassador/military acceptance odds for tracked pols: **100%/75%/50%** (POST 2248).
  - **Elections (Gov):** **CANNOT** pull from track to run — the track's "moderate risk" must bite (POST 448, 451). Deliberate GM design stance, not crisp in the rules.
  - **Only the matching-sector track** fills a vacancy (State Exec → Gov/CC-Exec; relevant branch for military) (POST 809). Track pols **ascend automatically** to fill sudden Gov/Sen vacancies (POST 3586).
  - **Recurring DATA BUG:** pols kept getting left flagged on the career-track tab after being pulled, causing missed gains and retro-fixes (POST 927-934, 953-963). GM: it's the player's responsibility to flag current-office; **no retcon once a turn is missed** (POST 963). → strong signal the **build should auto-manage track state** (the sheet can't).

### 2d. First Continental Congress (FCC) → Confederation Congress
- **No election** in Independence era; CC **delegates are APPOINTED by the faction with the most pols in each state** (POST 168). Per-state delegate counts (= colony size): CT 3, DE 2, GA 2, MA 4, MD 4, NC 3, NH 2, NJ 3, NY 3, PA 4, RI 2, SC 3 (was 2, +1 later), VA 4 (POST 168, 219, 454).
- **FCC requires 0 Legis**; the **Second CC (post-Declaration) requires ≥1 Legis** — 0-Legis delegates become ineligible once the Declaration is signed → forced replacements (POST 173-174, 454, 766, 934).
- **CC President** chosen by the faction with the **most delegates** that turn (needs ≥1 Command); assigns **4 committees** (Domestic, Foreign/Military, Economic, Judicial) + chairs. ★ **CC committee chairs need NO 2-Legis** (the 2-Legis chair rule is House/Senate-only, POST 592). FCC forms end of turn 1 but **doesn't act until turn 2** (POST 193).
- **CC gains:** each committee member rolls expertise per their committee. ★ **Judicial committee can ONLY grant Justice expertise** — a flagged dead-end ("no reason to stay once you get it"); proposed fix 25% random trait (POST 250-251). **No duplicate gains** — already-held rolled gain = gain nothing (rule may have changed; POST 607-608).
- **CC confirmation:** appointees auto-confirmed **unless Controversial**, then a vote needs **60% OF THE STATES** (each state = 1 vote; internal majority decides the state; **tie = state abstains**) (POST 354, 357-358). Used to confirm Controversial Benedict Arnold as Admiral (POST 307, 349).
- **Articles of Confederation ratification renames CC → "Confederation Congress"** (Era Evo, POST 1065); same body, new name. Articles add: Congress requests funds from states; **no consecutive CC reappointment** (POST 625, 706); **2/3 states to pass a bill** (later 9-of-13 in practice); **unanimous for amendments**.
- **9 of 13 states required to pass a bill** in the CC; states vote as a bloc (POST 1127, 1773). **Spending-bill crisis lock:** when Revenue-Budget is at crisis (1), only **ONE spending bill** passes per turn (non-spending bills exempt) (POST 1280, 1290). **CC President tie-break power** added by bill: lets the Pres **decide the vote of a deadlocked/abstaining state** (NOT break an overall tie, since 9 are always required) (POST 1286, 1290, 1924). **Confederation Cabinet** created by bill unlocks SecWar, SecTreasury, **Postmaster General** (ruled a Domestic-committee appointment; rules were silent) (POST 1289, 1446).

### 2g. Constitutional Convention machinery (the headline transition — POST 1673–2075)
- **Annapolis Convention** (Era Evo, POST 1673): President/CC-Pres picks Option A → **triggers the Constitutional Convention**; it must fire before any "propose a constitution" is possible (POST 1511).
- **Delegate selection:** Govs appoint **up to 3/state, ≥1 Legis, 2 own-party + 1 opposing**; CC Pres/Chairs/Ambassadors barred; military officers barred **unless Military Leader + Celebrity** (only Arnold qualified); career-track pols may leave to attend; sitting officeholders need NOT resign (one-off) (POST 1693).
- Convention auto-elects a **Convention President** (rallied unanimously around war hero **Benedict Arnold**) and names a **"Father of the Constitution"** (Caleb Strong, who proposed it) (POST 1726). Father gains +1 Cmd/Legis/Admin/Celebrity; Pres +1 Cmd; **all delegates lose Obscure**.
- **FIVE items MUST be resolved** before the Constitution completes (POST 1728): **Art 1 (legislature) / Art 2 (executive) / Art 3 (judiciary) / Slave-State Compromise / Amendment Process.** Options actually in the game (POST 1759, 1772):
  - **Art 1:** A = House + Senate (bicameral); C = Unicameral Senate-only.
  - **Art 2:** A = elected President 4-yr terms; C = elected life-term President.
  - **Art 3:** A = Federal Judicial Branch (Supreme Court).
  - **Slave-State Compromise:** A = 3/5 (historical); B = all slaves count toward EVs; C = slaves not counted. (GM "Ban Slavery" house-rule folds in as an Option D that combines no-count + ban while still giving the South the EV bump — documents a known rough edge, POST 1728.)
  - **Amendment Process:** A = 3/4 of states; B = majority; **D = 2/3** (GM-allowed house addition, not originally a Convention option, POST 1736, 1796).
  - **Add-on amendments** needing a specific delegate trait: **Voting Rights for Women** (needs an **LW Activist** delegate — Paine), **Natural Born Citizenship** (foreign-born-pre-Constitution; born-in-territories), **Bill of Rights**, **Lifetime Senate Appointments**.
- **Proposal rule:** each delegate makes **one** proposal; only items tagged "For Constitutional Convention only" are proposable there (POST 1736). Rules say propose in **highest-Legis order**; GM let everyone propose at once as an MP shortcut, repeatedly flagging this **won't match the computer version** (POST 1730, 1738, 1740). **Convention shenanigans:** only the **Convention President and Father of the Constitution** can sway votes (if Debater/Orator), each item independently (POST 1720, 1722).
- **Voting:** **9 states must approve each item**; add-ons (items 6-8) fail if <9; core items (1-5) re-vote until something hits 9.
- ★ **Resulting ALT-HISTORY Constitution this timeline** (POST 1824): bicameral; 4-yr President; federal judiciary; **Slave-State Option B (all slaves count, NOT 3/5)**; **2/3-of-states amendment threshold**; **women's suffrage**; both natural-born-citizenship amendments. Confirms the Convention is a **real branching system, not scripted to the historical text** — and these choices **feed forward into election math** (see 2h).

### 2h. Ratification & the first Presidential election (1788)
- **Ratification** = a Gov vote needing **≥9 Govs**; if it fails, the game **continues with the Continental Congress** (POST 1824). Adopting **women's suffrage tanked Domestic Stability to 1** — controversial-amendment backlash is modeled (POST 1834). Constitution triggers follow-on Era Evos: **Federalist / Anti-Federalist Papers** (President endorses/condemns; authors are flavor only) (POST 1836, 1840).
- **1788 election** (pre-12A/pre-17A, POST 1939-2016): faction with most Kingmakers per party picks a **President nominee (Faction Leader OR Celebrity)** + **VP nominee (≥1 Command)**, both ≥35; may nominate from other factions; other factions add **Alternates** (no mechanical difference between slots once voting starts — most EVs = President, 2nd = VP). **EV allocation:** most states assign EVs by **how their Kingmakers vote** (or the Gov if none); a subset (DE/MD/NH/PA/VA) assign by **popular vote (PV-driven)** because they lacked the per-state historical "State Legislature selects President" activation flag (default = popular vote) (POST 1981-1987). Era of Federalism opens with the **1788 Draft**, then President must appoint an **entire Supreme Court**; Senate seats split into **Classes I/II/III** (next elected 1790/1792/1794) (POST 2059, 2080). *(Corroborates the "points reset at each era boundary" fact already in the federalism digest; here reached organically from a 1772 start — confirms the continuous multi-era campaign model.)*

## 3. Generic cross-era WAR ENGINE — war is repeatable, not Rev-War-only (corroborates #45/#56)

Two distinct wars run in this thread, plus a parallel lost track — strong evidence the war engine is a generic, repeatable system.

- **Revolutionary War combat (formula-level, POST 414):** **5 Generals (1 Senior + 4)** and **3 Admirals (1 Senior + 2)** appointed (POST 295-296, 319); **Senior officer must have "Military Leader"** (at start, Esek Hopkins was the only eligible Senior Admiral; POST 301, 305). Battle: `Chance = Difficulty (Easy 0 / Medium -10 / Difficult -25) + Planning Phase (Sr officer bonus, DOUBLED for the Sr) + Military Officer bonus (scales with leading officer's Mil: +10/+20/+30/+40/+50) + Military Preparedness (+5..+15) + Military Benchmarks (+10..+15)`; roll **d100 ≤ Chance = victory** (POST 414, 1549, 1935). **War Score** accumulates ± per battle (victory +2/+3, defeat -1/-2/-3); Naval then Ground phase each turn, **50% chance to roll another battle** after each (one turn had 6 ground battles); then a **Momentum Roll** then a **War Result Roll** decides continue/end.
- **Per-battle consequences:** winner rolls +1 Mil / Military Leader / Leadership / loses Obscure; loser → -1 Mil, Incompetent, **auto-fired** (Esek Hopkins fired after losing most naval battles, POST 416); pols can be **killed in battle** (Tallmadge, POST 1935). ★ **Battle points go to the SENIOR officer's faction, NOT the battle-leading officer's** (POST 419, 432) — counterintuitive, players got it wrong repeatedly. **Court-martial** option: the F/M Chair may court-martial an officer who loses an Easy battle (POST 416, 425).
- **Parallel war: INVASION OF CANADA** ran as a SEPARATE war track with its own battles/war-score (triggered by the "Invade Canada" bill, a chair's replacement of "Invite Canada"); the **US LOST it** (war-score went negative → "rolls to end — US loses", POST 377-416). Content gap: **"Invite Canada" only adds Quebec** — players flag Nova Scotia ("14th colony") should be includable (POST 364).
- **Post-RevWar:** all officers **except Senior General & Senior Admiral vacate** (POST 898). **Treaty of Paris** negotiated by CC President + UK Minister + French Ambassador per the War Chart — but the bill's Proposed-Legislation requirements say the envoy must NOT hold office; **War-Chart tab vs bill-requirements CONFLICT** (POST 886-894). Treaty grants land west to the Mississippi (POST 1009).
- ★ **Northwest Indian War** (Era Evo *after* the army was disbanded, POST 1461, 1473-1486) — proves war is generic. GM had to **improvise**: rules don't cover **reinstating Generals/Admirals when a new war starts after the Rev War**; he ruled the needed offices (Generals only, ground war) re-open until war ends (POST 1486). US victory → **gained OH, IN, MI, IL, WI via the Treaty of Greenville** (implementation roll) (POST 1936) — confirms **war victories drive territorial/map expansion.** *(Standing officers only persist permanently once "Standing Army" / "Navy of six frigates + wooden sail navy" bills pass — POST 2444-2446.)*

## 5. First party-ticket convention (1796) — after passing the 12A-analog amendment

- Pre-12A presidential elections used **state legislatures** (and earlier the **Kingmaker** abstraction). This thread **PASSES the "Presidential Elections by Party Ticket Amendment"** (the **12A analog**), turning on **party conventions + running mates** for 1796 (POST 3460). Before it, **ALL candidates run for President and the top 2 become President/VP** (no conventions/primaries/debates) (POST 2296, 2665).
- **Convention mechanics (richly documented):** each player runs **1 major** (≥1 Command AND Faction Leader OR Celebrity; Faction Leaders are automatically majors) + **1 minor/favorite-son** (just ≥1 Command). Nominator must be a **non-candidate from the same faction** (POST 3478, 3482). **Nomination speech:** nominator rolls; **Orator + high roll → +1 Momentum**. **Ballots** to a delegate majority (~183 of ~365 Red). Between ballots, candidates take **convention actions** (count scales with standing: leader/front-runner up to 5): **Whip Party into Compliance** (Leadership 5-6; +2 if Iron Fist & rivals Pliable), **Appeal to Integrity** (vs Controversial rivals), **Appeal to Will of the People / Credibility**, **Influence smoke-filled rooms** (needs to be an active protégé of a National Kingmaker), and **Presidential Promises** (offer Cabinet/General/Admiral posts or the VP slot to a faction for its endorsement).
- ★ **EMERGENT EXPLOIT (GM explicitly intrigued, invented a live fix):** a **minor candidate who is the only candidate of a common ideology surges to front-runner** once minors can win delegates **outside their home state**. **Ralph Izard (Cons) jumped from 0 → 121 delegates (+102) on the 2nd Red ballot** — "Izard benefits tremendously from being the only Cons candidate in the race with a lot of Cons states in the country" — and became a major (POST 3498, chunk-035 ~1285). **Muhlenberg did the same on the Blue side** (POST 3532). Rules don't cover a faction holding TWO majors; GM ruled **split that faction's Gov/Sen delegates between them like ideology delegates** (POST 3498, 3508). Rule: a minor → major only if it finishes 1st/2nd after a ballot (POST 3521).
- **VP scoring** = a fixed checklist (different factions? Mod/Cons/Lib on ticket? ≥20yrs apart? someone >50 / <60? incumbent? exactly one Independent/out-of-office? big+small state? different regions? + Obscure roll) → numeric score that shifts **Party Preference** (POST 3511, 3541). **Platform** = **5 planks** (Domestic/Economic/Judicial/FA-Military/Executive), each scored on 3 yes/no tests → up to 3/3, shifts enthusiasm; **Iron Fist nominee cannot delegate planks** to other factions (POST 3513).
- **1796 outcome (concrete):** Red **Washington/Rush** (Washington won a 3-ballot fight after the Izard scare); Blue **Henry/Clinton** (Henry won on the 3rd ballot, buying endorsements with SecState→Jefferson's faction, SecTreasury→ebrk85, Admiralty→Muhlenberg). **Washington wins 81-76 EV while LOSING the popular vote** (~45.3% vs 52.98% two-party for Henry, POST 3563, 3573).
- ★ **Popular-vote winner lost the presidency THREE straight elections** (1788, 1792, 1796 — across both the pre-12A top-2 era and the post-12A convention era): *"We're already on our third Presidential election, and we still haven't had one in which the winner of the popular vote won the office"* (chunk-036 ~1523; players note the same at 2790, 3577). A recurring artifact of the legislature/EV-vs-popular split + planter-gatekept suffrage flavor — a balance/feel observation, not a bug.

## 6. Other founding-era mechanics (corroborated; terse, with cites)

- **Relocations:** alt-state move = **auto-success** (grants **Carpetbagger**, timed e.g. "until 1806"); over→under-populated move = **25% diff region / 50% same region (+25% if Expansionist card)** — so even over→under can FAIL (POST 982, 2507, 3213). Overpop = PA early, PA+VA later (KY/OH count as VA); **underpopulated = <20 pols** (GA at exactly 20 is NOT underpop, POST 719). Relocations are **simultaneous** (can't condition one on another); **no per-turn relocation cap** (POST 97, 102). *(Cross-era relocation cap-4-vs-shipped-5 delta lives in other digests; this thread doesn't state the cap.)*
- **Ideology shifts:** up to **3 attempts/turn** (±1 step); **75% if toward the Faction Leader's ideology**; **+2 if leader has Iron Fist, +1 Propagandist, +1 Manipulative** (Jefferson got 6, POST 190, 2122). Failing often inflicts **Flipflopper → Two-Faced** and **Pliable**; **Integrity caps success at 10%**; **Puritan blocks shifts** (POST 523, 2872). Flipflopper penalty clears at start of next turn.
- **Conversions (poaching):** within your own party (target needs **Pliable**, ideology same/adjacent to leader); base **10%** (some leaders 33%, +10% vs leader's far-ideology e.g. Jefferson 43% vs RW Pops). **Harmonious & Passive leaders cannot convert** (POST 208, 556). Cross-party only via **"Can Party Flip"** pols (Pliable 10% / Moderate 5% / both 15%; **Manipulative leader → any Can-Flip pol at 33%**) or **auto-defection of a "Disgruntled" ideology** (JQA R1→B1, POST 3217). ★ Players ran an **informal non-aggression truce** ("pass unless targeted") for most of the era — emergent cooperative norm, not a rule (POST 557+, 1394-1407, 2517).
- **Kingmakers/Protégés:** Kingmaker **age ≥35, not on career track**; protégé **same state + same ideology**; **1 pairing/turn**; protégé gains +1 skill + expertise + sometimes a trait. **Master Kingmaker** doubles expertise AND grants protégé **+1 Command regardless** + passes an extra trait (POST 1016, 1014, 2904). A Kingmaker with **Leadership** can take a protégé without being a Kingmaker proper. An active Kingmaker **can also be a protégé** of a Master Kingmaker elsewhere (POST 1165).
- **Governors (from 1776):** no primaries this era → two general nominees = factions with most Kingmakers in the state (tie → most pols from state) (POST 445, 448); to primary a same-party incumbent your candidate must be a **different ideology** (POST 682). Govs **appoint the next CC delegation** and (post-Constitution) **appoint US Senators** (pre-17A abstraction, POST 2050, 2057). **Gov actions:** ~1/turn (2 if **Efficient**), success vs Gov skill (POST 650, 838) — the run had a famous **~1.1%-probability streak of EVERY Gov action failing** (POST 832-834). Actions seen incl. Improve Industry, Establish State Bank, Raise Taxes, Encourage settlement on Native lands (**not repeatable per state**, POST 1271, 1866), **Abolish Slavery** (per-state, one-shot, irreversible; big points but an **electoral penalty** to the acting Gov — Irwin -2 to elections until 1796; plantation→agriculture at 2:1 round-down, POST 1877, 1896), Gerrymander (needs Controversial or Iron Fist, POST 3046), Build Political Machine (+1 to faction's candidates in-state), 4-Year/One-Term limits (sitting Govs grandfathered, POST 3182). **States entering as slave states by default** unless prevented: KY, TN, MO, MS, AL, LA, AR, TX, NM, AZ, CA, DC, HI (POST 1882).
- **Faction cards** (Ideologies / Special Interests / Lobbies) **redistributed each turn** by roster drift; gate leader eligibility + bill/conversion options (POST 1020, 2176, 3232). **Iron-Fist party leaders must share one card** with their party (POST 3273). Card oddities: **every ideology card MUST be assigned even with zero holders**, to an **adjacent** ideology → the **RW-Pop card went to a faction with ZERO RW Pops** because it held the Trad card (POST 113, 132, 145); **special-interest cards need ≥5 pols** to assign (early game many factions get none); **lobby cards can't go to a wrong-type Mod faction** (POST 132). ★ **Player-to-player pol trading is house-allowed but NOT a real rule** — repeatedly cited as the **#1 AMPU-2 wishlist item** (used to fix mis-drafted card distributions, e.g. 3 Trads ↔ 3 Cons, POST 147-158, 148).
- **National meters** (founding set): Revenue-Budget, Economic Stability, Domestic Stability, Military Preparedness, Honest Gov, + per-power Relations (UK/France/Spain, later Prussia). Clamp at **6** (Relations at **9**) (POST 3031, 3139, 3344). **Meter caps are policy/era-gated:** Rev-Budget can't exceed 2 until **Assume Rev War debts** passes (POST 2398, 2424); Econ Stab capped while on Spanish currency; **Mil Prep stuck at 4** with no standing army; **Quality of Life should be INACTIVE until a late-1800s labor event** (GM was running it erroneously, POST 2292). **Crisis:** Revenue-Budget=1 caps spending bills to 1/turn AND gives a **10% game-over chance** each Anytime-Evos phase (POST 360, 840).
- **Lingering phase** = lingering effects + **reversion-to-mean** + **volatility rolls** + **cabinet/Key-Advisor meter contributions** + corruption + war effects + **meter-change cascades** (e.g. Dom Stab→1 forces -1 Econ Stab) + per-ideology **Enthusiasm** shifts by party (capped at **3 movement/calc**, POST 2602) (POST 1070, 1267, 2640). ★ **"Dominant party" is ambiguous when control is split** — Red had 7/13 delegations but a Blue CC President; GM ruled **delegation majority = dominant party** (POST 413).
- **Anytime Evos** can **contradict each other same turn** (Charles Scott got both "Not Fit for Command" -1 Mil AND "Military Curiosity" +1 Mil; Carter Braxton holds both Integrity AND Two-Faced — "the math isn't mathing", POST 624, 636).
- **Federalism legislative depth** (1788+): two-chamber Congress, committees **per chamber** (Domestic/Foreign-Military/Economic/Judicial), **committee balance measured PER PARTY per committee** (POST 2970); chair needs **≥2 Legis OR Leadership** + prior service on that committee (CC experience does NOT carry over, POST 2178); chair can **block/replace only an EQUAL-OR-LOWER-Legis proposer's bill** and **can't block an Efficient proposer unless also Efficient** (POST 3065-3066); **amendments can't be packaged** and need **2/3** (House 54/76, Senate 18) (POST 2395, 3111); **amendments go to the states (10 Govs to ratify), NOT the President** (POST 3128); **Debater sways 1 / Orator 3** (House sways move in ~5-vote blocs, Senate 1, POST 3111, 3119). **Cabinet** (State/Treasury/War/AG + PMG/Key Advisor + Ministers): a pol **2 ideological shifts from the President can't serve** (Integrity excepted, POST 2579, 2989); **no cabinet firing until "Give the President Authority to Fire Cabinet Officers" passes** (POST 2578). **Job-hopping rule:** a pol who just took elected office can't immediately accept a cabinet seat (Pinckney blocked, POST 3273). **Foreign Affairs:** Ambassador **Admin** drives success (5 Admin = 75%); **"fails" vs "does not succeed" are distinct outcomes** (POST 2461); **NO "decrease relations" action exists** — "increase relations" can secretly hurt on a bad roll (POST 3136). **Executive Actions:** President takes actions = his **Command**; actions are one-time.

## 7. Design holes & GM rulings (hand-off for the gap log)

Numbered for the consolidation pass. The thread is a goldmine of under-specified areas the build must nail down.

1. **Career-track access** under-specified — appointments yes-if-no-one-else, **elections never**, gains round down; pols repeatedly left flagged on-track after being pulled. **Build should auto-manage track state.** (§2b; POST 322-338, 963.)
2. **Vermont has no home-state mapping** in rule 3.0.31 — "Schrödinger's state": VT pols are draftable/trackable though VT isn't in the US in 1772; `vcczar` unsure; consensus is "VT counts as US unless the VT event declines annexation," NY's claim can't prevail. Open request: map VT→NY or treat as foreign until petition. (POST 527-552, 554.) **Note:** pols from un-acquired foreign land (Caribbean, Jean-Jacques Dessalines) are **NOT draftable** until the US acquires it (POST 531).
3. **Which lobbies/laws are active at each start date is undocumented** — GM assigns ALL lobbies ("inactive ones just never get points"); players hand-clean the LegisActive/LegisProp tabs which ship with 2 centuries of irrelevant default laws. **Build must filter active legislation/lobbies by era/start-date.** (POST 367-372, 491-494, 510, 2342.)
4. **Senate-/Senator-requiring bills & Gov-actions appear pre-Constitution** where no Senate can exist; ruled void pre-Senate (rule 2.5) — **menu should filter by era.** (POST 1082, 1292.)
5. **No mechanic to reinstate Generals/Admirals for a post-RevWar war** — GM improvised; **war offices should re-open on any war.** (POST 1486.)
6. **Convention proposal-ordering / all-at-once** is an MP shortcut that won't match the computer version — **the computer must sequence delegates by Legis.** (POST 1730, 1740.)
7. **Constitutional Convention all-human deadlock unhandled** — the CPU-historical fallback assumes CPU factions; GM used a **weighted-roll by state support + a Convention-Pres sway** (Amendment Process landed on 2/3). **Need a human-deadlock rule.** (POST 1808, 1824.)
8. **No "Rogue Island" path** — states **can't ratify late / abstain / be embargoed**; CT & MD declined but were pulled in anyway because excluding them needs election/representation rules that don't exist. **Amendments are one-time votes with no multi-year ratification window.** Top AMPU-2 gap. (POST 1844, 1958, 1972.)
9. **Slave-State Compromise EV bumps never expire** and the **EV-on-abolition** question (do freed slaves still count under Option B? not citizens pre-14A?) is **genuinely unresolved**; EV changes should only apply at the **next census**, no mid-decade adjustments. **Needs a designed expiration/abolition rule.** (POST 1888, 2684-2704, 2690.)
10. **Post-12A legislature-decided states: vote-counting undecided** — Kingmakers vs **Gov + Senators + focus Reps headcount** (majority party wins, Gov breaks ties)? GM "always assumed Kingmaker = legislature abstraction," was corrected by a player; **both used interchangeably this thread, canonical interpretation unresolved.** (POST 3553-3561.)
11. **Statehood + its prerequisite territory bill same turn** — rule suspected to exist, never found; GM allowed a do-over. **Confirm whether allowed.** (POST 3076-3078.)
12. **No "decrease foreign relations" action exists** — intended omission or gap? "Increase relations" can secretly hurt on a bad roll. (POST 3136-3137.)
13. **"Set number of SCOTUS justices" packaging** — GM ruled it does NOT auto-package with "Establish the Court" but rules are silent; if the court passes and the number bill fails, **the game cannot advance.** (POST 3096.)
14. **"Invite Canada" only annexes Quebec** (Nova Scotia / "14th colony" missing) — content gap. (POST 364.)
15. **Treaty-of-Paris envoy** — War-Chart tab vs bill-requirements **conflict** on who negotiates. (POST 886-894.)
16. **Judicial CC committee only grants Justice expertise** — dead-end; wants more variety. (POST 250-251.)
17. **Duplicate-gain handling** (already-held rolled gain) — rule may have changed; GM ruled "gain nothing." (POST 607-608.)
18. **No default starting capital city** — "Move Capital" bills with no current capital; GM suggests defaulting to Philadelphia. **Set a starting capital.** (POST 1925.)
19. **Pre-12A there is genuinely NO convention mechanic** — the game must run **top-2 (highest-EV = President, 2nd = VP)** elections until the 12A-analog passes. (POST 2296, 2306.)
20. **Conversion base-chance is undocumented** and the party-leader conversion section is "disorganized / needs rewriting"; **adjacency-vs-Moderate/Pliable framing is inconsistent.** (POST 2524-2530, 2886.)
21. **Franchise divergence (likely deliberate):** **white women already have the vote** in this ruleset (POST 2670); women appear on career tracks; whether a woman can have a **military** career this era is questioned (POST 2847). Confirm intended franchise rules.
22. **Navy-officer scarcity** at game start vs Army; designer's fix is to let future admirals (Decatur/Bainbridge/Hull) be **drafted before age 25** — **conflicts with the shipped `draftYear = birthYear + 25` rule.** (POST 2626-2628.)
23. **"Limit President's war power" bill** is set to the Federalism era but is based on the 1973 War Powers Act — should enter the **Nuclear era** and be an amendment, not a statute. (POST 2354-2367.)
24. ★ **Manual Google-Sheets bookkeeping is error-prone and unsustainable** — career-track flags, formula breakage from editing the Master tab, missed gains, and ultimately **GM burnout that killed the game** (POST 3607). The single strongest cross-cutting case that **the build should own all upkeep/bookkeeping.** (POST 311-312, 439, 957, 3607.)
