## Which systems drive emergent narrative (the synthesis — the heart of this digest)

Ranked by how often they generate a "best moment" here. This is **design-value signal**: these
are the systems the app-that-subsumes-the-GM must keep producing surprises from.

1. **★ Pre-12th-Amendment presidential election + EC chaos (the single richest source).** First-place
   = President, second = VP; ties go to SCOTUS challenge, then to the House/Senate contingency.
   Produces: intended-VP-becomes-President via kingmaker scheming (POST 78/80, 1796), former-President-
   demoted-to-VP (POST 78), 8-candidate open scrambles (POST 24), VP decided by the Senate from two
   rival Federalists (POST 78), the "1848 clustertruck" (POST 4-5, 8 — 5 tied states + 6 faithless
   electors → 140-141-2, eligible for House contingency). vcczar: "pre-12th mechanics are probably a
   highlight of this game… when it rains it pours for SCOTUS election challenges" (POST 79).
2. **★ VP-succession / death / illness / scandal cascades (#61 territory).** "When it rains it pours":
   USS Princeton kills President Fillmore + Henry Clay + 2 faction leaders at once → VP Broom II
   ascends and immediately fills 2 SCOTUS seats + Chief Justice (POST 26-28, 153). A *near-cascade*
   in the 1948 game: ill President Barkley refuses to resign because Speaker Nixon is next in line, then
   a scandal hits, and players note that "all within one general events cycle" the line could have run
   Barkley→Nixon→Senate-PPT Vandenberg — and Vandenberg was only PPT because a NY Senator had just
   died (POST 102-107, 152). **No mechanism to fill a VP vacancy** is a live pain point (POST 152;
   an in-game Amendment was passed to add one, POST 153/499).
3. **★ Skill/trait/illness swings that rewrite who rises (the command-bootstrap engine).** Faction
   leaders get periodic skill rolls; a pol can gain/lose `Command`, `Leadership`, `Incompetent`,
   `Celebrity` and thereby become or stop-being presidential-eligible. Produces the corpus's signature
   absurdity: **90/94-yr-old botanist John Bartram elected first President** (Adams illness −3 Command
   → ineligible; Washington gains Incompetent; Bartram gains Celebrity then +1 Command; wins on
   tiebreakers) — the most-cited single moment (POST 43-49, 58, 137). Also Whipple's rise from a lucky
   naval battle (loses "obscure", gains "leadership") (POST 24).
4. **★ Alt-history divergence from real lives (the whole point).** Washington killed in the
   Revolution (POST 9, 21) → 29-yr-old Hamilton presides over the Constitutional Convention and swings
   one Georgia delegate to enfranchise non-white males in the 1788 Constitution (POST 17, 21-22);
   Reagan-as-Democrat (POST 111, 163), Robert E. Lee elected President as a Union-loyalist Whig 1872
   (POST 158), WEB DuBois elected in GA at 82 (POST 96-99), David Duke Gov of LA (POST 65, 168),
   Ron Paul names his son VP (POST 127-129).

Secondary engines (fewer mentions but clearly fun):
- **CPU "AI consistency" producing in-character behavior** — players *praise* it: anti-Mormon Whigs
  buy Mexican land for "Mormon removal" (POST 130-131); McCarthy investigates a raccoon that "tried to
  kill" Gov Warren as a communist plot (POST 93-94); a 3-term AI President Granger (POST 133-134, 153).
  This is the CPU-AI subsystem succeeding (contrast the many threads where it under-performs).
- **Congress / legislation horse-trading** — an in-game Compromise of 1850 with two Californias
  (POST 61-62, 85-86); a GOP-Congress passing a "Green New Deal" via cross-party deal after a
  catastrophic hurricane (POST 149, 155-157); 19th Amendment passing near-unanimously (POST 60).
- **Cabinet / confirmation feuds** — Hamilton vs Jefferson take each other out via Senate-committee
  tie-breaks, both permanently barred from future cabinet posts (POST 66-68); a full "unity cabinet"
  giving every electoral-vote-getter a seat (POST 48).
- **SCOTUS as kingmaker + election-decider** — IL awarded to Taft 6-3 ("an early Bush v Gore", POST
  119, 164); a 32-year-locked Whig Court (POST 167, 177); poll-taxes invalidated the same turn the AI
  governors learned to enact them (POST 177).
- **Governors / down-ballot color** — Reagan elected CA Gov as a Democrat (POST 163), Huckabee Gov of
  two states (POST 169), husband-and-wife CA House delegation (POST 72).
- **"Worst moments" are also fun** — players explicitly run a "worst playtest moments" sub-genre
  (POST 25, 65, 150, 168); the framing is comedic, not a complaint. Design note: the *darkly funny
  failure* (Haley ignores a 16×-Katrina hurricane on 3-1 cabinet advice; POST 149-150, 155) is as
  valued as the triumph.

**Takeaway for the GM-subsume charter:** the memorable moments come overwhelmingly from
**(a) the pre-12th EC/contingency/SCOTUS-challenge stack, (b) succession/death/illness/scandal
chains, and (c) per-2-year skill/trait rolls reshuffling eligibility** — i.e. systemic
*interactions*, not scripted events. An app that keeps these three loops intact (and surfaces them
legibly, as these write-ups do) reproduces the fun. CPU in-character consistency is the sleeper
requirement — players cite it as *delightful* when it works.

## Concrete mechanics / rules glimpsed incidentally (for the consolidation agent)

- **Faction-leader skill roll** happens "every two years"; faction leaders are the main path to gain
  Admin, hence early Presidents/cabinet are drawn from leaders (POST 24 step 3-4, 49). Corroborates the
  command-bootstrap finding.
- **Era-of-Independence phase gating:** players note the Presidency/Executive phase "isn't supposed to
  kick in" in the Independence era and rules say "skip the Executive Phase for the Era of Independence"
  (POST 50, 53) — and cross-party nominee restriction is assumed OFF in that era (POST 49). Corroborates
  the era-as-subsystem-gate finding (founding subsystems OFF until 1788).
- **House contingency threshold:** "needs to win 16 states" in 1848 (POST 8); the contingency vote is by
  state delegation.
- **Pre-rolled census for new states:** two Californias get 5 EV (slave/Lower) and 3 EV (free/Upper),
  rolled at admission (POST 62). Corroborates event/territory-driven EV.
- **"Penalty for keeping the same party leader too long"** is a real, escalating mechanic that caps
  dynasties — cited as why a 3rd/4th term is hard (POST 83, 133, 138). vcczar adds: a party that swaps
  the *candidate* often enough can skirt it while holding kingmakers (POST 83).
- **Kingmakers decide pre-12th presidential elections** "regardless of what's going on with the
  meters" — flagged by the dev as a possible balance problem (POST 81-82); proposed fix = dom-stab +
  party-pref drop for re-electing an unpopular incumbent (POST 82). (Kingmakers ARE shipped:
  `src/pages/Kingmakers.tsx`.)
- **"Acting President" ruling (designer-authoritative):** under a hypothetical lifetime-term Presidency
  amendment, if the President dies the VP is **acting only until the next election** — does NOT inherit
  the lifetime term, does NOT get the dice roll to shed "acting", cannot pass the exec action removing
  the "acting" tag (vcczar, POST 140-142, 146-147). Note an "acting" tag + a roll/exec-action to
  *remove* it both exist as mechanics.
- **Economy can be maxed by flavor actions** — Dewey "opened brothels in NYC" to max the economy
  (POST 91-92); a player-cited funny exploit. (Not found as a named event in shipped data — likely a
  pres/gov action outcome; candidate to verify.)
- **House under "3-2-1 focus reps"** in the ~1916 era (POST 71-72) — a per-era House-size/representation
  rule worth noting.
- **Climate/economic "crisis that threatens to end the game"** — there is a loss/game-over condition
  tied to unaddressed crises (POST 151, 487-style framing in POST 151). Corroborates a terminal-state
  mechanic.
