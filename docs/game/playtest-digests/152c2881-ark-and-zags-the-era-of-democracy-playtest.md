## §2. Election & convention engine (canonical — batch 10 never reached this)

### ★ Platform scoring (ch7 POST 548; ch21 POST 1750; ch33 POST 2530)
A party's election platform is **5 planks** (Economic / Domestic / Judicial /
Foreign-military / **a Presidential pledge to enact if elected**). Each plank lists
±pts to ideology/interest **cards**; cards are tallied **by-card → by-faction → by-party**,
then three yes/no questions set Party Pref ±1:
1. Do the party's platform points exceed the other party's? 2. Does the party's
**lowest-scoring** faction exceed the other party's lowest? 3. Does the **nominee's
personal ideology** score the most (or tied)? — The plank menu IS the era agenda
(1832 Red planks: Establish the Bank, **Abolish Slavery Amendment**, **Enact Spoils
System**, internal improvements, embargo).

### ★ General-election 8-stage sequence (ch7 POST 584)
**1st Pres Debate → 1st Action Phase → VP Debate → 2nd Pres Debate → 2nd Action Phase
→ 3rd Pres Debate → October Surprise → Election Day** (some unlocked by era/traits).
- **Action Phase:** send the **VP or faction leader** to *give a speech* (any state) or
  *shore up support* (the surrogate's **home region**); each rolls a d6 for ±1 in a
  state/region. **The presidential candidate CANNOT campaign in person until the Primary
  Era** (era-gated; "campaigning as nominee was socially forbidden," POST 598). An
  incumbent may also *Use the Incumbent Power of the Office* (ch15 POST 1232).
- **Scandal roll** each phase: **skipped for Integrity** pols; only bites a
  **Controversial** candidate.
- **Per-state result = d6 vs d6**, ties broken; reaching the EV majority = president-elect
  (ch7 POST 591–597). Loser of the presidency takes **−1 on all future presidential
  bids** (ch7 POST 606).

### ★ Brokered convention (ch21 POST 1717–1750; ch32 POST 2465) — refines #111 / DH-57
- Each faction runs a **major candidate** (with a **nominator**; an **Orator** nominator
  gives a momentum bonus, rolled on a d6) plus **minor / favorite-son** candidates.
- **Nomination threshold differs by party:** the National Republican convention needs
  **2/3** (436/649), the Democratic-Republican needs **50%+1** (343/685) (ch32 POST
  2465–2466) — the historical 2/3 rule modeled for one party only.
- **Command-gated convention actions:** a candidate with N command may take **N** of:
  *force a rules change*, *offer a presidential promise* (to a candidate with **fewer
  delegates**) for an endorsement, *make an appeal to credibility*, *drop out with /
  without endorsement* (ch21 POST 367/425). **Presidential-promise buyouts decide the
  1832 nomination** (Cheves buys Key's and Clay's endorsements with VP and Sec-State
  promises, POST 444–488).
- **★ VP-selection scoring** = an **11-question balance questionnaire** (ch21 POST 1721):
  VP from another faction? ticket has a Mod/Cons/Lib? age gap? someone >50 / <60?
  incumbent ticket? one can-be-independent / not-in-office? big+small state? different
  regions? VP obscure? → numeric VP score (the lowest-scoring faction tends to get the VP).
- **Delegate classes (5 categories, EV×1…×4 with party-pref modifiers):** Zagnut moved
  to **AI-assign** them (§4); the **1840 convention reverted to human-set classes**
  (ch32 POST 2466) — the fork stayed unsettled in practice.

## §4. ★★ The canonical 4-step enthusiasm-shift model — SETTLES the #18/#51 fork (ch33 POST 195–207)

The final chunk publishes the **legislation-scoring → enthusiasm** rule **verbatim**.
After a Congress's bills are scored **by-card → by-faction**, four shifts fire (and
**stack**):

1. **Faction that gained the MOST points for the dominant party** → its ideology cards
   move **+1 enthusiasm toward the dominant party**.
2. **Faction that gained the LEAST points for the dominant party** → **−1 enthusiasm away
   from the dominant party**.
3. **Faction that gained the MOST points for the opposition party** → **+1 enthusiasm
   toward the DOMINANT party** ("the dominant party is taking care of the opposition
   faction's needs") — **only if it actually gains points** (not merely loses the least).
4. **Faction that scored the LEAST points from the opposition party** → **+2 enthusiasm
   toward the OPPOSITION party** ("furious at the incumbent") — **even if it gains points.**

**This is the same 4-step reshuffle `drums` independently documented for #51.** So batch
11 is a **fork-RESOLUTION**, not a new fork: the GAs here converged on the canonical
`drums`/#51 model (applied per-Congress on the legislation tally), not on Ted's batch-10
"every state unless penalized" / Matt's "primaries only" variants. The model is paired
with the **±cap** (meters clamp to avoid overflow, ch32 POST 155–177) and the standard
**Lingering 10-step** meter movement (ch4 POST 360). **Recommendation: log #51's
algorithm as settled to this version; mark the #18 "which states does enthusiasm apply
to" sub-question still open** (this model expresses shifts per-ideology-card, leaving the
state-application step to the meter→election map).
