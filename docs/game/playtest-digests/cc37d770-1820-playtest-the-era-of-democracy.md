## §3. Mechanics exercised + GA rulings

### ★ Player-controlled SCOTUS — the live design debate (POST 420-435, refines #52)
A player-controlled Supreme Court appeared "for the first time in years" (ebrk, POST
420) and triggered a multi-party design argument. **Resolution (the canonical rule):**
- **Votes are NOT player-decided — they follow @vcczar's by-ideology voting chart;
  ties roll a die** (matt, POST 424; the build's #52 model). A player Justice does NOT
  get to vote individual cases.
- **Delay/dismiss IS a player action** with caveats (ebrk, POST 430): a player can
  **delay** a case but can **only DISMISS a case brought by a Gov Action**, never a
  historical case; and **must hear at least one case** (can't delay them all). A
  majority-party-on-the-court always hears; CPU only delays when it lacks the numbers.
- **Worcester v. Georgia** → NAY 5-1 (flagged "historical outcome"), lone dissenter
  Todd; **Charles River Bridge** → initial 3-3, no swaying succeeds → **split affirms
  the lower court, randomly rolled AYE (ahistorical)** (POST 442-443). Confirms the
  **5-5/tie-affirms-lower-court + ahistorical-flag** mechanic (#52 from `hd`).
- Designer-intent flavor: ebrk + Lars think a **player-controlled court is "how the
  game should be"** (POST 421-422); Vee wants it **trait-gated** (only Integrity/
  Disharmonious/Predictable justices auto-vote; Controversial/Pliable/Lackey let the
  player decide, gated by a Manipulative leader) — an unbuilt design wish (POST 426).

### ★ The meter→election interpretation (the biggest GA ruling, POST 569/575/618, refines #18/#51)
Ted re-derived (from his 2000 playtest) and published a **concrete enthusiasm-processing
rule** — load-bearing for the election engine:
- The **non-enthusiasm meters MOVE the per-ideology enthusiasm boxes** (e.g.
  Revenue/Budget "-1 for Progs/Libs" → shift Prog + Lib enthusiasm +1 Red each).
- **Enthusiasm boxes then apply to EVERY state** (a +3 Blue LW-Pop = +3 to all Blue
  LW-Pops everywhere) **UNLESS a state penalizes that ideology** — Ted's deliberate
  divergence from V's model (V's version "takes populists a huge hit in every state…
  in reality that's when populists rise — Tea Party, Bernie, MAGA"). This is a
  GA-vs-designer model fork the build must pick between.
- **Hard ±3 cap on ideology + party-pref bonuses** (state-specific bonuses uncapped).
- Matt's alternate (POST 569): enthusiasm only matters in **primaries** + in states
  that lean that ideology — i.e. **two GAs run two different enthusiasm models.**
  **Published "State of the Meters" table** before the 1822 election (POST 618).

### ★ House abstraction + focus-Rep incumbency (the most contested ruling, POST 665-696, refines #55/A9)
The House is **abstracted**: only "focus Reps" exist, each controlling a roughly equal
share of a state's hidden seats; **Rep count = (EV − 2) / 5, rounded up** (POST 643).
KevinStorm + Ted argued for ~30 posts over whether incumbency is seat-specific:
- **vcczar weighs in (POST 676): focus Reps "should be viewed as incumbents"** of a
  specific seat. **Final rule (POST 682/696):** you keep the **+2 incumbency bonus
  only in your own seat/district**, EXCEPT in **census/redistricting years (1822, 1832,
  1842…)** when you may run in any seat and keep it. A Rep whose focus-seat is
  eliminated in redistricting keeps the bonus elsewhere.
- **Census timing ruling:** the decennial census changes House seats in the **following
  half-term** (1820-22) and is reflected in the **first election of the new decade
  (1822)**, with EV/presidential impact at the **first year-ending-in-2 presidential
  election** (so 1810 census → 1812 pres; 1830 → 1832). POST 683-686.
- vcczar's long-term wish: **every real US House district + 30,000 more pols** in the
  game eventually (POST 704) — flagged as out-of-scope-for-now.

### Other rulings (terse)
- **PPT gains nothing** (no traits, only 100 pts) because pre-Maj-Leader the PPT
  rotated; an incumbent Speaker/PPT **can't be challenged** unless they fail
  prerequisites OR the challenger's ideology enthusiasm drops below neutral (POST
  159, 168, 172). Minority leadership offices **don't exist yet** this era (POST 156).
- **Kingmaker rule (2.1.7):** an **active Kingmaker must be ≥35 and NOT on a career
  track**; max one active Kingmaker per state; **no age limit on protégés** (POST 117).
- **New replacements (incl. faction leaders) get NO gains until they hold the post
  through the next appointment/election phase — EXCEPT military positions, always
  eligible on appointment** (verbatim 2.4 note, POST 291). Ted revoked a Command point
  he'd wrongly given a mid-turn faction-leader replacement.
- **Controversial Senior General needs a full Senate vote** (no Maj Leader to waive);
  Gaines confirmed 26/48 (needs 25). Non-Controversial general (Zachary Taylor)
  **auto-confirms** (POST 309-347).
- **Card-distribution order-of-operations** (Ted's 3 dilemmas, POST 149-154): (1) a
  card **isn't distributed if no faction has ≥5 of the relevant experience** (e.g. no
  Environment card in 1820); (2) an interest card conflicting with a faction's
  ideology goes to the **non-conflicting** faction, else not given; (3) **era minimums
  are MINIMUMS not maximums** — but the **bordering-ideology rule is the "prime
  directive"** (give a 1-Trad faction the Trad card so it can also hold the required
  RW-Pop card). Refines #24.
- **Faction-nickname "era exits" column:** a nickname **cannot be used once its
  exit-era begins** (POST 215-216) — but Ted notes for the forum he just uses handles;
  the constraint is "**true for the computer game once programmed**" (POST 227),
  confirming the per-(party,era) nickname-table gate (#40) is a build requirement.
- **Sudden-vacancy fill order (3.0.28, POST 837):** death/retirement/appointment-out
  → can leave a seat empty in some cases; but the **normal Senate appointment is
  separate.** After a long argument the GA **changed the appointment-eligibility rule
  mid-thread (POST 859):** *your-party-not-on-CT → your-party-on-CT → opposite-party-
  not-on-CT → opposite-party-on-CT → generate a 1-skill pol.* You **cannot leave a
  full-term Senate seat vacant** + **cannot pull a career-track pol if any non-CT pol
  of your party is eligible** (preserves the strategic cost of career-tracking). Senate
  appointees must be **30+** (a 27-yr-old was rejected, POST 882).
- **New states init with era-keyed industry, not zero:** Arkansas + Michigan start
  with 1 Agriculture (AR also +1 Plantation) per the era industry table (POST 532-534).
- **Diplomacy roster is era-gated (4 powers):** UK / France / Spain / Russia (Prussia
  = the "minister since 1797"; Germany doesn't exist yet; players want a Prussia→Germany
  resigns-ambassador event at the Franco-Prussian War). Corroborates #25b era-gated
  power list. POST 528, 535-546.

## §5. Bugs / friction / design holes

### Dataset + data-tagging bugs (build-relevant)
- **★ DATASET DEMOGRAPHIC CORRUPTION (BUG class):** the shared "Master" tab rendered
  **every politician as Black, openly LGBT, several as women, one Asian** — "James
  Monroe comes up as Black and gay" (POST 104, 106, 125). Root cause = a **column
  mismatch from trimmed columns** (the player tabs and Master fell out of sync; bios
  labeled "alternate state 2"). Fixed by re-deriving trait formulas with INDEX/MATCH.
  The forum sheet is a fragile hand-me-down; the **build owning the data model
  eliminates this entire class** (reinforces DH-36 the bookkeeping-fragility theme).
- **★ Bill EV-effect data is mis-signed (corroborates `nuke` DH-53):** several laws are
  worded "**Until passed, −1 EV in each state**" instead of "**When passed, +1 EV**" —
  Ted: *"Nobody is checking laws that were NOT passed when calculating EVs… should have
  just been a simple 'when passed' "* (POST 462-466). The Native-American laws "punish
  EVs until passed." **Per-bill EV/meter effect tables need an authored, sign-checked
  pass** (the "Offer Land Grants" and Indian-Removal laws are concrete instances).
- **Lingering tab pulled phantom "Alabama Territory"/"Arkansas Territory"** rows after
  those became states (POST 522) — stale-territory data not cleaned on statehood.
- **Trait-conflict not flagged in boot data (corroborates DH-27):** the GA had to hand-
  build conditional formatting for "**3.0.34**" trait conflicts across all 10 sheets
  (POST 577, 596) — the conflict check isn't native; boot/draft seed data slips
  conflicting traits through.

### Rules gaps / GA rulings flagged for fixing
- **★ "No rules for how to CREATE a game" (NEW design hole):** the most repeated
  meta-complaint — the rulebook is written for *players*, not GMs; there is **no
  documented scenario-setup procedure**, so house rules (strip-Command-at-boot,
  inaugural career-track seeding details) are improvised and undisclosed. Ted: *"rules,
  or a how-to guide, on how to create a playtest is maybe our greatest need but to date
  nobody has stepped up to write one"* (POST 92). **Directly motivates the build's
  scenario-boot pipeline as the canonical setup spec.** NEW.
- **★ Career-track "can't run/be appointed" rule is self-contradictory (sharpens
  DH-25):** 2.1 says CT pols "will be unavailable to run in elections / to be
  appointed," but **many other sections let CT pols be pulled by election/appointment**
  — Ted: *"the rules on this definitely need work… we'll ignore the 'cannot run or be
  appointed' rule because it isn't consistently applied"* (POST 850, 859). The build
  must pick ONE coherent CT-eligibility rule. Corroborates DH-25 + DH-56 (conflicting
  rule-sets) from a 3rd thread.
- **Senate-class boot data wrong (corroborates DH-24):** the GA ran the 1822 midterm as
  **Class I** then discovered it should be **Class II**, forcing a full re-submission
  of every Senate appointment ("Oh for fuck's sake… I love this game a lot and I also
  really hate it," POST 758). A Senate-class verifier is needed (DH-24, now 2-thread).
- **House seat-count + redistricting chaos:** the GA mis-counted Reps multiple times
  (adapting a "modern" sheet to 1820), repeatedly added/removed/merged races mid-
  election ("either I was looking at a wrong map or there was a gas leak," POST 733).
  The (EV−2)/5 formula and per-seat biases are unbuilt in the sheet — pure scaling-wall
  tedium (A9). The 1822 midterm took **~10 forum pages / 8 real days** ("the
  congressional election from hell," POST 938).
- **Replace-proposal Passive/Delegator gate (POST 456-459):** Ted/Ark agree a **Passive
  party leader should be 25% (or 0%) to bother replacing a low-Legis proposal**, and
  Micromanager is exec-only — a proposed 2.6 rule that "Anthony already coded this
  section so the edit might never be incorporated" (live forum-drives-build evidence).
- **Diplomacy meter labels** ("Unhappy Neutral"/"Happy Neutral") flagged as
  unintuitive; players want "Cordial"/"Unfriendly" (POST 535) — UX nit.

### The campaign-killer (DH-36 class)
