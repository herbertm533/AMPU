## §1 — Scenario-boot model (THE HEADLINE)

**This is the digest's single most valuable contribution.** Every prior
modern-era thread (`modern` digest) entered modern from a continuous 1948
campaign that had played 60 in-game years forward. **This thread is the first
record of how a fresh modern-era scenario BOOTS from cold.** The shape is
strikingly similar to the shipped 1856 boot and the documented 1788 boot — all
three are mid-government clean-starts.

### What the 2012 boot loads (from a "pre-built sheet", POST 1)

Rodja: *"setting up the sheet as I used the pol data from my old 2012 sheet"*
(POST 1). The boot is a **single pre-built sheet** containing:

1. **Two parties only** (Dems / Reps), each **pre-split into 5 named factions**
   — B1–B5 + R1–R5 (the canonical per-party faction cap, corroborated 6th
   time). Each faction is loaded with archetype politicians (the 3-name
   shorthand below), era-tuned ideology cards, interest cards, and lobby cards.
   **No new card types are introduced.** The "Era of Populism" identity is
   **emergent from the seed configuration** (which cards each faction holds at
   boot), not from new mechanical content.

   RW Media + Isolationists). **B1's deck IS "Bernie-populism"** (LW Pop +

2. **Sitting government, fully pre-loaded** (POST 1, 12, 419, 422, 426, 475):
   - **Pres. Obama (B5)** is the 2012 incumbent facing reelection
   - **VP Biden (B2)** is incumbent VP
   - Cabinet has incumbents (Sebelius / Geithner / Hillary as "former Sec of

     Kennedy, Sotomayor, Scalia, RBG, Breyer)

   - **No faction leaders** (selection happens *after* the first general

4. **50-state union, not 53.** DC appears (POST 264) but **Cuba and Puerto
   Rico are NOT states.** This differs from the `modern` digest's 1948→ run,
   which generated a 53-state alt-history after 60 years of in-game annexation
   events. **A fresh 2012 boot starts at 50 + DC.**

| Boot | Era at start | Pre-loaded gov | Faction leaders? | Career tracks? | EV map |

### Rule 3.0.18 — procedural pols begin in the 2020 draft (POST 1031–1033)

Blockmon flags **rulebook 3.0.18**: in the Era of Populism, the **2020 draft is
when CPU-generated rookies start appearing** (one new pol per state per cycle).
Before 2020, drafts are "really small handful of real ones" only. **This means
procedural pol generation (gap-log E8/#43) is era-coded by the rulebook** —
generation kicks in at a specific year, not when the real-person dataset
exhausts. The dataset-exhaustion model (`modern` digest) and the era-coded
model (this thread) **must reconcile**: build needs both a year trigger AND a
dataset-exhaustion fallback.

## §2 — Era-of-Populism mechanics & content (era-as-data)

### Defining issues — DOUBLE-POINTS multipliers (POST 699)

GM Rodja: *"Keep in mind that bills related to Climate Crisis or Immigration
are worth double points this turn."* **Era-coded double-points scoring** on
specific issue categories. The era's mechanized tension. Populism-era doubles
**Climate Crisis** and **Immigration**; analogous era-doubles likely exist for
prior eras (slavery in Nationalism, tariff in Gilded, etc.) but this is the
first explicit double-multiplier rule documented.

### APOCALYPSE — Planet Health 10-year game-end clock (POST 542, 548)

The single biggest **new endgame condition** in the gap log. After Bill #3
lifted drilling subsidies and Planet Health crashed into the bottom band:

> "END OF THE WORLD IN 10 YEARS IF WE DON'T FIX THIS." (GM Rodja, POST 542)

A meter falling into a specific bottom tier starts a **10-game-year countdown
clock to mandatory game-end** (climate apocalypse). Distinct from
`triggersGameEnd` era-event end-states already in the build: this is
**meter-driven**, not event-driven. Players acknowledge climate is the most
likely game-end path for the populist arc.

### Era-event-creates-office: Secretary of Environment & Climate

The 2015–17 Climate Crisis bill slate (POST 699, then 1100) included **"Create
Department of Environment and Climate" (Wasserman Schultz)**. It passes, and
the **2017 cabinet has a new "Environment & Climate" seat (Sally Jewell)**.
**Legislation creates a real cabinet office mid-game.** Extends the
institutional-layer gap (#66): not era-coded `cabinetSeatsForYear`, but
**bill-creates-seat** as a generic mechanic.

## §4 — Committee / legislation refinements

### Chair-can-block-one + chair-can-package (POST 487–507)

Two committee mechanics confirmed cleanly in sequence:

1. **Chair-blocks-one:** committee chair may BLOCK exactly **one proposal**
   from the slate **before** the vote (POST 488, 489, 490). Then the committee
   votes on the remaining.
2. **Chair-packages:** after passing votes, the chair **packages passing bills
   together** OR leaves them separate (POST 502–507). **Packaging is
   load-bearing** — 4 unrelated GOP bills jammed into Bill #3 (POST 507, 542)
   to amortize sign/veto cost across the package.

Together these sharpen the chair-block (#8) and bundling (#9) gap-log entries.

### Named-Senator filibuster + 60-vote cloture (POST 519, 521)

Specific Senators flagged "can-filibuster" by trait/ideology: **Rand Paul, Tom
Coburn, Mike Lee, Ilhan Omar**. Omar filibustered Bill 2 (Stand Your Ground +
Federal Prisons); needed **60 votes for cloture**, got **58 — bill killed**.
Matches the `drums` deterministic per-Senator filibuster spec (#10).
**5+ era confirmed.**

### Iron-Fist Sen Maj Leader = auto-54-votes (POST 523)

McConnell (5 legis + Iron Fist) passed everything else unilaterally.
**Iron-Fist SML floor effect ≈ 54 automatic yes votes** — sharpens §25.9 / #77
Iron-Fist overload (one specific cascade of the overloaded trait).

### Bill-signing triggers ideology + lobby + meter ticks simultaneously (POST 539, 542)

A signed bill triggers **points to ideologies** (±50/100/150/250/350) **+ meter
ticks** (Planet Health, Economic Stability) **+ lobby points** ("+100 for High
Tech", "+50 for Human Rights") **in a single resolution event**. Sharpens #12
(bill scoring iterates all faction cards) — confirms scoring is ALL THREE
simultaneously, not sequential phases.

### Iron-Fist players issue conditional vote-rules (POST 1111)

Player issues an **explicit conditional-vote policy** for the Iron-Fist SML:
> "McConnell will vote NAY to all Libs, Progs, and LW Pops with less than 3
> Admin, AYE to everyone else."

This is a **CPU-handler insight**: Iron-Fist controllers can publish
**conditional vote rules** that the engine then applies to all controlled
votes. Sharpens the #25 / #77 Iron-Fist cluster — the rule is data-driven, not
hardcoded.
