## What this thread is

The **first captured 1916-start**, exercising the realignment-hinge era (1896–1932, §5)
directly — the era documented as the bridge with **no modern polarity yet**. It is a solo
roleplay (one human voicing all 10 factions, with subjective dice for inter-faction
goodwill/grudges), so GA-level not designer-authoritative; its value is **(a)** a clean
demonstration of the hinge polarity in faction-roster form, **(b)** an explicit era-evo
ordering bug that both the GM and the community flag as needing a WWI-end prerequisite, and
**(c)** corroboration of the era-evo / random-event texture of the Progressive era. No
scenario exists for 1916 (runs on `modern`/`progressive` tuning — unbuilt), so every delta
is against designed-but-unbuilt content.

## ★ The era-evo ORDERING BUG (the headline finding)

- In the **2.4.3 Era Evos** of the **first half-term (1916-18)**, the GM fired
  **"Czechoslovakia Gains Independence from Austria"** and **"Hungary Gains Independence from
  Austria"** *before WWI had ended* — flagging it himself: "**I wonder if this should have a
  requirement that ww1 has ended**, but I'm not actually that knowledgeable about it" (POST
  31). The community confirmed: "**These two should not trigger until after WWI is over**"
  (POST 34). Both events fired in the **same events phase as the WWI-entry decision** (the
  Unrestricted-Submarine-Warfare "Enter the World War" choice was still being made, POST 31).
- This is a **missing era-event PREREQUISITE / wrong ordering**: the Austro-Hungarian-
  successor-state events should gate on a prior event (WWI's end), not fire on the calendar
  schedule alongside WWI's *start*. **Corroborates DH-60** (the era-event territory/prereq
  gating gap — `dem1820`/`arkzag`/`smallbugs` flagged the same class: "Stubborn Cherokee" /
  "Force Open Trade with Japan" firing without the prerequisite). It also corroborates the
  **#92/#109** picture: era *content* is schedule-fired and **needs selective per-event
  preconditions** (a few events keyed to a prior choice) — exactly the WWI-end gate missing
  here. → logged as a **NEW bug DH-68** (Progressive-era successor-state events lack a
  WWI-end prerequisite) cross-referencing DH-60.
- Side discussion (POST 34-37): why Hungary/Czechoslovakia but no **Poland**? The collapse of
  Austria-Hungary is **flavor** (no countries are "in the game" beyond mention); future-era
  events hint at a "Polish Great Power" — confirms these are decorative era-flavor events,
  not a modeled foreign-state system (consistent with #106's "engine wars vs flavor" split).

## ★ Hinge polarity (the 1916 faction roster — do NOT read as modern)

**Faction leaders + nicknames selected** (POST 25-26) make the hinge explicit:

- **BLUE 1 = Eugene V. Debs → "the Socialists"** (LW Pop/Prog/Lib; gains Iron Fist) — the
  far-LEFT pole sits in the BLUE party.
- **BLUE 2 = President Woodrow Wilson → "the Internationalists"** (the in-power Democratic
  faction; Wilson auto-installed as Democratic Party Leader, POST 26).
- **BLUE 3 = Charles Culberson → "Moderate Democrats"**; **BLUE 4 = Nelson A. Miles →
  "Conservative Democrats"**; **BLUE 5 = Benjamin Tillman → "Traditionalist Democrats"**
  (the segregationist Solid-South right wing — *inside* the Blue party, the hinge analog of
  the Dixiecrats-inside-Blue pattern, cf. #108).
- **RED 1 = William Borah → "the Populists"** (with La Follette + Cummins + Pinchot — the
  **progressive/insurgent Republican** wing; LW-Pop enthusiasm shifts RED, POST 26) — i.e.
  **progressivism and left-populism live on BOTH B1 (Debs) AND R1 (Borah/La Follette)**.
- **RED 2 = James R. Mann → "the Imperialists"** (with **Theodore Roosevelt** + Hughes +
  Stimson — TR's faction is the *Imperialist* Republicans, not a modern-right faction).
- **RED 3 = Joseph Cannon → "Wall Street Republicans"**; **RED 4 = John W. Weeks →
  "Schoolbook Conservatives"**; **RED 5 = Samuel Young → "Conservative Republicans."**

So the board has **progressive/LW-pop cards on both parties**, the segregationist far-right
inside BLUE, and TR labelled "Imperialist" in RED 2. **This corroborates the §5 hinge
framing**: the modern BLUE=left / RED=right mapping does **not** apply in 1916.

## Bugs / dataset / friction

- **★ DH-68 (NEW): Czechoslovakia/Hungary-independence Era Evos fire without a WWI-end
  prerequisite** (POST 31, 34) — they triggered in the same phase as WWI's *start*, when they
  should gate on WWI's *end*. The clearest live instance of a missing era-event prereq in the
  Progressive band; corroborates **DH-60** (era-event prereq/territory gating).
- **No-candidate-withdraw in leadership elections** (POST 26): the Republican PL race had "no
  option for a candidate to withdraw," forcing a run-off and a coin-flip outcome — a friction
  in the leadership-IRV model worth a withdraw step (relates to #110).
- "**Military pensions** event has 3 recipients — does this count as a decision or are they
  pass/fail options?" (POST 30) — an unresolved event-shape ambiguity (multi-recipient
  pass/fail vs decision), minor.
- janetp built **infographics / Westminster seat charts** (POST 11, 20-22) and shared hex
  codes — reinforces the "the build should ship charts/visualizations" community wish (cf.
  #166 industry-tally + the general data-viz desire), not a mechanic.
