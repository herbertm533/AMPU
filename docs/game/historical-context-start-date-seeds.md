# AMPU — Historical Context: Start-Date Boot Seeds (15 windows)

Companion to `docs/game/sources/start-date-info/analysis.md` (the gitignored
`Start_Date_Info.xlsx` preprocess). This file is a **light historical grounding
pass** over the scenario-boot SEED matrix: who really held office at each of the
15 playable start-date windows. It documents (a) that the sheet's era-appropriate
office/state evolution is historically sound, (b) the real-history anchor for each
transition, and (c) the **start-date turnover ambiguities** and **compression
divergences** the game-master/PM must know when seeding a single boot snapshot.

Scope note: this is well-known US history; names are NOT individually verified
(2,480 of them). Verified here = the structural transitions and the mid-window
successions that force a single-snapshot choice. Confidence tags: **[C]**onsensus
(cited), **[V]**erified-this-ingest (cited below), **[I]**nference/synthesis.

## Office evolution — historically grounded (verified anchors)

The sheet's changing office SET (not a flat roster) tracks real US institutional
history. Each transition confirmed:

| Transition in sheet | Real-history anchor | Conf. |
|---|---|---|
| Interior appears from 1856 window | Dept of the Interior created **Mar 3, 1849** (30th Congress) | **[V]** |
| Agriculture appears ~1892 window | Made a **cabinet** dept **Feb 9, 1889** (25 Stat. 659); USDA existed 1862 sub-cabinet | **[V]** |
| Commerce & Labor / split | Dept of Commerce and Labor created **1903**; **split into Commerce + Labor 1913** | **[V]** (matches "empty `Sec of Commerce and Labor` reserved row") |
| Sec of War → Sec of Defense (1948 window) | **National Security Act of 1947**; Sec of Defense effective **Sep 18, 1947** (Forrestal 1st) | **[V]** |
| Sec of Navy vanishes after 1928 | Navy Dept folded into DoD via **1949 NSA amendments** | **[V]** |
| PM Gen cabinet 1840→1948, `n/a` from 1972 | Postmaster left cabinet **Jul 1, 1971** (Postal Reorganization Act 1970; USPS operational Jul 1971) | **[V]** |
| Fed Reserve Chair from 1916 window | Federal Reserve Act **Dec 1913**; Board operating 1914 → first chair appears 1916 | **[V]** |
| SCOTUS 6→7→9 seats | Started at 6 (Judiciary Act 1789); fluctuated 5–10; **settled at 9 by Judiciary Act of 1869** (last change) | **[V]** |
| Formal Maj/Min Leaders + Whips only from 1916 window | Party floor-leadership formalized **early 20th c.** (~1910s–1920s); Speaker + Pres Pro Tem from 1789 | **[C]** |
| DHS (`Sec of HL Sec`) + DNI from 2012 window | **Homeland Security Act Nov 2002**; **DNI created 2004** (IRTPA) | **[V]** |
| VA / Energy / Education (2000 window) | Energy **1977**, Education **1979**, VA cabinet **1989** | **[C]** |

**Conclusion: the office evolution is historically sound.** The only "reserved but
empty" rows (`Sec of Commerce and Labor`, `Amb to Ger/Japan/Israel`) reflect real
gaps (combined 1903–1913 dept; ambassadorships the sheet chose not to fill), not errors.

- **13** (1788): original states (RI last to ratify, May 1790).
- **16** (1800-02): + VT(14,1791), KY(15,1792), TN(16,1796). **[V]**
- **24** (1820-22): … Maine 23rd (1820), **Missouri 24th (Aug 1821)** — the Compromise pair. **[V]**
- **26** (1840-42): + AR(25,1836), MI(26,1837). **[I]**
- **31** (1856-58): … **California 31st (1850)**; MN 32nd not until May 1858 → 31 correct. **[V]**
- **37** (1868-70): … **Nebraska 37th (Mar 1867)**. **[V]**
- **44** (1892-94): + the 1889–90 wave (ND/SD/MT/WA 39–42; ID/WY 43–44). **[I]**

## Divergence flags for the game-master / PM

**A. Single-snapshot turnover (highest-value — the sheet must pick ONE holder for a 2-year window, but real history changed mid-window):**

1. **1840-1842 — Harrison/Tyler.** The sheet's `President = W.H. Harrison` + `VP = Tyler`
   pairing was real for only **31 days (Mar 4 – Apr 4, 1841)**. Harrison died; Tyler
   became president (first VP succession, "His Accidency," left with no VP). If the game
   seeds Harrison as a living president for a 1840 boot, that is a deliberate
   fixed-point simplification — GM should be aware a Harrison-death event is the real
   arc. **[C]**

2. **1972-1974 — Nixon/Agnew.** `President = Nixon` + `VP = Agnew` was real only through
   **Oct 10, 1973** (Agnew resigned; Ford confirmed Dec 1973; Nixon resigned Aug 9, 1974
   → Ford president). This window contains TWO successions; the seed captures neither. **[V]**

3. **Inauguration-boundary picks (sheet chose the *incoming* president, not the incumbent
   at the calendar start of the window):** 1800-02 = Jefferson (not Adams), 1856-58 =
   Buchanan (not Pierce), 1868-70 = Grant (not A. Johnson), 1892-94 = Cleveland (not
   B. Harrison), 1928-30 = Hoover (not Coolidge), 2000-02 = Bush (not Clinton),
   2024-26 = Trump (not Biden). Consistent convention = **the president elected into
   the cycle**, seated ~Mar (pre-1937) / Jan (post-1937). GM/PM should treat each window
   as "the administration that governs the cycle," not "who held office on Jan 1 of the
   first year." **[I]**

**B. Reconstruction seat realism (1868-1870):** several ex-Confederate states had **no
seated senators/representatives** early in the window (readmitted Jun 1868; VA/TX/MS/GA
in 1870). The sheet correctly shows e.g. `VA Sen = vacant`. A boot that force-fills all
74 Senate seats for 1868 would be *less* accurate than the sheet. **[V]**

**C. Government-birth boundary (1788-1790):** in 1788 there is no president, cabinet,
Supreme Court, or Attorney General — those exist only from 1789 (AG/DOJ offices Sep
1789). The sheet leaves several such offices blank for this window. A 1788 boot models
the *founding of the executive*, not a running one. **[C]**

**D. Compression divergences (structural, apply to every modern window):** the seed is
deliberately compressed vs. real government — **148 House seats (not 435); 8 flag
officers (not the full general/admiral corps); single ambassador per country; one
snapshot per 2-year cycle.** These are game-scale abstractions, not historical claims,
and downstream roles should not "correct" them toward real headcounts. **[C per analysis.md]**

**E. 2024-2026 is a real-but-partial column (~280-seat hole):** fed-exec only, 0 states/
Court/Congress. This is a **data gap to flag, not to infer**. **[C per analysis.md]**

**F. Minor data quirks (flag, don't block):** anomalous `PM` 2-letter state code (likely
stray/typo); duplicate `General`/`AssocJ`/`Admiral` labels are distinct seats; `Meville
Fuller` in-sheet is Melville Fuller (CJ 1888–1910). **[I]**
